      {Scale-Separated Dyadic Cancellation\\

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{abstract}
We close the two open amplitude hypotheses of
Paper~VII \cite{paperVII} under a single additional analytic
input: a uniform Airy increment estimate for PSWF eigenvalue
gaps in the transition window (Proposition~\ref{prop:U},
stated as a conditional assumption pending full extraction
from ORX \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao}).

The main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:A} (Uniform Airy Increment): a
  discrete-to-continuous stability result for PSWF eigenvalue
  differences $\lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}$ in the Airy
  transition regime, reducing to an explicit Lipschitz
  argument on $\FAi$.
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:B} (Airy Ratio Bound): uniform control
  of the ratio $\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2 / \overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x)$
  on the bulk side of the transition.
\item Corollary~\ref{cor:C} (H2 for $m \ge m_0$): hypothesis
  (H2) of Theorem~2.1 in \cite{paperVII} holds for all
  dyadic blocks of scale $m \ge m_0$ (fixed), with
  $\delta_m = Cc^{-1/3} \to 0$ uniform in $c$.
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:E} (Core--WKB Split): the Core regime
  $m < m_0$ is controlled directly by Assumption~B-strong
  (H3), and contributes $O(1)$ to row sums by an elementary
  counting argument.
\end{itemize}
Together, these results show that hypotheses (H1)--(H3) of
Paper~VII are fully verified conditional on
Assumption~B-strong and Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, and that
no additional structural assumptions are required.
\end{abstract}

% ============================================================
\section{Introduction and Context}
\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

Paper~VII \cite{paperVII} established an abstract dyadic
cancellation theorem (Theorem~2.1) and reduced the
contraction problem for the PSWF Galerkin operator
$\mathcal{T}_c^{(N)}$ to the verification of three
hypotheses:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(H1)] Non-degenerate oscillatory phase: verified
  unconditionally from Widom asymptotics.
\item[(H2)] Blockwise amplitude regularity: the relative
  variation of the amplitude $A_{ij}$ within each dyadic
  block $\mathcal{B}_m(i)$ is $o(1)$ uniformly in $c$.
\item[(H3)] Uniform amplitude bound: $A_{ij} \le C$ for
  all $i \ne j$, equivalent to Assumption~B-strong
  (\cite{paperVI}, Assumption~B-strong).
\end{itemize}

H2 was stated in \cite{paperVII} as Conjecture~6.2 (amplitude
regularity on dyadic blocks). The present paper closes this
conjecture under a precise analytic input
(Proposition~\ref{prop:U}) which is itself a uniformization
of existing ORX asymptotics.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Central observation.}
H2 does not hold uniformly at all dyadic scales:
for the smallest blocks ($m = 0$, i.e., $|i-j| = 1$),
the relative amplitude variation can be of order $c^{1/3}$,
not $o(1)$. This is not a defect of the approach but a
structural feature: at microscopic scales, the problem
requires direct operator control (H3/B-strong), not
semiclas\-sical smoothness. The correct statement is
therefore a \emph{two-regime} one:
\[
  \text{H2 holds for } m \ge m_0 \text{ (WKB regime)};
  \qquad
  m < m_0 \text{ controlled by H3 (Core regime)}.
\]
This scale separation is the main structural content of
the present paper.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{What is not proved here.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:U} (ORX Uniformity) is stated as
an assumption; see Section~\ref{sec:propU} for a detailed
discussion of its status. B-strong (H3) remains open;
it is not addressed in this paper.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Structure.}
Section~\ref{sec:setup} fixes notation.
Section~\ref{sec:propU} states and discusses Proposition~U.
Section~\ref{sec:lemmaA} proves Lemma~A.
Section~\ref{sec:lemmaB} proves Lemma~B.
Section~\ref{sec:H2} proves Corollary~C (H2 for $m\ge m_0$).
Section~\ref{sec:split} proves Lemma~E (Core--WKB Split).
Section~\ref{sec:closure} collects the implication chain.

\section{Setup and Notation}
\label{sec:setup}
% ============================================================

Throughout, $c \ge 1$ is the bandwidth parameter,
$N = \lfloor A c^{1/3}\rfloor$ is the Galerkin truncation,
and $\psi_k^{(c)}$ denotes the $k$-th prolate spheroidal
wave function with corresponding eigenvalue $\lambda_k =
\lambda_k^{(c)} \in (0,1)$, ordered decreasingly.

Define:
\begin{align*}
  \NSh &:= \frac{2c}{\pi} \qquad \text{(Shannon number)},\\
  x_l  &:= (l - \NSh)(c/2)^{-1/3}
           \qquad \text{(Airy-rescaled index)},\\
  \FAi(x) &:= \int_x^\infty \mathrm{Ai}(t)^2\,dt
           \qquad \text{(complementary Airy CDF)},\\
  A_{ij} &:= P_{ij} \cdot c^{1/2},
  \qquad
  P_{ij} := c \cdot
  \frac{\abs{\lambda_i - \lambda_j}}
       {(1-\lambda_i)(1-\lambda_j)}
  \qquad (i\ne j).
\end{align*}

Dyadic blocks: for fixed $i$ and $m = 0,\ldots,M$ with
$M = \lfloor \log_2 N\rfloor$,
\[
  \Bm(i) := \{j : 2^m \le |i-j| < 2^{m+1}\}.
\]
The matrix $T_{ij} = A_{ij}/|i-j| \cdot e^{i\Phi_{ij}}$ is
the normalised PSWF Galerkin kernel studied in \cite{paperVII}.

\section{ORX Uniformity in the Transition Window}
\label{sec:propU}
% ============================================================

The following proposition is the key analytic input.

\begin{proposition}[ORX Uniformity]
\label{prop:U}
There exist constants $A_0, C_1, c_0 > 0$ such that for all
$c \ge c_0$ and all $l$ with $|l - \NSh| \le A_0 c^{1/3}$:
\[
  \abs{\lambda_l - \FAi(x_l)} \le C_1\, c^{-2/3},
\]
uniform in $l$ throughout the transition window.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Status of Proposition~\ref{prop:U}]
\label{rem:propU_status}
Proposition~\ref{prop:U} is not proved here. Its content is
a \emph{uniformization} of the pointwise Airy approximation
for PSWF eigenvalues established in
\cite[Theorem~4.6]{OsipovRokhlinXiao} (ORX).

ORX establishes, for each fixed rescaled argument $x$, that
$\lambda_l \to \FAi(x_l)$ in a controlled sense as
$c \to \infty$. What Proposition~\ref{prop:U} adds is
\emph{uniformity in $l$} over the full window of width
$O(c^{1/3})$. This is expected from the structure of the
ORX proof (which proceeds via WKB matching at the turning
point $s_* = 1 - c^{-2/3}$, with matching error uniform
across the transition zone), but the uniform error bound
$O(c^{-2/3})$ is not stated as a theorem in ORX.

Proving Proposition~\ref{prop:U} rigorously requires a
careful re-examination of ORX Sections~4.4--4.7 with
explicit tracking of uniformity constants. This is a
well-defined analytic task that does not require
Riemann--Hilbert or steepest-descent methods; it reduces
to verifying that the WKB matching error is bounded by
a constant times $c^{-2/3}$ uniformly in the transition
window. We state it here as an assumption and intend to
address it in a subsequent note.
\end{remark}

% ============================================================
\section{Lemma A: Uniform Airy Increment}
\label{sec:lemmaA}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Uniform Airy Increment]
\label{lem:A}
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, for all $c \ge c_0$ and all
$l$ with $|l - \NSh| \le A_0 c^{1/3}$:
\[
  \lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}
  = (c/2)^{-1/3}\,\mathrm{Ai}(x_l)^2 + R_l,
\]
where $|R_l| \le C\,c^{-2/3}$, uniform in $l$.
Equivalently,
\[
  (c/2)^{1/3}(\lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1})
  = \mathrm{Ai}(x_l)^2 + O(c^{-1/3}),
\]
uniform in $l$ in the transition window.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
We decompose the difference using Proposition~\ref{prop:U}:
\[
  \lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}
  = \bigl[\FAi(x_l) - \FAi(x_{l+1})\bigr]
    + (E_l - E_{l+1}),
\]
where $E_l := \lambda_l - \FAi(x_l)$ satisfies
$|E_l| \le C_1 c^{-2/3}$ by Proposition~\ref{prop:U}.
Hence $|E_l - E_{l+1}| \le 2C_1 c^{-2/3}$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Main term.}
Since $\FAi' = -\mathrm{Ai}^2$ and
$x_{l+1} - x_l = -(c/2)^{-1/3}$,
the mean value theorem gives, for some
$\xi_l \in [x_l, x_{l+1}]$:
\[
  \FAi(x_l) - \FAi(x_{l+1})
  = \mathrm{Ai}(\xi_l)^2 \cdot (x_{l+1} - x_l)^{-1}
    \cdot (x_{l+1} - x_l)
  = \mathrm{Ai}(\xi_l)^2 \cdot (c/2)^{-1/3}.
\]
(Note the signs cancel: $\FAi(x_l) - \FAi(x_{l+1}) > 0$
since $\FAi$ is decreasing and $x_{l+1} > x_l$.)

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Transition $\xi_l \to x_l$.}
On $[-A_0, A_0]$, the function $\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2$ is
$C^1$ with Lipschitz constant
$L := \sup_{|x|\le A_0} 2|\mathrm{Ai}(x)||\mathrm{Ai}'(x)| < \infty$.
Since $|\xi_l - x_l| \le |x_{l+1} - x_l| = (c/2)^{-1/3}$:
\[
  \abs{\mathrm{Ai}(\xi_l)^2 - \mathrm{Ai}(x_l)^2}
  \le L\,(c/2)^{-1/3}.
\]
The additional error from replacing $\xi_l$ by $x_l$ is
therefore $(c/2)^{-1/3} \cdot L \cdot (c/2)^{-1/3}
= L(c/2)^{-2/3} = O(c^{-2/3})$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Conclusion.}
Combining:
\[
  \lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}
  = (c/2)^{-1/3}\,\mathrm{Ai}(x_l)^2
    + R_l,
\]
where
$|R_l| \le (2C_1 + L)\,c^{-2/3} =: C\,c^{-2/3}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Relation to ORX]
\label{rem:lemmaA_ORX}
ORX establishes the pointwise approximation
$\lambda_l \approx \FAi(x_l)$ but does not treat
$\lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}$ as an object of study.
Lemma~\ref{lem:A} is therefore not a corollary of ORX
but rather an application of the mean value theorem
to the ORX approximation, with explicit error bookkeeping.
The only input from ORX used here is
Proposition~\ref{prop:U}.
\end{remark}

\section{Lemma B: Airy Ratio Bound}
\label{sec:lemmaB}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Airy Ratio Bound]
\label{lem:B}
Let $\overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x) := \FAi(x)
= \int_x^\infty \mathrm{Ai}(t)^2\,dt$.
For each $A_0 > 0$ there exists $C_{A_0} < \infty$ such
that
\[
  \frac{\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2}{\overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x)}
  \le C_{A_0}
  \qquad \text{for all } x \in [-A_0, A_0].
\]
For $x \to +\infty$, the ratio grows like $x^{1/2}$
(standard Airy asymptotics), so the bound is restricted
to the bulk side of the transition.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $[-A_0, A_0]$, both $\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2$ and
$\overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x)$ are continuous and strictly
positive (the Airy function has no zeros on $(-\infty, 0)$
and its first positive zero is $a_1 \approx 2.34$,
so for $A_0 \le 2.3$ both quantities are bounded away
from zero). The ratio is therefore bounded on compact sets
by continuity. For general $A_0$, one uses that
$\overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x) \ge c_0 e^{-Cx^{3/2}}$
for $x > 0$ and that $\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2 \lesssim x^{-1/2}
e^{-2x^{3/2}/3}$ for large $x > 0$, so the ratio is
$O(x^{1/2})$, bounded on $[0, A_0]$ for any fixed $A_0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
In the Galerkin regime $l \le N = \lfloor Ac^{1/3}\rfloor$,
the rescaled arguments $x_l = (l - \NSh)(c/2)^{-1/3}$
lie in $[-A, 0]$ for the bulk modes (indices well below
the Shannon number), ensuring that Lemma~\ref{lem:B}
applies with $A_0 = A$.
\end{remark}

\section{Corollary C: H2 for Large Dyadic Scales}
\label{sec:H2}
% ============================================================

\begin{corollary}[H2 for $m \ge m_0$]
\label{cor:C}
Fix $m_0 \ge 1$ (independent of $c$). Under
Proposition~\ref{prop:U} and Lemma~\ref{lem:B},
for all $c \ge c_0$, all $i \le N$, all $m \ge m_0$,
and all $j, j+1 \in \Bm(i)$:
\[
  \frac{|A_{ij} - A_{i,j+1}|}{A_{ij}}
  \le C\,c^{-1/3},
\]
uniform in $i, j, m, c$.
In particular, hypothesis \textup{(H2)} of
Theorem~2.1 in \cite{paperVII} holds for all
$m \ge m_0$ with $\delta_m = Cc^{-1/3} \to 0$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Recall $A_{ij} = P_{ij}\cdot c^{1/2}$ with
$P_{ij} = c \cdot |\lambda_i - \lambda_j|
/ ((1-\lambda_i)(1-\lambda_j))$.
At fixed $i$, the variation of $A_{ij}$ in $j$ comes
entirely from the $j$-dependence of $\lambda_j$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Step 1: Relative variation of $\lambda_j$.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:A}:
\[
  \lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}
  = (c/2)^{-1/3}\mathrm{Ai}(x_j)^2 + O(c^{-2/3}).
\]
Hence $|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}| \le C(c/2)^{-1/3}$
uniform in $j$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Step 2: Relative variation of $A_{ij}$.}
A direct computation gives (fixing $i$, varying $j \to j+1$):
\begin{align*}
  \frac{|A_{ij} - A_{i,j+1}|}{A_{ij}}
  &\lesssim
  \frac{|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}|}{|\lambda_i - \lambda_j|}
  +
  \frac{|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}|}{1 - \lambda_j}.
\end{align*}

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Step 3: Control of each term.}
For the second term, Lemma~\ref{lem:B} and
Proposition~\ref{prop:U} give
\[
  \frac{\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}}{1-\lambda_j}
  \sim
  \frac{(c/2)^{-1/3}\mathrm{Ai}(x_j)^2}
       {\overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}(x_j)}
  \le C_{A_0}(c/2)^{-1/3}
  = O(c^{-1/3}).
\]
For the first term, on a dyadic block $\Bm(i)$ with
$m \ge m_0 \ge 1$, we have $|i - j| \ge 2$ so
$|\lambda_i - \lambda_j| \ge \lambda_{j_0} - \lambda_{j_0+1}$
for at least two consecutive steps; by Lemma~\ref{lem:A}
this is $\ge c' (c/2)^{-1/3}$ for some universal $c' > 0$
(away from Airy zeros, which don't occur in $[-A_0, 0]$).
Hence the first term is also $O(1)$ times
$c^{-1/3}/1 = O(c^{-1/3})$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Conclusion.}
Both terms are $O(c^{-1/3}) \to 0$,
giving H2 with $\delta_m = Cc^{-1/3}$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Why H2 fails for $m=0$]
\label{rem:H2_m0}
For $m = 0$ (i.e., $|i-j| = 1$), the first term in Step~2
becomes $|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}| / |\lambda_i - \lambda_{i\pm 1}|$,
which is $O(1)$ (ratio of two adjacent gaps, not $O(c^{-1/3})$).
Thus H2 in the form $\delta_0 \to 0$ fails at the
microscopic scale.
This is not a defect: it means that the dyadically smallest
scale requires a different type of control,
not semiciassical regularity but direct operator domination
--- which is exactly what H3 (B-strong) provides.
See Lemma~\ref{lem:E}.
\end{remark}

\section{Lemma E: Core--WKB Split}
\label{sec:split}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Core--WKB Split]
\label{lem:E}
Fix $m_0 \ge 1$ independent of $c$.
Assume Proposition~\ref{prop:U},
Lemma~\ref{lem:A}, Lemma~\ref{lem:B},
and Assumption~B-strong \cite{paperVI} (i.e., H3:
$A_{ij} \le C_2$ for all $i \ne j$, $i,j \le N$).
Then for all $i \le N$:
\[
  \sum_{j \ne i} |T_{ij}| \le C(m_0, A, C_2)
\]
uniform in $i$, $N$, and $c \ge c_0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Split the sum at scale $m_0$:
\[
  \sum_{j \ne i} |T_{ij}|
  = \underbrace{\sum_{m=0}^{m_0-1} \sum_{j\in\Bm(i)}
    |T_{ij}|}_{\text{Core regime}}
  + \underbrace{\sum_{m=m_0}^{M} \sum_{j\in\Bm(i)}
    |T_{ij}|}_{\text{WKB regime}}.
\]

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Core regime ($m < m_0$).}
By H3, $A_{ij} \le C_2$, so
$|T_{ij}| = A_{ij}/|i-j| \le C_2/|i-j|$.
Each block $\Bm(i)$ contains at most $2^{m+1}$ indices
with $|i-j| \ge 2^m$, so
\[
  \sum_{j \in \Bm(i)} |T_{ij}|
  \le C_2 \cdot \frac{2^{m+1}}{2^m} = 2C_2.
\]
Summing over $m_0$ fixed blocks:
\[
  \sum_{m=0}^{m_0-1}\sum_{j\in\Bm(i)} |T_{ij}|
  \le 2C_2 m_0 = O(1).
\]

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{WKB regime ($m \ge m_0$).}
By Corollary~\ref{cor:C}, H2 holds on all blocks
$\Bm(i)$ with $m \ge m_0$ (with $\delta_m = Cc^{-1/3}$).
Combined with H1 (unconditional, \cite{paperVII})
and H3 (B-strong), all hypotheses of
Theorem~2.1 of \cite{paperVII} are satisfied
for the truncated sum over $m \ge m_0$.
That theorem gives
\[
  \sum_{m=m_0}^{M}\sum_{j\in\Bm(i)} |T_{ij}|
  = O(1).
\]

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Conclusion.}
$\sum_{j\ne i}|T_{ij}| \le 2C_2 m_0 + O(1) = O(1)$,
uniform in $i$ and $c$.
\end{proof}

\section{Closure: Full Hypothesis Verification}
\label{sec:closure}
% ============================================================

\begin{corollary}[Full Hypothesis Closure]
\label{cor:closure}
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U} and
Assumption~B-strong \cite{paperVI},
all three hypotheses \textup{(H1)--(H3)} of
Theorem~2.1 in \cite{paperVII} are verified:
\begin{itemize}
\item[\textup{(H1)}] Verified unconditionally
  (\cite{paperVII}, Proposition~3.1).
\item[\textup{(H2)}] Verified for $m \ge m_0$ by
  Corollary~\ref{cor:C}; the Core regime $m < m_0$
  is absorbed into the H3-controlled sum in Lemma~\ref{lem:E}.
\item[\textup{(H3)}] Assumption~B-strong by hypothesis.
\end{itemize}
Consequently, by Lemma~\ref{lem:E} and
Remark~2.3 of \cite{paperVII}:
\[
  \sup_{i\le N}\,\sum_{j\ne i}|P_{ij}|
  \le C\,c^{-1/2}\log c,
\]
which is Assumption~$B'$ of \cite{paperVI}.
The contraction estimate
$\norm{D\mathcal{T}_c^{(N)}} \le C(A)\,c^{-1/2}\log c < 1$
(for $c \ge c_0$) follows, establishing Assumption~A
conditional on Proposition~\ref{prop:U} and B-strong.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[What remains open]
\label{rem:open}
The program is now reduced to exactly two open problems:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Proposition~\ref{prop:U}:} Uniformization
  of ORX asymptotics over the transition window.
  Expected from ORX methods; requires explicit constant
  tracking in ORX Sections~4.4--4.7.
\item \textbf{Assumption~B-strong:} Global amplitude bound
  $P_{kl} \le C_2 c^{1/2}$ in the transition zone.
  This requires control of a correlated Airy ratio
  (Szeg\H{o}-type problem for $\mathrm{Ai}^2 /
  \overline{F}_{\mathrm{Ai}}$ in two parameters);
  it is the deepest remaining open problem in the program.
\end{itemize}
All other hypotheses are now closed.
\end{remark}
