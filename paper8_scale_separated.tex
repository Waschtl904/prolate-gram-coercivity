      {Scale-Separated Dyadic Cancellation\\
       for PSWF Galerkin Operators:\\
       Core--WKB Decomposition and Hypothesis Closure}

\author{[Author]}
\date{\today}
\maketitle

% ------------------------------------------------------------
\begin{abstract}
We close the open amplitude hypothesis~(H2) of
Paper~VII \cite{paperVII} under a single additional analytic
input: a uniform Airy approximation for PSWF eigenvalues in
the transition window (Proposition~\ref{prop:U},
stated as a conditional assumption pending full extraction
from ORX \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao}; all results of this
paper are conditional on it).

The main contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:A} (Uniform Airy Increment): a
  discrete-to-continuous stability result for PSWF eigenvalue
  differences $\lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}$ in the Airy
  transition regime.
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:B} (Airy Ratio Bound): uniform control
  of the ratio $\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2 / \FAi(x)$
  on a compact interval containing no zero of $\mathrm{Ai}$.
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:F} (Airy Rigidity): a quantitative
  bi-Lipschitz bound on $\FAi$ and a corresponding spectral
  separation estimate for $|\lambda_i - \lambda_j|$, with
  explicit dependence on $|i-j|$.
\item Corollary~\ref{cor:C} (H2 for $m \ge m_0$): hypothesis
  (H2) of Theorem~2.1 in \cite{paperVII} holds for all
  dyadic blocks of scale $m \ge m_0$ (fixed), with
  relative variation bounded by $2^{-m} + Cc^{-1/3} \to 0$
  uniformly in $c$.
\item Lemma~\ref{lem:E} (Core--WKB Split): the Core regime
  $m < m_0$ is controlled directly by Assumption~B-strong
  (H3), contributing $O(1)$ to row sums.
\end{itemize}
Together, these results show that hypotheses (H1)--(H3) of
Paper~VII are fully verified conditional on
Assumption~B-strong and Proposition~\ref{prop:U}.
\end{abstract}

% ============================================================
\section{Introduction and Context}
\label{sec:intro}
% ============================================================

Paper~VII \cite{paperVII} established an abstract dyadic
cancellation theorem (Theorem~2.1) and reduced the
contraction problem for the PSWF Galerkin operator
$\mathcal{T}_c^{(N)}$ to the verification of three
hypotheses:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(H1)] Non-degenerate oscillatory phase: verified
  unconditionally from Widom asymptotics.
\item[(H2)] Blockwise amplitude regularity: the relative
  variation of the amplitude $A_{ij}$ within each dyadic
  block $\mathcal{B}_m(i)$ is $o(1)$ uniformly in $c$.
\item[(H3)] Uniform amplitude bound: $A_{ij} \le C$ for
  all $i \ne j$, equivalent to Assumption~B-strong
  (\cite{paperVI}).
\end{itemize}

H2 was stated in \cite{paperVII} as Conjecture~6.2.
The present paper closes this conjecture under
Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, which is a uniformization
of existing ORX asymptotics.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Central observation.}
H2 does not hold uniformly at all dyadic scales.
For the smallest blocks ($m = 0$, i.e., $|i-j| = 1$),
the relative amplitude variation can be $O(1)$, not $o(1)$.
The correct statement is a \emph{two-regime} one:
\[
  \text{H2 holds for } m \ge m_0 \text{ (WKB regime)};
  \qquad
  m < m_0 \text{ controlled by H3 (Core regime).}
\]
The key mechanism in the WKB regime is \emph{dyadic separation}:
for $j \in \mathcal{B}_m(i)$, the denominator
$|\lambda_i - \lambda_j|$ is $\Omega(c^{-1/3} \cdot 2^m)$,
which strictly dominates the numerator
$|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}| = O(c^{-1/3})$.
This produces a factor $2^{-m}$ in the relative variation,
which is the genuine content of H2.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{What is not proved here.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:U} (ORX Uniformity) is stated as
an assumption; this paper is logically independent of
a proof of it, and all subsequent results are
explicitly conditional on Proposition~\ref{prop:U}.
B-strong (H3) likewise remains open.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Structure.}
Section~\ref{sec:setup} fixes notation and the global
Airy parameter $A_0$ together with the three
compactness constants $c_*$ (for $\FAi$) and
$c_{\min}, C_{\max}$ (for $\mathrm{Ai}^2$).
Section~\ref{sec:propU} states Proposition~U.
Sections~\ref{sec:lemmaA}--\ref{sec:lemmaF} prove the
four supporting lemmas.
Section~\ref{sec:H2} proves Corollary~C.
Section~\ref{sec:split} proves Lemma~E.
Section~\ref{sec:closure} collects the closure.

\section{Setup and Notation}
\label{sec:setup}
% ============================================================

Throughout, $c \ge 1$ is the bandwidth parameter and
$\lambda_k = \lambda_k^{(c)} \in (0,1)$ are the PSWF
eigenvalues, ordered decreasingly.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Airy zeros and the parameter $A_0$.}
All real zeros of $\mathrm{Ai}$ are negative; we denote
them $a_1 > a_2 > \cdots$ (ordered by decreasing value),
with $a_1 \approx -2.3381$ the largest real zero
\cite[Sec.~9.9]{DLMF}.
Since $a_1$ is the largest real zero, every real zero
satisfies $x < a_1$, and hence $(a_1,+\infty)$ is
zero-free; in particular $\mathrm{Ai}(x)>0$ for all
$x > a_1$ (and trivially for all $x \ge 0$).

We fix once and for all:
\[
  \boxed{A_0 \in (0,\, |a_1|) \approx (0,\, 2.3381)}
\]
Then $[-A_0, A_0] \subset (a_1,+\infty)$ and hence
contains no zero of $\mathrm{Ai}$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Compactness constants.}
We introduce three constants, depending only on $A_0$,
that encode the quantitative behaviour of $\FAi$ and
$\mathrm{Ai}^2$ on the zero-free interval:
\begin{align*}
  c_* &:= \min_{x\in[-A_0,\,0]}\FAi(x) > 0,\\
  c_{\min} &:= \min_{x\in[-A_0,\,A_0]}\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2 > 0,
  \qquad
  C_{\max} := \max_{x\in[-A_0,\,A_0]}\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2 < \infty.
\end{align*}
Positivity and finiteness follow from continuity on a
compact set and the zero-free condition.
The convention is: $c_*$ bounds $\FAi$ from below on
the transition window $[-A_0,0]$; $c_{\min}$ and
$C_{\max}$ bound $\mathrm{Ai}^2$ on $[-A_0,A_0]$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Rescaled index.}
We define $x_l$ as a \emph{deterministic linear rescaling}:
\[
  x_l := (l - \NSh)\,(c/2)^{-1/3},
  \qquad \NSh := \frac{2c}{\pi}.
\]
This is a definition, not an asymptotic formula;
the spacing $x_{l+1} - x_l = (c/2)^{-1/3}$ is exactly
constant in $l$.

The Galerkin truncation is $N = \lfloor Ac^{1/3}\rfloor$
with $A < |a_1|$. For $l \le \NSh$ (bulk modes)
$x_l \le 0$; for $l = N$, $x_N \approx -A$.
All Galerkin indices therefore satisfy
$x_l \in [-A,0] \subset [-A_0,A_0] \subset (a_1,+\infty)$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Further definitions:}
\begin{align*}
  \FAi(x) &:= \int_x^\infty \mathrm{Ai}(t)^2\,dt
    \qquad \bigl(\FAi(x) > 0 \text{ for all }
    x \in \mathbb{R};\;
    \FAi(x)\to 0 \text{ only as } x\to+\infty\bigr),\\
  A_{ij} &:= P_{ij}\cdot c^{1/2},
  \quad
  P_{ij} := c\cdot
  \frac{|\lambda_i-\lambda_j|}{(1-\lambda_i)(1-\lambda_j)}
  \quad (i\ne j).
\end{align*}

Dyadic blocks: $\Bm(i) := \{j : 2^m \le |i-j| < 2^{m+1}\}$,
$m = 0,\ldots,M$, $M = \lfloor\log_2 N\rfloor$.

\medskip\noindent
\textbf{Approximation of $1-\lambda_j$.}
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U},
$\lambda_j = \FAi(x_j) + E_j$ with $|E_j| \le C_1 c^{-2/3}$,
so $1 - \lambda_j = \FAi(x_j) - E_j$.
All transition-window indices satisfy $x_j \in [-A_0, 0]$,
so $\FAi(x_j) \ge c_* > 0$ uniformly in $j$.
Hence
\[
  1-\lambda_j = \FAi(x_j)\bigl(1 + O(c^{-2/3})\bigr),
  \qquad\text{i.e.}\quad
  1-\lambda_j \asymp \FAi(x_j)
\]
uniformly in the transition window.
This is used explicitly in the proof of
Corollary~\ref{cor:C}.

\section{ORX Uniformity in the Transition Window}
\label{sec:propU}
% ============================================================

\begin{proposition}[ORX Uniformity]
\label{prop:U}
With $A_0$ as fixed in Section~\ref{sec:setup},
there exist constants $C_1, c_0 > 0$ such that for all
$c \ge c_0$ and all $l$ with $|l - \NSh| \le A_0 c^{1/3}$:
\[
  \abs{\lambda_l - \FAi(x_l)} \le C_1\, c^{-2/3},
\]
uniform in $l$ throughout the transition window.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[Status of Proposition~\ref{prop:U}]
\label{rem:propU_status}
This is a \emph{uniform} version of the pointwise Airy
approximation in \cite[Theorem~4.6]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
ORX establishes pointwise convergence $\lambda_l \to \FAi(x_l)$
for fixed rescaled $x$; Proposition~\ref{prop:U} requires
this with a uniform $O(c^{-2/3})$ error over
$O(c^{1/3})$ indices simultaneously.
This is expected from the WKB matching structure of the ORX
proof, but is not explicitly stated there.
Proving it requires tracking uniformity constants in
ORX Sections~4.4--4.7; no Riemann--Hilbert methods
are needed.
This paper is logically independent of a proof of
Proposition~\ref{prop:U}; all results below are
explicitly conditional on it.
\end{remark}

\section{Lemma A: Uniform Airy Increment}
\label{sec:lemmaA}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Uniform Airy Increment]
\label{lem:A}
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, for all $c \ge c_0$ and all
$l$ with $|l - \NSh| \le A_0 c^{1/3}$:
\[
  \lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}
  = (c/2)^{-1/3}\,\mathrm{Ai}(x_l)^2 + R_l,
\]
where $|R_l| \le C\,c^{-2/3}$, uniform in $l$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From Proposition~\ref{prop:U}:
\[
  \lambda_l - \lambda_{l+1}
  = [\FAi(x_l) - \FAi(x_{l+1})]
    + (E_l - E_{l+1}),
\]
where $|E_k| \le C_1 c^{-2/3}$, so
$|E_l - E_{l+1}| \le 2C_1 c^{-2/3}$.

\textbf{Main term.}
Since $\FAi' = -\mathrm{Ai}^2$ and
$x_{l+1} - x_l = (c/2)^{-1/3}$ (exactly, by definition),
the mean value theorem gives $\xi_l \in [x_l, x_{l+1}]$
with $\FAi(x_l) - \FAi(x_{l+1})
= \mathrm{Ai}(\xi_l)^2 \cdot (c/2)^{-1/3}$.

\textbf{Replacing $\xi_l$ by $x_l$.}
$\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2$ is $C^1$ on $[-A_0, A_0]$
(zero-free, hence smooth) with bounded derivative;
since $|\xi_l - x_l| \le (c/2)^{-1/3}$,
the replacement error is $O(c^{-2/3})$.

Combining: $|R_l| \le (2C_1 + L)c^{-2/3}$
for a finite constant $L$ depending only on $A_0$.
\end{proof}

% ============================================================
\section{Lemma B: Airy Ratio Bound}
\label{sec:lemmaB}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Airy Ratio Bound]
\label{lem:B}
With the constants of Section~\ref{sec:setup},
\[
  \frac{\mathrm{Ai}(x)^2}{\FAi(x)}
  \le \frac{C_{\max}}{c_*}
  \qquad \text{for all } x \in [-A_0, A_0].
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $[-A_0, A_0]$: numerator $\le C_{\max}$ and
denominator $\ge c_* > 0$ (both from
Section~\ref{sec:setup}; note $\FAi(x)\to 0$ only
as $x\to+\infty$, so $\FAi$ is bounded away from
zero on every bounded interval). The bound follows.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
The constant $C_{\max}/c_*$ plays the role of $C_{A_0}$
in the proof of Corollary~\ref{cor:C}.
\end{remark}

\section{Lemma F: Airy Rigidity}
\label{sec:lemmaF}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Airy Rigidity]
\label{lem:F}
With the constants of Section~\ref{sec:setup},
$\FAi$ satisfies the bi-Lipschitz bound
\[
  c_{\min}\,|x_i - x_j|
  \le |\FAi(x_i) - \FAi(x_j)|
  \le C_{\max}\,|x_i - x_j|
  \qquad \text{for all } x_i, x_j \in [-A_0, A_0].
\]
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U},
for any $i \ne j$ with $x_i, x_j \in [-A_0, A_0]$
and $c$ sufficiently large:
\[
  |\lambda_i - \lambda_j|
  \ge \tfrac{1}{2}\,c_{\min}\,(c/2)^{-1/3}\,|i-j|.
\]
In particular, for $j \in \Bm(i)$ (so $|i-j| \ge 2^m$):
\[
  |\lambda_i - \lambda_j|
  \ge \tfrac{1}{2}\,c_{\min}\,(c/2)^{-1/3}\cdot 2^m.
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Bi-Lipschitz bound.}
By the fundamental theorem of calculus,
for $x_i, x_j \in [-A_0, A_0]$:
\[
  |\FAi(x_i)-\FAi(x_j)|
  = \left|\int_{x_j}^{x_i}\mathrm{Ai}(t)^2\,dt\right|.
\]
The integrand satisfies
$c_{\min} \le \mathrm{Ai}(t)^2 \le C_{\max}$
on $[-A_0,A_0]$ (Section~\ref{sec:setup}),
giving both bounds simultaneously.
With $|x_i - x_j| = (c/2)^{-1/3}|i-j|$, this yields
the stated bi-Lipschitz estimate.

\textbf{Spectral separation.}
Proposition~\ref{prop:U}: $\lambda_k = \FAi(x_k)+E_k$,
$|E_k| \le C_1 c^{-2/3}$. Triangle inequality:
\[
  |\lambda_i-\lambda_j|
  \ge c_{\min}(c/2)^{-1/3}|i-j| - 2C_1 c^{-2/3}.
\]
For $c$ sufficiently large (depending only on $A_0$
and $C_1$), the error term $2C_1 c^{-2/3}$ is absorbed
by $\frac{1}{2}c_{\min}(c/2)^{-1/3}|i-j|$
(since $|i-j| \ge 1$ and $(c/2)^{-1/3} \gg c^{-2/3}$).
For $j \in \Bm(i)$, $|i-j| \ge 2^m$ gives the
final displayed estimate.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
Only the lower bound of Lemma~\ref{lem:F} is used
in Corollary~\ref{cor:C}, and crucially with the
explicit $2^m$ factor (dyadic separation).
The upper bound completes the bi-Lipschitz structure
of $\FAi$ on the zero-free interval and will be
useful in later work.
\end{remark}

\section{Corollary C: H2 for Large Dyadic Scales}
\label{sec:H2}
% ============================================================

\begin{corollary}[H2 for $m \ge m_0$]
\label{cor:C}
Fix $m_0 \ge 1$ (independent of $c$). Under
Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, Lemma~\ref{lem:B},
and Lemma~\ref{lem:F},
for all $c \ge c_0$, all $i \le N$, all $m \ge m_0$,
and all $j, j+1 \in \Bm(i)$:
\[
  \frac{|A_{ij} - A_{i,j+1}|}{A_{ij}}
  \lesssim 2^{-m} + C\,c^{-1/3}.
\]
In particular, for $m \ge m_0$ this is bounded by
$2^{-m_0} + Cc^{-1/3} \to 0$ as $c \to \infty$
(with $m_0$ fixed).
Hypothesis~\textup{(H2)} of Theorem~2.1 in
\cite{paperVII} therefore holds for all $m \ge m_0$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
At fixed $i$, the variation of
$A_{ij} \propto |\lambda_i-\lambda_j|/
((1-\lambda_i)(1-\lambda_j))$ in $j$ yields:
\[
  \frac{|A_{ij} - A_{i,j+1}|}{A_{ij}}
  \lesssim
  \underbrace{\frac{|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}|}{|\lambda_i - \lambda_j|}}_{\text{Term 1}}
  +
  \underbrace{\frac{|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}|}{1 - \lambda_j}}_{\text{Term 2}}.
\]

\textbf{Term 2.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:A},
$|\lambda_j-\lambda_{j+1}| \le C(c/2)^{-1/3}$.
By Section~\ref{sec:setup},
$1-\lambda_j \asymp \FAi(x_j) \ge c_* > 0$.
Lemma~\ref{lem:B} gives
$\mathrm{Ai}(x_j)^2/\FAi(x_j) \le C_{\max}/c_*$,
so:
\[
  \text{Term 2}
  \lesssim \frac{C_{\max}}{c_*}\cdot(c/2)^{-1/3}
  = O(c^{-1/3}).
\]

\textbf{Term 1.}
Using Lemma~\ref{lem:F} with the explicit $2^m$ factor:
since $j \in \Bm(i)$ implies $|i-j| \ge 2^m$,
\[
  |\lambda_i - \lambda_j|
  \ge \tfrac{1}{2}\,c_{\min}\,(c/2)^{-1/3}\cdot 2^m.
\]
Combining with Lemma~\ref{lem:A}
($|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}| \le C(c/2)^{-1/3}$):
\[
  \text{Term 1}
  \le \frac{C(c/2)^{-1/3}}
      {\frac{1}{2}c_{\min}(c/2)^{-1/3}\cdot 2^m}
  = \frac{2C}{c_{\min}}\cdot 2^{-m}.
\]
The $(c/2)^{-1/3}$ factors cancel, leaving a pure
$2^{-m}$ bound with no $c$-dependence.

\textbf{Conclusion.}
\[
  \frac{|A_{ij} - A_{i,j+1}|}{A_{ij}}
  \lesssim 2^{-m} + C\,c^{-1/3}.
\]
For fixed $m_0$ and all $m \ge m_0$, both terms
vanish: $2^{-m} \le 2^{-m_0}$ (fixed, controlled by
choice of $m_0$) and $Cc^{-1/3} \to 0$ as
$c \to \infty$. This is $o(1)$ uniformly in $j$,
establishing H2.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Dyadic mechanism]
\label{rem:dyadic}
The cancellation of the $(c/2)^{-1/3}$ factors in
Term~1 is the key structural point: the numerator
$|\lambda_j - \lambda_{j+1}|$ and denominator
$|\lambda_i - \lambda_j|$ are both $O(c^{-1/3})$,
but the denominator carries an additional factor of
$|i-j| \ge 2^m$, which produces the $2^{-m}$ decay.
This is the dyadic separation mechanism that
drives H2.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why H2 fails for $m = 0$]
\label{rem:H2_m0}
For $m = 0$, $|i-j| \ge 1$ only, so Term~1
$\lesssim 2^0 = O(1)$: no decay.
H2 fails at the microscopic scale;
H3/B-strong provides the necessary control.
\end{remark}

\section{Lemma E: Core--WKB Split}
\label{sec:split}
% ============================================================

\begin{lemma}[Core--WKB Split]
\label{lem:E}
Fix $m_0 \ge 1$ independent of $c$.
Assume Proposition~\ref{prop:U}, Lemmas~\ref{lem:A},
\ref{lem:B}, \ref{lem:F}, and Assumption~B-strong
\cite{paperVI} (H3: $A_{ij} \le C_2$ for all
$i \ne j$, $i,j \le N$). Then for all $i \le N$:
\[
  \sum_{j \ne i} |T_{ij}| \le C(m_0, A, C_2)
\]
uniform in $i$, $N$, and $c \ge c_0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\[
  \sum_{j \ne i} |T_{ij}|
  = \underbrace{\sum_{m=0}^{m_0-1}\sum_{j\in\Bm(i)}
    |T_{ij}|}_{\text{Core}}
  + \underbrace{\sum_{m=m_0}^{M}\sum_{j\in\Bm(i)}
    |T_{ij}|}_{\text{WKB}}.
\]

\textbf{Core ($m < m_0$).}
H3: $|T_{ij}| \le C_2/|i-j|$.
Each $\Bm(i)$ has $\le 2^{m+1}$ elements with
$|i-j| \ge 2^m$, giving $\sum_{\Bm}|T_{ij}| \le 2C_2$.
Summing over $m_0$ blocks: $\le 2C_2 m_0 = O(1)$.

\textbf{WKB ($m \ge m_0$).}
Corollary~\ref{cor:C} gives H2 for all $m \ge m_0$;
with H1 (unconditional) and H3 (B-strong), Theorem~2.1
of \cite{paperVII} applies, giving $O(1)$.
(H3 is used here only for the application of
Theorem~2.1; it is not used in the proof of H2 itself.)

\textbf{Total:} $O(1)$, uniform in $i, c$.
\end{proof}

\section{Closure: Full Hypothesis Verification}
\label{sec:closure}
% ============================================================

\begin{corollary}[Full Hypothesis Closure]
\label{cor:closure}
Under Proposition~\ref{prop:U} and
Assumption~B-strong \cite{paperVI},
all three hypotheses \textup{(H1)--(H3)} of
Theorem~2.1 in \cite{paperVII} are verified.
Consequently:
\[
  \sup_{i\le N}\,\sum_{j\ne i}|P_{ij}|
  \le C\,c^{-1/2}\log c,
\]
which is Assumption~$B'$ of \cite{paperVI}.
The contraction estimate
$\norm{D\mathcal{T}_c^{(N)}} \le C(A)\,c^{-1/2}\log c < 1$
for $c \ge c_0$ follows, establishing Assumption~A
conditional on Proposition~\ref{prop:U} and B-strong.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Logical dependency chain]
\[
  \text{Prop.~U}
  \;\Rightarrow\; \text{Lem.~A, F}
  \;\Rightarrow\; \text{Cor.~C (H2, }m\ge m_0\text{)}
  \;\xrightarrow{+\,\text{H3}}\; \text{Lem.~E}
  \;\Rightarrow\; B' \;\Rightarrow\; \text{Assumption~A}.
\]
H1 enters unconditionally; Lemma~B is used in
Cor.~C (Term~2). B-strong (H3) enters only in
Lemma~E; it is not used in proving H2.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[What remains open]
\label{rem:open}
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Proposition~\ref{prop:U}:}
  Uniform Airy approximation over the transition window;
  requires explicit constant tracking in
  ORX Sections~4.4--4.7.
\item \textbf{Assumption~B-strong:}
  $P_{kl} \le C_2 c^{1/2}$ in the transition zone;
  the deepest remaining open problem.
\end{itemize}
All other hypotheses are closed.
\end{remark}
