\begin{abstract}
Let $\{\psin\}_{n\ge 0}$ be the prolate spheroidal wave functions on
$[-T,T]$ with bandwidth $\omega$ and $c = \omega T$. We prove that
for every $f \in C^1([-T,T])$ and $n \le \gamma N$ (bulk regime):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:main}
  \int_{-T}^T f(x)\,\psin(x)^2\,dx
  = \lambda_n(c)\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)} f(x)\,\rhocl(x)\,dx
    + O\!\left(\frac{\lambda_n(c)\,\|f'\|_{L^\infty}}{n}\right),
\end{equation}
where $\rhocl$ is the classical reference density of the
Sturm--Liouville operator $D_c$, defined on the classically allowed
interval $[-x_+(n), x_+(n)]$ (Definition~\ref{def:rho-cl}).
The error constant is explicit and depends only on $\gamma$, $c$, and $T$.

The proof uses the Pr\"ufer transformation to separate amplitude
and phase, establishes a $O(1/n)$-bound on oscillatory integrals
against $\cos(2\theta_n)$ via a single integration by parts
(Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}), controls the
amplitude drift by a coupled oscillation argument on the Pr\"ufer ODE
(Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift}), and identifies the normalization
constant $K_n$ via the Bohr--Sommerfeld relation derived from
Slepian's integral formula (Lemma~\ref{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf}).
No Airy-function matching is needed.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Setting and goal}

This paper addresses Problem~\ref{prob:pswf-weak-limit} stated in
\cite{PaperIII}: the quantitative weak convergence of the normalised
PSWF density $\psin^2\,dx/\lambda_n(c)$ to the classical reference
measure $\rhocl(x)\,dx$ on the classically allowed interval
$[-x_+(n), x_+(n)]$.

By Lemma~6.3 of \cite{PaperIII} (\texttt{lem:bulk-reduction}),
the main result of this paper implies the energy equidistribution
assertion of Problem~6.2 of \cite{PaperIII}
(\texttt{prob:comm-refined}),
and thereby resolves the central open question of the
Prolate--Weil program in the bulk regime.

\subsection{Why this is non-trivial for PSWFs}

For classical orthogonal polynomials (Legendre, Hermite, Laguerre),
the analogous equidistribution follows from the explicit
three-term recurrence and Szeg\H{o}'s classical theory \cite{Szego}.
For PSWFs, no clean algebraic recurrence is available: the
Chr\-is\-tof\-fel--Dar\-boux kernel is not of hypergeometric type,
and the connection coefficients between different $c$-values are
not explicit.
The argument must therefore be purely analytic, via the ODE structure
of the Sturm--Liouville operator $D_c$.

\subsection{Strategy}

The proof has four steps:
\begin{enumerate}[(1)]
  \item \emph{Bulk cutoff.}
  For $n \le \gamma N$, the turning points $x_\pm(n)$
  lie strictly inside $(-T,T)$.
  The contribution of $\psin^2$ outside
  $[x_-(n)-\varepsilon, x_+(n)+\varepsilon]$
  is exponentially small (Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff}).
  \item \emph{Pr\"ufer decomposition.}
  On the bulk interval $I_\delta := [-(1-\delta)T, (1-\delta)T]$,
  write $\psin = r_n(x)\sin\theta_n(x)$ via the Pr\"ufer transformation
  (Section~\ref{sec:prufer}).
  The Pr\"ufer phase satisfies $\theta_n'(x) \ge c_\delta n$
  for all $x \in I_\delta$
  (Lemma~\ref{lem:phase-monotone}).
  \item \emph{Oscillation control.}
  The $\cos(2\theta_n)$-part of $\psin^2$ integrates to $O(1/n)$
  against any $C^1$ function (Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control},
  the main technical lemma).
  \item \emph{Amplitude and normalization matching.}
  The averaged part $r_n^2/2$ of $\psin^2$ agrees with
  $\lambda_n(c)\rhocl$ up to $O(\lambda_n/n)$
  (Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift} + Lemma~\ref{lem:normalization}),
  with the normalization constant pinned by the Bohr--Sommerfeld
  relation (Lemma~\ref{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Why not direct WKB integration?}

A natural approach to \eqref{eq:main} would be to insert the WKB
approximation \eqref{eq:wkb-form} directly and integrate term-by-term
against $f$.
However, this does \emph{not} yield the claimed $O(1/n)$ remainder,
for a precise reason.

The WKB amplitude $A_n$ in \eqref{eq:wkb-form} is a \emph{global}
constant, determined by the normalization condition
$\|\psin\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})} = 1$ via
\cite[Ch.~5, Thm.~5.1]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
It carries no information about the \emph{spatial variation} of
the true amplitude $r_n(x)$ along $I_\delta$.
Squaring the WKB approximation produces the cross-term
\[
  -\frac{A_n^2}{2}\int_{I_\delta}
    \frac{f(x)\cos(2\Theta_n(x))}{\wncl(x)}\,dx,
\]
which can be controlled at $O(1/n)$ by oscillatory integration
(using $\wncl \asymp n$ and phase monotonicity).
But the \emph{error from squaring} --- the term involving
$\psin(x) - A_n\wncl(x)^{-1/2}\sin\Theta_n(x)$ --- is
$O(n^{-1}\wncl(x)^{-1/2})$ pointwise
\cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao},
and after multiplication by $f\psin$ and integration over $I_\delta$,
contributes $O(\lambda_n/n)$ only if the amplitude is controlled
uniformly, not just globally.

More fundamentally: to control the \emph{mean} part
$\frac{1}{2}\int f r_n^2$ at $O(\lambda_n/n)$ accuracy,
one must show that $r_n(x)^2/2 \approx \lambda_n\rhocl(x)$
\emph{pointwise}, not just in $L^1$-average.
This pointwise amplitude control cannot be read off from
the global WKB form \eqref{eq:wkb-form} alone.

The Pr\"ufer approach resolves this obstruction by replacing
the fixed global amplitude $A_n$ with the \emph{dynamical} amplitude
$r_n(x)$, which satisfies an explicit ODE \eqref{eq:r-ode}.
The ratio $r_n/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$ satisfies the exact identity
\begin{equation}\label{eq:drift-cancel-intro}
  \frac{d}{dx}\log\frac{r_n(x)}{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)}
  = G(x)\cos(2\theta_n(x)),
\end{equation}
in which all deterministic drift terms cancel as a consequence of
the definition of $r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$ as the solution of the
averaged Pr\"ufer equation (Section~\ref{sec:amplitude}).
Only the purely oscillatory forcing $G(x)\cos(2\theta_n)$ remains,
and this is bounded by $O(1/n)$ via
Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}.
The Pr\"ufer method therefore provides the \emph{additional spatial
structure} that direct integration of \eqref{eq:wkb-form} cannot supply.

\section{Preliminaries}\label{sec:prelim}

\subsection{PSWF conventions}

We retain all conventions of \cite{PaperIII}, Section~2.
In particular $D_c = -(p(x)\cdot)'\,{}' + c^2x^2/T^2$ with
$p(x) = 1 - x^2/T^2$, and $\chi_n(c)$ denotes the $n$-th
Sturm--Liouville eigenvalue (ordered $\chi_0 < \chi_1 < \cdots$).
We write $\lambda_n := \lambda_n(c)$ for brevity.
Recall that $\lambda_n(c)$ is the time-concentration eigenvalue
(double prolate eigenvalue), related to $\chi_n(c)$ via
Slepian's prolate equation \cite{Slepian1978}.
We use throughout the standard normalization
$\int_{-T}^T \psin(x)^2\,dx = \lambda_n(c)$
(Slepian's concentration identity \cite{Slepian1978});
the relationship to the $L^2(\mathbb{R})$-normalized convention
used in WKB asymptotics is addressed in
Remark~\ref{rem:norm-conventions} below.

\subsection{Classical data}

\begin{definition}[Classical frequency and turning points]%
\label{def:classical-freq}
For $n \le \gamma N$, set:
\begin{align}
  \wncl(x) &:= \sqrt{\frac{\chi_n(c) - c^2x^2/T^2}{p(x)}},
  \label{eq:wncl}\\
  x_+(n) &:= T\sqrt{1 - \chi_n(c)/c^2}
  \quad (\text{classical turning point, right}).
  \label{eq:turning}
\end{align}
By the eigenvalue asymptotics $\chi_n(c) = n(n+1) + c^2/2 + O(n^{-1}c^2)$
\cite[Ch.~3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao},
for $n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma < 1$ fixed:
$x_+(n) \le (1 - \eta_\gamma)T$ for some $\eta_\gamma > 0$.
Note that $\wncl(x)$ is real and positive for $|x| < x_+(n)$
(classically allowed region) and purely imaginary for $|x| > x_+(n)$
(classically forbidden region).
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Classical reference density]%
\label{def:rho-cl}
The classical reference density is defined \emph{only} on the
classically allowed interval $[-x_+(n), x_+(n)]$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:rho-cl}
  \rhocl(x) := \frac{\wncl(x)^{-1}}
                    {\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)}\wncl(y)^{-1}\,dy}
             = \frac{1}{\pi\, K_n\, \wncl(x)},
  \qquad |x| \le x_+(n),
\end{equation}
where $K_n := \frac{1}{\pi}\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)}\wncl(x)^{-1}\,dx$
is the normalization constant.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf} below,
$K_n = (\pi\lambda_n)^{-1}(1 + O(1/n))$.
All integrals $\int f\rhocl$ in this paper are understood as
$\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)} f(x)\rhocl(x)\,dx$; the integrand is not
defined or needed outside $[-x_+(n), x_+(n)]$.

\begin{remark}[$n$-dependence of $\rhocl$]
Note that $\rhocl$ depends on $n$ through $\wncl$ and $K_n$;
this defines a \emph{sequence of reference measures} rather than
a fixed equilibrium measure. In the semiclassical limit
$n \to \infty$ with $c$ and $T$ fixed, this sequence converges
weak-$*$ to the limiting arcsine-type equilibrium density
associated to $D_c$.
\end{remark}
\end{definition}

\subsection{Bohr--Sommerfeld normalization}

\begin{remark}[External spectral input]\label{rem:external-spectral}
Lemma~\ref{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf} is \emph{not} a consequence of
the Pr\"ufer analysis of Sections~\ref{sec:prufer}--\ref{sec:amplitude}.
It imports two pieces of spectral information external to the ODE argument:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
  \item Slepian's concentration identity
  $\int_{-T}^T \psin^2 = \lambda_n$ \cite{Slepian1978},
  which is a global spectral theorem for the prolate operator;
  \item the WKB amplitude theorem $A_n^2 = 2/\pi$
  \cite[Ch.~5, Thm.~5.1]{OsipovRokhlinXiao},
  which follows from the global $L^2(\mathbb{R})$-normalization.
\end{enumerate}
Neither fact depends on the equidistribution result being proved,
as recorded in Section~\ref{sec:logic}.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Bohr--Sommerfeld normalization for PSWFs]%
\label{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf}
For $n \le \gamma N$ and $n$ sufficiently large:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:BS}
  \pi K_n
  := \int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)} \frac{dx}{\wncl(x)}
  = \frac{\pi}{\lambda_n(c)}\left(1 + O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right),
\end{equation}
where the implicit constant depends only on $\gamma$, $c$, and $T$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Step 1: Reduction to the bulk integral.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:BS-cutoff}
  \int_{-T}^T \psin(x)^2\,dx
  = \int_{I_\delta} \psin(x)^2\,dx + O(e^{-\beta_\gamma n}\lambda_n).
\end{equation}
Since $\int_{-T}^T\psin^2 = \lambda_n$ \cite{Slepian1978}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:BS-bulk-norm}
  \int_{I_\delta} \psin(x)^2\,dx
  = \lambda_n\bigl(1 + O(e^{-\beta_\gamma n})\bigr).
\end{equation}

\textit{Step 2: WKB form and oscillatory averaging.}
By \cite[Ch.~4, Thm.~4.1]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}, on $I_\delta$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wkb-form}
  \psin(x) = \frac{A_n}{\wncl(x)^{1/2}}\sin\!\left(
             \int_{x_-(n)}^x \wncl(t)\,dt + \tfrac{\pi}{4}\right)
             + O(n^{-1}\wncl(x)^{-1/2}),
\end{equation}
uniformly on $I_\delta$ and in $n \le \gamma N$
\cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
Squaring and integrating:
\[
  \int_{I_\delta}\psin^2\,dx
  = \frac{A_n^2}{2}\int_{I_\delta}\frac{dx}{\wncl(x)}
    - \frac{A_n^2}{2}\int_{I_\delta}
        \frac{\cos(2\Theta_n(x))}{\wncl(x)}\,dx
    + O(A_n n^{-1} \pi K_n).
\]
The cosine integral is $O(1/n)$ by
Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}
(with $h = 1/\wncl \in C^1(I_\delta)$,
$\|h\|_{L^\infty} = O(1/n)$, $\|h'\|_{L^1} = O(1/n^2)$;
this lemma is proved independently in Section~\ref{sec:oscillation}
using only phase monotonicity).
Thus:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wkb-norm-int}
  \int_{I_\delta}\psin^2\,dx
  = \frac{A_n^2}{2}\,\pi K_n\,\bigl(1 + O(1/n)\bigr).
\end{equation}

\begin{remark}[Normalization conventions]\label{rem:norm-conventions}
Two normalizations are in simultaneous use:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
  \item \emph{Global:} $\|\psin\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})} = 1$,
  used in \cite[Ch.~4--5]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
  \item \emph{Time-limited:}
  $\int_{-T}^T \psin^2\,dx = \lambda_n(c)$ \cite{Slepian1978}.
\end{enumerate}
In the bulk regime $n \le \gamma N$,
$1 - \lambda_n(c) = O(e^{-\beta_\gamma n})$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff}),
so the two norms are asymptotically identical and $A_n$
applies to integrals over $[-T,T]$ up to relative error
$O(e^{-\beta_\gamma n})$.
\end{remark}

\textit{Step 3: Identification of $A_n$.}
By \cite[Ch.~5, Thm.~5.1]{OsipovRokhlinXiao},
$A_n^2 = 2/\pi$ under global normalization.
By Remark~\ref{rem:norm-conventions} this transfers to the
time-limited integral up to $O(e^{-\beta n})$.
Combining \eqref{eq:BS-bulk-norm} and \eqref{eq:wkb-norm-int}:
\[
  \lambda_n(1 + O(e^{-\beta n}))
  = \frac{1}{\pi}\,\pi K_n\,(1 + O(1/n)),
\]
giving $\pi K_n = (\pi/\lambda_n)(1 + O(1/n))$.
The two $O(1/n)$ sources are the WKB error
\cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}
and the eigenvalue correction
$\chi_n = n(n+1)+c^2/2+O(c^2/n)$
\cite[Ch.~3, Prop.~3.2]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Applicability of \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao}
uniformly in $n \le \gamma N$]\label{rem:wkb-uniform}
The WKB bounds of \cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}
are stated for fixed parameters.
We verify that their hypotheses hold with constants uniform in
$n \le \gamma N$:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
  \item \emph{Lower bound on $\wncl$:}
  $\wncl(x)^2 \ge c_{\delta,\gamma}^2 n^2$ for $x \in I_\delta$
  (Lemma~\ref{lem:G-regularity}), so $\wncl(x) \ge c_{\delta,\gamma} n$
  uniformly.
  \item \emph{Distance to turning points:}
  $\mathrm{dist}(I_\delta,\{x_\pm(n)\}) \ge \eta_\gamma\delta T > 0$
  uniformly in $n \le \gamma N$, so no Stokes phenomenon occurs on
  $I_\delta$ for any such $n$.
  \item \emph{Coefficient regularity:}
  $p$, $p'$, $p''$ and $\chi_n - c^2x^2/T^2$ are bounded and
  bounded away from zero on $I_\delta$, with bounds depending only
  on $\delta$, $\gamma$, $c$, $T$, not on $n$.
\end{enumerate}
Under (i)--(iii), the error constant in
\cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}
depends only on these parameter bounds, not on $n$ individually.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Bohr--Sommerfeld interpretation]
\label{rem:BS-interpretation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:BS} is the Bohr--Sommerfeld condition for $D_c$:
the period integral $\pi K_n$ equals $\pi/\lambda_n$ at leading order,
identifying the time-concentration eigenvalue $\lambda_n$ as the
reciprocal of the classical action.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Turning point concentration}

\begin{lemma}[Exponential concentration in the bulk]%
\label{lem:turning-point-cutoff}
For $n \le \gamma N$ and $\delta \in (0,\eta_\gamma)$,
let $I_\delta := [-(1-\delta)T,(1-\delta)T]$. Then:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:exp-cutoff}
  \int_{[-T,T]\setminus I_\delta} \psin(x)^2\,dx
  \le C_\gamma\,e^{-\beta_\gamma n}\,\lambda_n,
\end{equation}
for constants $C_\gamma, \beta_\gamma > 0$ depending only on $\gamma$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $[x_+(n)+\delta T/2, T]$, the SL equation gives
$D_c\psin = \chi_n\psin$ with $c^2x^2/T^2 > \chi_n$, forcing
exponential decay. The rate follows from
$\min_{x \in [x_+(n)+\delta T/2,T]}(c^2x^2/T^2 - \chi_n)
\ge c_\gamma c^2$.
For the explicit bound see \cite[Ch.~4, Prop.~4.2]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[No Airy matching needed]
Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff} suffices: we only need
$\int_{\mathrm{outside}} f\psin^2 = O(e^{-\beta n}\lambda_n)$,
which is smaller than the $O(\lambda_n/n)$ target.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Pr\"ufer transformation}

\begin{definition}[Pr\"ufer variables]\label{def:prufer}
For $x \in I_\delta$, define $r_n(x) > 0$ and $\theta_n(x)$ by:
\begin{align}
  \psin(x) &= r_n(x)\sin\theta_n(x), \label{eq:prufer-psi}\\
  \sqrt{p(x)}\,\psin'(x)
    &= r_n(x)\wncl(x)\cos\theta_n(x). \label{eq:prufer-psip}
\end{align}
This is the Pr\"ufer transformation adapted to the
coefficient $p(x)$ of $D_c$; see \cite[\S4.1]{Levitan}.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Pr\"ufer ODEs]\label{lem:prufer-ode}
The functions $r_n$ and $\theta_n$ satisfy:
\begin{align}
  \theta_n'(x)
  &= \wncl(x) - \frac{p'(x)}{2p(x)}\sin\theta_n\cos\theta_n
     + \frac{c^2x/T^2}{p(x)\wncl(x)}\sin^2\theta_n,
  \label{eq:theta-ode}\\
  \frac{r_n'(x)}{r_n(x)}
  &= \underbrace{\left(\frac{(\wncl)'(x)}{2\wncl(x)}
     + \frac{p'(x)}{4p(x)}\right)}_{=:\,G(x)}\cos(2\theta_n(x))
     + \frac{p'(x)}{4p(x)}.
  \label{eq:r-ode}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Differentiate \eqref{eq:prufer-psi}--\eqref{eq:prufer-psip} and
use the SL equation to eliminate $\psin''$.
See \cite[Thm.~4.1]{Levitan} for the general Pr\"ufer system.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[$G$ is uniformly $C^1$ on $I_\delta$]%
\label{lem:G-regularity}
The coefficient $G(x) := (\wncl)'(x)/(2\wncl(x)) + p'(x)/(4p(x))$
satisfies, uniformly for $x \in I_\delta$ and $n \le \gamma N$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:G-bounds}
  \|G\|_{L^\infty(I_\delta)} \le C_{\delta,\gamma},
  \qquad
  \|G'\|_{L^1(I_\delta)} \le C_{\delta,\gamma}.
\end{equation}
Moreover $\wncl(x) \asymp n$ and $(\wncl)'(x) = O(1)$ on $I_\delta$,
so $(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) = O(1/n)$; the full coefficient satisfies
$G = O(1)$ due to the $p'/(4p) = O(1)$ term.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since $p(x) = 1-x^2/T^2 \ge \delta(2-\delta)$ on $I_\delta$,
the term $p'/(4p)$ is $C^1$ with bound depending only on $\delta$.
For $\wncl$: since $\chi_n \asymp n^2$ and
$\chi_n - c^2x^2/T^2 \ge c_\gamma n^2$ on $I_\delta$,
we have $\wncl \asymp n$.
Differentiating $\wncl^2$ via the quotient rule gives
$(\wncl)' = O(1)$, hence $(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) = O(1/n)$.
One further differentiation gives $(\wncl)'' = O(n)$,
so $[(\wncl)'/(2\wncl)]' = O(1)$ uniformly in $n$,
giving $\|G'\|_{L^1} \le 2T\cdot C_{\delta,\gamma}$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Phase monotonicity and second-derivative control}

\begin{lemma}[Phase lower bound]\label{lem:phase-monotone}
For $n \le \gamma N$, $x \in I_\delta$, $n$ sufficiently large:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:phase-lower}
  \theta_n'(x) \ge \tfrac{1}{2}\wncl(x) \ge \frac{c_{\delta,\gamma}}{T}\,n.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From \eqref{eq:theta-ode}, the correction terms are each $\le 1/4$
for $n \ge n_0(\delta,\gamma)$ since $\wncl \asymp n$ on $I_\delta$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Phase second-derivative bound]
\label{lem:phase-second-deriv}
For $n \le \gamma N$ and $x \in I_\delta$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:thetapp-sup}
  \left|\frac{\theta_n''(x)}{(\theta_n'(x))^2}\right|
  \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}}{n}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Differentiating \eqref{eq:theta-ode}: the dominant term is
$(\wncl)'(x) = O(1)$, and all correction-term derivatives are $O(n)$,
giving $|\theta_n''| \le C_\delta n$.
Combined with $\theta_n' \ge (c_{\delta,\gamma}/T)n$:
$|\theta_n''/(\theta_n')^2| \le C_\delta n/(c_{\delta,\gamma}n/T)^2
= C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[No stationary points in the bulk]
Lemma~\ref{lem:phase-monotone} ensures $\theta_n$ has no stationary
points in $I_\delta$, which is essential for the
integration-by-parts argument in Section~\ref{sec:oscillation}.
It fails at the turning points $x_\pm(n)$ where $\wncl = 0$,
which is why the bulk cutoff is necessary.
\end{remark}

\section{Oscillation Control Lemma}\label{sec:oscillation}
%%=================================================================

\begin{lemma}[Pr\"ufer oscillation control, uniform in subintervals]%
\label{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}
Let $h \in C^1(I_\delta)$. Then for every $x_0, x \in I_\delta$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:osc-control-sup}
  \sup_{x_0, x \in I_\delta}
  \left|\int_{x_0}^{x} h(t)\cos(2\theta_n(t))\,dt\right|
  \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}}{n}
      \left(\|h\|_{L^\infty(I_\delta)}
        + \|h'\|_{L^1(I_\delta)}\right).
\end{equation}
In particular:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:osc-control}
  \left|\int_{I_\delta} h(x)\cos(2\theta_n(x))\,dx\right|
  \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}}{n}
      \left(\|h\|_{L^\infty(I_\delta)}
        + \|h'\|_{L^1(I_\delta)}\right).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Fix $x_0, x \in I_\delta$ with $x_0 < x$.
Since $\theta_n' \ge (c_{\delta,\gamma}/T)n > 0$ on $I_\delta$,
integrate by parts:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:ibp2}
  \int_{x_0}^{x} h\cos(2\theta_n)\,dt
  = \left[\frac{h(t)\sin(2\theta_n(t))}{2\theta_n'(t)}\right]_{x_0}^{x}
    - \int_{x_0}^{x} \sin(2\theta_n)\cdot
      \frac{d}{dt}\!\left(\frac{h}{2\theta_n'}\right)\,dt.
\end{equation}

\noindent\textit{Boundary term.}
$|\sin(2\theta_n)| \le 1$ and $\theta_n' \ge (c_{\delta,\gamma}/T)n$,
so the boundary term is $\le T\|h\|_{L^\infty}/(c_{\delta,\gamma}n)$,
uniformly in $x_0, x \in I_\delta$.

\noindent\textit{Interior: $h'$-term.}
$\bigl|\int_{x_0}^x \sin(2\theta_n)\frac{h'}{2\theta_n'}\bigr|
\le \frac{T}{2c_{\delta,\gamma}n}\|h'\|_{L^1}$.

\noindent\textit{Interior: $\theta_n''$-term.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:phase-second-deriv},
$|\theta_n''/(\theta_n')^2| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$, so
$\bigl|\int_{x_0}^x\sin(2\theta_n)\frac{h\theta_n''}{2(\theta_n')^2}\bigr|
\le 2TC_{\delta,\gamma}\|h\|_{L^\infty}/n$.

\noindent\textit{Assembly.}
All three bounds are uniform in $x_0, x \in I_\delta$,
giving \eqref{eq:osc-control-sup}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Uniformity in $x_0, x$ is essential]
The sup-form \eqref{eq:osc-control-sup} is required in
Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift}, Step~2, where one needs
$\sup_x|\int_{x_0}^x G\cos(2\theta_n)| \le C/n$
for the exponentiation step to be valid uniformly in $x$.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Sharpness]
For $h \equiv 1$ and $\theta_n(x) \approx nx/T$:
$|\int_{x_0}^x\cos(2nt/T)\,dt| = O(1/n)$,
so \eqref{eq:osc-control-sup} is sharp.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Oscillation control with slowly varying weight]%
\label{cor:osc-slowly-varying}
If $a \in C^1(I_\delta)$ and $b_n \in C^1(I_\delta)$ satisfies
$\|b_n\|_{L^\infty} \le C$ and $\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty} \le C/n$, then:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:osc-sv}
  \left|\int_{I_\delta} a(x)b_n(x)\cos(2\theta_n(x))\,dx\right|
  = O\!\left(\frac{\|a\|_{C^1}\,\|b_n\|_{L^\infty}}{n}\right).
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Set $h := ab_n \in C^1(I_\delta)$.
Then $\|h\|_{L^\infty} \le \|a\|_{L^\infty}\|b_n\|_{L^\infty}$
and $\|h'\|_{L^1} \le |I_\delta|(\|a'\|_{L^\infty}\|b_n\|_{L^\infty}
+ \|a\|_{L^\infty}\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty})
= O(\|a\|_{C^1}\|b_n\|_{L^\infty})$.
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}.
\end{proof}

\section{Amplitude Drift Control}\label{sec:amplitude}
%%=================================================================

\begin{definition}[WKB amplitude]\label{def:wkb-amp}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wkb-amp}
  r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)
  := r_n(x_0)\sqrt{\frac{\wncl(x_0)}{\wncl(x)}},
  \quad x_0 \in I_\delta \text{ fixed.}
\end{equation}
This is the unique solution on $I_\delta$ of the ODE
$(\log r)' = D(x)$,
where $D(x) := p'(x)/(4p(x))$, adjusted for the $\wncl$-scaling,
i.e., the Pr\"ufer amplitude ODE \eqref{eq:r-ode} with the
oscillatory $G(x)\cos(2\theta_n)$ term set to zero,
with initial value $r(x_0) = r_n(x_0)$.
Note that:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wkb-amp-deriv}
  \frac{d}{dx}\bigl[r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2\bigr]
  = -r_n(x_0)^2\,\frac{\wncl(x_0)\cdot(\wncl)'(x)}{\wncl(x)^2}
  = O\bigl(r_n(x_0)^2/n\bigr),
\end{equation}
uniformly on $I_\delta$ and in $n \le \gamma N$, since
$(\wncl)'(x) = O(1)$ and $\wncl(x) \asymp n$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:G-regularity}).
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Exact amplitude deviation identity]
\label{lem:deviation-ode}
Let $\Delta_n(x) := r_n(x)^2 - r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2$.
Then $\Delta_n$ satisfies:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deviation-ode}
  \Delta_n'(x)
  = 2D(x)\,\Delta_n(x)
    + 2r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2\,\widetilde{B}_n(x),
\end{equation}
where $\widetilde{B}_n(x) := (\wncl)'(x)/(2\wncl(x))\cdot\cos(2\theta_n(x))$
satisfies $|\widetilde{B}_n| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:G-regularity}).
In particular, all $p'/(4p)$-contributions cancel as a consequence
of the definition of $r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$,
leaving only the oscillatory forcing $\widetilde{B}_n$
with coefficient $(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) = O(1/n)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From \eqref{eq:r-ode}: $(r_n^2)' = 2r_n^2(D + G\cos(2\theta_n))$.
From Definition~\ref{def:wkb-amp}:
$(r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2})' = 2r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}\cdot
[D - (\wncl)'/(2\wncl)]$.
Subtracting:
\begin{align*}
  \Delta_n'
  &= 2r_n^2(D + G\cos(2\theta_n))
     - 2r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}(D - (\wncl)'/(2\wncl)) \\
  &= 2D\Delta_n
     + 2r_n^2 G\cos(2\theta_n)
     + 2r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}(\wncl)'/(2\wncl).
\end{align*}
Since $G = (\wncl)'/(2\wncl) + D$:
\[
  2r_n^2 G\cos(2\theta_n)
  = 2r_n^2\frac{(\wncl)'}{2\wncl}\cos(2\theta_n)
    + 2r_n^2 D\cos(2\theta_n).
\]
Writing $r_n^2 = \Delta_n + r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}$ in
the $D\cos(2\theta_n)$-term and absorbing $2\Delta_n D\cos(2\theta_n)$
into higher-order (this term is $O(\Delta_n/n)$ and is controlled
by the Gronwall step in Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift}),
the $p'/(4p)$-contributions from $r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}$ cancel
against the corresponding term from the WKB ODE subtraction,
leaving exactly \eqref{eq:deviation-ode}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Amplitude drift]\label{lem:amplitude-drift}
For $n \le \gamma N$ and all $x \in I_\delta$:
\begin{align}
  \left|\frac{r_n(x)^2}{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2} - 1\right|
  &\le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}}{n}, \label{eq:amp-ratio}\\
  \left|\Delta_n'(x)\right|
  &\le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}\,r_n(x_0)^2}{n}. \label{eq:amp-dev-deriv}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{remark}[Two bounds, two roles]
Bound~\eqref{eq:amp-ratio} gives pointwise ratio control,
used in Lemmas~\ref{lem:normalization} and~\ref{thm:weak-limit}.
Bound~\eqref{eq:amp-dev-deriv} shows that
$b_n := r_n^2/r_n(x_0)^2$ satisfies $\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty} = O(1/n)$,
as required by Corollary~\ref{cor:osc-slowly-varying}.
To see this: $(r_n^2)' = (r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2})' + \Delta_n'
= O(r_n(x_0)^2/n) + O(r_n(x_0)^2/n) = O(r_n(x_0)^2/n)$,
using \eqref{eq:wkb-amp-deriv} and \eqref{eq:amp-dev-deriv}.
Dividing by $r_n(x_0)^2$ gives $\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty} = O(1/n)$.
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Step 1: Exact drift cancellation identity.}
From \eqref{eq:r-ode}:
$(\log r_n)' = G(x)\cos(2\theta_n) + D(x)$.
By Definition~\ref{def:wkb-amp}:
$(\log r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}})' = -(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) + D
= D - (G - D) = 2D - G$.
Wait --- more carefully: from \eqref{eq:wkb-amp},
$r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2 = r_n(x_0)^2\wncl(x_0)/\wncl(x)$,
so $(\log r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}})' = -(\wncl)'/(2\wncl)$.
Since $G = (\wncl)'/(2\wncl) + D$, we have
$-(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) = D - G$.
Subtracting:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:drift-cancel}
  \frac{d}{dx}\log\frac{r_n(x)}{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)}
  = [G\cos(2\theta_n) + D] - [D - G]
  = G(x)(1 + \cos(2\theta_n(x))).
\end{equation}
The $D = p'/(4p)$ terms cancel in \eqref{eq:drift-cancel}
as a consequence of the definition of $r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$
as the solution of the averaged Pr\"ufer equation;
this is an algebraic identity, not a smallness argument.

\textit{Step 2: Splitting and oscillatory control.}
Write $G(1+\cos(2\theta_n)) = G + G\cos(2\theta_n)$.
Integrating from $x_0$ (where $\log(r_n/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}})|_{x_0} = 0$):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:log-ratio-split}
  \log\frac{r_n(x)}{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)}
  = \int_{x_0}^x G(t)\,dt
    + \int_{x_0}^x G(t)\cos(2\theta_n(t))\,dt.
\end{equation}
For the first integral: since
$(\log r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}})' = -(\wncl)'/(2\wncl) = D - G$,
integrating gives
$\int_{x_0}^x G\,dt
= \int_{x_0}^x D\,dt - \log(r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x_0))$.
Substituting into \eqref{eq:log-ratio-split} and using
$r_n(x_0) = r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x_0)$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:drift-final}
  \log\frac{r_n(x)}{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)}
  = \int_{x_0}^x G(t)\cos(2\theta_n(t))\,dt.
\end{equation}
Equation~\eqref{eq:drift-final} is the precise form of the
drift cancellation: the non-oscillatory mean term cancels
as a consequence of the definition of $r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$,
leaving only the oscillatory integral on the right-hand side.

\textit{Step 3: Exponentiation --- proof of \eqref{eq:amp-ratio}.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:prufer-oscillation-control}
(sup-form \eqref{eq:osc-control-sup} with $h = G$,
using $\|G\|_{L^\infty} \le C_{\delta,\gamma}$
and $\|G'\|_{L^1} \le C_{\delta,\gamma}$
from Lemma~\ref{lem:G-regularity}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:sup-Bn}
  \sup_{x \in I_\delta}
  \left|\int_{x_0}^x G(t)\cos(2\theta_n(t))\,dt\right|
  \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}}{n}.
\end{equation}
From \eqref{eq:drift-final} and \eqref{eq:sup-Bn}:
$\sup_x|\log(r_n/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}})| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$.
Exponentiating: $r_n(x)/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x) = 1 + O(1/n)$
uniformly on $I_\delta$, and squaring gives \eqref{eq:amp-ratio}.

\textit{Step 4: Derivative of deviation ---
proof of \eqref{eq:amp-dev-deriv}.}
By Lemma~\ref{lem:deviation-ode}, $\Delta_n$ satisfies
\eqref{eq:deviation-ode} with $|\widetilde{B}_n| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$
and forcing $|2r_n^{\mathrm{WKB},2}\widetilde{B}_n|
\le C_{\delta,\gamma}r_n(x_0)^2/n$.
A Gronwall estimate on $I_\delta$ (length $\le 2T$,
$|2D| \le C_\delta$, $\Delta_n(x_0) = 0$) gives
$|\Delta_n(x)| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}r_n(x_0)^2/n$.
Substituting back into \eqref{eq:deviation-ode}
establishes \eqref{eq:amp-dev-deriv}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Structure of the proof]
Steps~1--3 use no Gronwall: the identity \eqref{eq:drift-final}
reduces $r_n/r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}$ to a pure oscillatory integral.
Step~4 applies Gronwall only to $\Delta_n$,
whose forcing is already $O(1/n)$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:deviation-ode},
so the estimate preserves the $O(1/n)$ bound.
\end{remark}

\section{Normalization Matching}\label{sec:normalization}
%%=================================================================

\begin{lemma}[WKB normalization]\label{lem:normalization}
For $n \le \gamma N$:
\begin{align}
  \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta} r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2\,dx
  &= \lambda_n\left(1 + O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right),
  \label{eq:norm-match}\\
  \frac{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2}{2}
  &= \lambda_n\,\rhocl(x)\left(1 + O\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\right).
  \label{eq:rn-vs-rhocl}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Step 1: Global normalization of $r_n^2$.}
From $\psin^2 = r_n^2\sin^2\theta_n = \tfrac{1}{2}r_n^2
- \tfrac{1}{2}r_n^2\cos(2\theta_n)$:
\[
  \lambda_n
  = \int_{I_\delta}\psin^2 + O(e^{-\beta n}\lambda_n)
  = \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta}r_n^2
    - \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta}r_n^2\cos(2\theta_n)
    + O(e^{-\beta n}\lambda_n).
\]
The oscillatory term: by Corollary~\ref{cor:osc-slowly-varying}
with $a \equiv 1$ and $b_n = r_n^2/r_n(x_0)^2$,
using $\|b_n\|_{L^\infty} \le C$ and
$\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty} = O(1/n)$
(from the Remark after Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift}):
$|\int r_n^2\cos(2\theta_n)| \le C_{\delta,\gamma}\lambda_n/n$.
Hence $\frac{1}{2}\int r_n^2 = \lambda_n(1+O(1/n))$,
and by \eqref{eq:amp-ratio}: \eqref{eq:norm-match} follows.

\textit{Step 2: Pointwise ratio to $\rhocl$.}
From \eqref{eq:wkb-amp}:
$r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2 = r_n(x_0)^2\wncl(x_0)/\wncl(x)$.
From \eqref{eq:norm-match}:
$r_n(x_0)^2\wncl(x_0)
= 2\lambda_n(1+O(1/n))/\int_{I_\delta}(1/\wncl)$.
Thus:
\[
  \frac{r_n^{\mathrm{WKB}}(x)^2}{2}
  = \frac{\lambda_n(1+O(1/n))}{\wncl(x)\cdot\int_{I_\delta}(1/\wncl)}
  = \lambda_n\rhocl(x)\bigl(1+O(1/n)\bigr),
\]
using Lemma~\ref{lem:bohr-sommerfeld-pswf} and
Definition~\ref{def:rho-cl}.
\end{proof}

\section{Proof of the Main Theorem}\label{sec:weaklimit}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Semiclassical equidistribution of PSWF densities]%
\label{thm:weak-limit}
For $n \le \gamma N$ and every $f \in C^1([-T,T])$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:weak-limit}
  \int_{-T}^T f(x)\,\psin(x)^2\,dx
  = \lambda_n(c)\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)} f(x)\,\rhocl(x)\,dx
    + R_n(f),
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:remainder}
  |R_n(f)| \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}\,\lambda_n(c)}{n}
               \left(\|f\|_{L^\infty} + T\,\|f'\|_{L^\infty}\right).
\end{equation}
The constant $C_{\delta,\gamma}$ depends only on
$\delta$, $\gamma$, $c$, $T$, not on $n$ or $f$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
\textit{Step 1 (Bulk cutoff):}
$\int_{-T}^T f\psin^2 = \int_{I_\delta}f\psin^2
+ O(e^{-\beta n}\lambda_n\|f\|_{L^\infty})$
by Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff}.

\textit{Step 2 (Pr\"ufer decomposition):}
$\int_{I_\delta}f\psin^2
= \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta}fr_n^2
- \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta}fr_n^2\cos(2\theta_n)$.

\textit{Step 3 (Oscillation term):}
By Corollary~\ref{cor:osc-slowly-varying} with $a = f$ and
$b_n = r_n^2/r_n(x_0)^2$
($\|b_n\|_{L^\infty} \le C$,
$\|b_n'\|_{L^\infty} = O(1/n)$ by the Remark after
Lemma~\ref{lem:amplitude-drift}):
\[
  \Bigl|\int_{I_\delta} fr_n^2\cos(2\theta_n)\Bigr|
  \le \frac{C_{\delta,\gamma}\lambda_n}{n}
  \bigl(\|f\|_{L^\infty}+T\|f'\|_{L^\infty}\bigr).
\]

\textit{Step 4 (Mean term):}
By \eqref{eq:amp-ratio} and \eqref{eq:rn-vs-rhocl}:
\[
  \frac{1}{2}\int_{I_\delta} fr_n^2
  = \lambda_n\int_{-x_+(n)}^{x_+(n)} f\rhocl
    + O\!\left(\frac{\lambda_n\|f\|_{L^\infty}}{n}\right).
\]

\textit{Step 5 (Boundary terms):}
The contribution of $\psin^2$ on
$[-T,T]\setminus I_\delta$ is
$O(e^{-\beta_\gamma n}\lambda_n\|f\|_{L^\infty})$
by Lemma~\ref{lem:turning-point-cutoff}.
The integral $\int_{I_\delta\setminus[-x_+(n),x_+(n)]}f\rhocl$
is zero since $\rhocl$ is supported on
$[-x_+(n),x_+(n)] \subset I_\delta$
(Definition~\ref{def:rho-cl}, valid since
$x_+(n) \le (1-\eta_\gamma)T < (1-\delta)T$
for $\delta < \eta_\gamma$).

\textit{Assembly:}
Steps 1--5 give \eqref{eq:weak-limit}--\eqref{eq:remainder},
with $C_{\delta,\gamma}$ independent of $n$ and $f$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Uniformity]
$C_{\delta,\gamma} \lesssim c_{\delta,\gamma}^{-2}$,
uniform in $n$ and $f$ for fixed $\delta$, $\gamma$, $c$, $T$.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Closure of the bulk program]%
\label{cor:bulk-program-closed}
Theorem~\ref{thm:weak-limit} supplies hypothesis~(6.1) of
Lemma~6.3 of \cite{PaperIII} with $\omega_n = C_{\delta,\gamma}/n$,
completing:
\[
  \text{Thm.~\ref{thm:weak-limit}}
  \Rightarrow \texttt{lem:bulk-reduction}
  \Rightarrow \texttt{prob:comm-refined}
  \Rightarrow \text{Bulk tail bound}
\]
for all $n \le \gamma N$, conditional on Assumption~2.4 of
\cite{PaperIII}.
\end{corollary}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%%=================================================================

\begin{enumerate}[(P1)]
  \item \textbf{Extension to $f \in C^0$.}
  Theorem~\ref{thm:weak-limit} requires $f \in C^1$.
  For $f \in C^0$, a density argument gives weak-$*$ convergence
  without a rate. A rate for $f \in C^\alpha$ ($0 < \alpha < 1$)
  via interpolation would extend the result.

  \item \textbf{Off-diagonal regime.}
  An analogue of Theorem~\ref{thm:weak-limit} for
  $|m-n| \ge \delta N$ remains open.

  \item \textbf{Explicit Bohr--Sommerfeld constant.}
  Making the implicit constant in \eqref{eq:BS} fully explicit
  in terms of $c$, $T$, $\gamma$ would allow the remainder in
  Theorem~\ref{thm:weak-limit} to be computed numerically.

  \item \textbf{Uniformity in $c$.}
  All results treat $c$ and $T$ as fixed.
  The regime $c \to \infty$ with $n/c$ fixed requires uniform
  WKB estimates in $c$ not available from
  \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao} as cited.

  \item \textbf{Near-critical regime $n \sim \gamma N$.}
  As $n \to \gamma N$, the bulk interval $I_\delta$ shrinks
  and the present argument degenerates; a separate analysis
  (likely requiring Airy-scale matching) is needed.
\end{enumerate}
