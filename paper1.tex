\\begin{abstract}
Let $\\PW$ be the Paley--Wiener space of $\\omega$-bandlimited functions
and $V_{N,c}:=\\operatorname{span}\\{\\psi_0^{(c)},\\dots,\\psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\\}$
the $N$-dimensional prolate subspace at time-bandwidth product $c=\\omega T$.
We study the stability of the discrete sampling form
$\\QM(f)=\\sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2$ as a finite frame operator on $V_{N,c}$.

We prove coercivity for random sampling configurations and for weighted sampling
models satisfying a Marcinkiewicz--Zygmund lower frame bound.
Our main structural result is an exact defect decomposition
$\\EN = M^*(I-D)M + (G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c} - G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c})$,
which reduces frame stability to the simultaneous control of a
weighted-frame defect and a spectral mismatch defect.

The central obstacle to making this reduction constructive is a
PSWF-specific off-diagonal decay mechanism for discrete quadrature defect matrices,
which we isolate as an explicit conjecture:
in the transition regime $N\\sim c$, Xiao--Rokhlin--Yarvin quadrature nodes
are expected to satisfy $|E_{mn}|\\le\\varepsilon(N,c)\\,e^{-\\alpha|m-n|}$
(banded decay hypothesis).
We prove that this conjecture, if established, implies a quantitative
Marcinkiewicz--Zygmund frame inequality on $V_{N,c}$ with explicit bounds $A(N,c)$, $B(N,c)$.
All results are finite-dimensional and restricted to fixed parameters $(N,c)$.
\\end{abstract}

\\section{Introduction}

The prolate spheroidal wave functions (PSWFs) provide an orthonormal basis
for the finite-dimensional subspace $V_{N,c}$ of the Paley--Wiener space $\\PW$
that is optimally concentrated in a time--frequency window \\cite{Slepian1978,BonamiKaroui}.
From the perspective of frame theory and sampling in reproducing kernel Hilbert spaces,
the weighted evaluation map $f\\mapsto(\\sqrt{w_j}f(p_j))_j$ defines a finite frame operator
on $V_{N,c}$, and its associated Gram matrix encodes the stability of the sampling scheme.

This paper addresses two questions.
First, which classes of sampling configurations yield coercive Gram operators on $V_{N,c}$?
Second, how does the discrete sampling Gram matrix compare to the reference spectral matrix
$G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c} = \\Kop|_{V_{N,c}}$ in the PSWF eigenbasis?
For the first question we prove coercivity for random sampling and for weighted MZ frames.
For the second, we establish an exact algebraic defect decomposition and identify the
genuine analytic obstacle: \\emph{off-diagonal decay of the quadrature defect matrix in the
PSWF eigenbasis}.

\\subsection*{Main obstacle and conjecture}
The main obstacle to constructive frame stability is not the algebraic decomposition itself,
but the spectral norm of the defect operator $\\EN$.
Standard entrywise-to-spectral-norm bounds give $\\norm{\\EN}_2\\le N\\varepsilon$,
which is too weak for the frame-stable regime condition $\\norm{\\EN}_2<\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$.
The actual content is a structural claim: in the transition regime $N\\sim c$,
the off-diagonal entries of $\\EN$ under PSWF-adapted quadrature decay rapidly in $|m-n|$,
so the effective operator norm is controlled not by the full $N\\times N$ matrix size
but by an effective bandwidth.
We isolate this as Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay} and prove that it implies
quantitative MZ frame bounds (Theorem~\\ref{thm:conditional_frame}).

All results are finite-dimensional and restricted to fixed parameters $(N,c)$.
The limit $N\\to\\infty$ and coupled limits are outside the scope of this paper.

\\section{Setup and notation}\\label{sec:setup}

\\subsection{Paley--Wiener space and concentration operator}

We use the engineering Fourier convention
$\\widehat{f}(\\xi)=\\int_{\\mathbb{R}}f(x)e^{-2\\pi ix\\xi}\\,dx$.
The \\emph{Paley--Wiener space} $\\PW$ consists of all
$f\\in L^2(\\mathbb{R})$ with Fourier support in $[-\\omega/(2\\pi),\\omega/(2\\pi)]$.
It is a reproducing-kernel Hilbert space with kernel
$K_{\\PW}(x,y)=\\tfrac{\\omega}{\\pi}\\operatorname{sinc}(\\omega(x-y)/\\pi)$
and $K_{\\PW}(x,x)=\\omega/\\pi$ \\cite[Eq.~(3)]{BonamiKaroui}.

\\begin{remark}[Fourier convention]
Many references use $\\widehat f(\\xi)=\\int f(x)e^{-i\\xi x}\\,dx$,
under which the same space has Fourier support in $[-\\omega,\\omega]$.
\\end{remark}

The \\emph{prolate concentration operator} is the self-adjoint compact operator
\\[
  \\Kop f(x)=\\int_{-T}^{T}\\frac{\\sin\\omega(x-y)}{\\pi(x-y)}f(y)\\,dy,
  \\qquad \\Kop:\\PW\\to\\PW,
\\]
with simple eigenvalues $1>\\lambda_0(c)>\\lambda_1(c)>\\cdots>0$
and PSWF eigenfunctions $\\{\\psin\\}_{n\\ge 0}$ forming an orthonormal basis of $\\PW$
\\cite{Slepian1978,BonamiKaroui}.
We set $c=\\omega T$ and
\\[
  V_{N,c}:=\\operatorname{span}\\{\\psi_0^{(c)},\\dots,\\psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\\}.
\\]
Since the PSWFs are eigenfunctions of $\\Kop$, the space $V_{N,c}$ is invariant under $\\Kop$.
The transition regime $N\\sim c$ is the natural operating range for this paper:
below this regime the PSWF eigenvalues $\\lambda_n(c)$ are near $1$ and the spectral
structure is trivial; above it they collapse toward $0$ and frame stability deteriorates.

\\subsection{Frame operator and Gram matrix}

\\begin{definition}[Weighted evaluation frame and Gram matrix]\\label{def:GramForm}
For distinct $\\mathbf{p}=(p_1,\\dots,p_N)\\subset[-T,T]$ and weights $w_j>0$,
the \\emph{evaluation matrix} is $M(\\mathbf{p},c)_{jn}:=\\psi_n^{(c)}(p_j)$
and $D:=\\operatorname{diag}(w_1,\\dots,w_N)$.
The \\emph{weighted Gram matrix} is
\\[
  G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\\mathbf{p},D) := M(\\mathbf{p},c)^* D\\, M(\\mathbf{p},c).
\\]
For the unweighted case $D=I$ we write $\\GVM:=M^*M$.

For $f=\\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}a_n\\psin\\in V_{N,c}$, the identification
$f\\leftrightarrow\\mathbf{a}$ is unitary on $(V_{N,c},\\norm{\\cdot}_{L^2})$,
so $\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2=\\norm{\\mathbf{a}}_2^2$ and
$\\QM(f):=\\sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2=\\ip{G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}\\mathbf{a}}{\\mathbf{a}}$.
\\end{definition}

\\begin{remark}[Spectral invariance]
The spectral quantities $\\lambda_{\\min}(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})$ and
$\\lambda_{\\max}(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})$ are basis-invariant on $V_{N,c}$.
\\end{remark}

\\begin{definition}[Reference spectral matrix and defect]\\label{def:ref_matrix}
The \\emph{reference spectral matrix} is
\\[
  G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}:=\\operatorname{diag}(\\lambda_0(c),\\dots,\\lambda_{N-1}(c)),
\\]
the matrix of $\\Kop|_{V_{N,c}}$ in the PSWF eigenbasis.
The \\emph{defect operator} is $\\EN:=G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\\mathbf{p},D)-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}$.
\\end{definition}

\\begin{definition}[Frame-stable regime]\\label{def:T2}
We say $\\mathbf{p}$ is in the \\emph{frame-stable regime} if there exists $\\varepsilon_N>0$ with
\\begin{enumerate}[(S.1)]
\\item $\\norm{\\EN}_2\\le\\varepsilon_N$,
\\item $\\varepsilon_N<\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$.
\\end{enumerate}
This is a near-tight perturbation condition; it is not generic.
\\end{definition}

\\subsection{The banded decay conjecture}

The following conjecture isolates the central open problem of this paper.

\\begin{conjecture}[PSWF banded decay of quadrature defect]\\label{conj:banded_decay}
Let $\\{(p_j,w_j)\\}_{j=1}^N$ be the Xiao--Rokhlin--Yarvin PSWF-adapted quadrature nodes
and weights \\cite{Xiao2001} in the transition regime $N\\sim c$.
Define the defect matrix entries
\\[
  E_{mn} := \\sum_{j=1}^N w_j\\,\\psi_m^{(c)}(p_j)\\overline{\\psi_n^{(c)}(p_j)} - \\lambda_n(c)\\delta_{mn},
  \\qquad 0\\le m,n\\le N-1.
\\]
Then there exist constants $\\alpha>0$ and $\\varepsilon(N,c)>0$, with $\\varepsilon(N,c)$
either exponentially small in $c$ or superalgebraically small in $N$ depending on the sub-regime, such that
\\[
  |E_{mn}| \\le \\varepsilon(N,c)\\, e^{-\\alpha|m-n|}
  \\qquad \\text{for all } 0\\le m,n\\le N-1.
\\]
\\end{conjecture}

\\begin{remark}[Why banded decay is the right object]
Standard entrywise-to-spectral-norm bounds give only $\\norm{\\EN}_2\\le N\\varepsilon(N,c)$,
which is too weak for the frame-stable regime when $N\\varepsilon(N,c)\\ge\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$.
The banded decay structure of Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay} implies instead
\\[
  \\norm{\\EN}_2 \\le \\norm{\\EN}_F
  \\le \\varepsilon(N,c)\\sum_{k=-(N-1)}^{N-1} e^{-\\alpha|k|}\\cdot\\sqrt{N-|k|}
  = O(\\varepsilon(N,c)\\sqrt{N}),
\\]
a gain of $\\sqrt{N}$ over the crude bound.
More importantly, when $\\varepsilon(N,c)$ decays faster than $N^{-1/2}\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$,
the frame-stable regime is triggered.
The PSWF-specific content of the conjecture is the off-diagonal damping factor
$e^{-\\alpha|m-n|}$, which encodes spectral localization of the concentration operator.
\\end{remark}

\\begin{remark}[Motivation from PSWF localization]
In the regime $N\\sim c$, PSWFs $\\psi_m^{(c)}$ and $\\psi_n^{(c)}$ with $|m-n|\\gg 1$
have essentially disjoint time--frequency concentration regions within $[-T,T]\\times[-\\omega,\\omega]$.
A high-quality quadrature rule adapted to this spectral structure should therefore produce
off-diagonal entries $E_{mn}$ that are suppressed by the oscillatory near-orthogonality
of the integrand $\\psi_m^{(c)}\\overline{\\psi_n^{(c)}}$.
Making this heuristic rigorous is the content of the conjecture.
\\end{remark}

\\section{Unisolvence and frame coercivity}\\label{sec:layerA}

\\begin{definition}[Frame condition on $V_{N,c}$]\\label{def:frame}
A sampling system $\\{(p_j,w_j)\\}_{j=1}^N$ is a \\emph{weighted frame for $V_{N,c}$}
if there exist $0<A\\le B<\\infty$ such that
\\[
  A\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2
  \\le \\sum_{j=1}^N w_j |f(p_j)|^2
  \\le B\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2
  \\qquad \\text{for all }f\\in V_{N,c}.
\\]
\\end{definition}

\\begin{definition}[Unisolvent set]\\label{def:unisolvent}
$\\mathbf{p}$ is \\emph{unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$} if the evaluation map
$f\\mapsto(f(p_1),\\dots,f(p_N))$ is bijective on $V_{N,c}$.
\\end{definition}

\\begin{remark}[Non-triviality]
For Chebyshev systems, every $N$-point set is unisolvent \\cite[Ch.~1]{KarlinStudden1966}.
Since $V_{N,c}$ is not known to be a Chebyshev system, unisolvence must be established
separately for each family.
\\end{remark}

\\begin{lemma}[Identity principle]\\label{lem:identity}
If $f\\in\\PW$ vanishes on a set with a limit point in $\\mathbb R$, then $f\\equiv 0$.
\\end{lemma}
\\begin{proof}
Every $f\\in\\PW$ is entire of exponential type; a non-discrete zero set forces $f\\equiv 0$
by the identity theorem.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{proposition}[MZ lower bound implies unisolvence]\\label{prop:mz_unisolvent}
If $\\{(p_j,w_j)\\}$ is a weighted frame for $V_{N,c}$ with lower bound $A>0$,
then $\\mathbf p$ is unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$.
\\end{proposition}
\\begin{proof}
If $f(p_j)=0$ for all $j$ then $A\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2\\le 0$, so $f\\equiv 0$
by continuity and Lemma~\\ref{lem:identity}.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{proposition}[Random sampling]\\label{prop:random}
Let $p_1,\\dots,p_N$ be i.i.d.\\ with an absolutely continuous law on $[-T,T]$.
Then $\\mathbf{p}$ is unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$ almost surely.
\\end{proposition}
\\begin{proof}
The map $(p_1,\\dots,p_N)\\mapsto\\det M(\\mathbf{p},c)$ is real-analytic.
Linear independence of the PSWFs and separation of evaluation functionals guarantee
the determinant is analytically non-degenerate, so its zero set has measure zero.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{lemma}[Pointwise RKHS bound]\\label{lem:upper}
For $f\\in\\PW$ and $x\\in\\mathbb R$,
$|f(x)|^2 \\le (\\omega/\\pi)\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2$.
\\end{lemma}
\\begin{proof}
Cauchy--Schwarz: $|f(x)|^2=|\\ip{f}{K_x}|^2\\le K_{\\PW}(x,x)\\norm{f}^2=\\omega/\\pi\\cdot\\norm{f}^2$.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{theorem}[Frame coercivity]\\label{thm:layerA}
Let $\\mathbf{p}$ be unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$ and $D=I$.
Then
\\[
  \\sqrt{\\lambda_{\\min}(\\GVM)}\\,\\norm{f}_{L^2}
  \\le \\QM(f)^{1/2}
  \\le \\sqrt{N\\omega/\\pi}\\,\\norm{f}_{L^2}
  \\qquad \\forall f\\in V_{N,c}.
\\]
In particular, coercivity holds for random sampling (a.s.) and every MZ lower frame.
\\end{theorem}
\\begin{proof}
Unisolvence gives $\\GVM\\succ 0$; the lower bound is then the Rayleigh quotient.
The upper bound sums Lemma~\\ref{lem:upper} over $N$ nodes.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{lemma}[Operator norm bound]\\label{lem:lambda_max_bound}
$\\lambda_{\\max}(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})\\le N\\omega/\\pi$.
\\end{lemma}
\\begin{proof}
Rayleigh quotient characterization plus Theorem~\\ref{thm:layerA}(upper).
\\end{proof}

\\section{Defect decomposition and conditional frame stability}\\label{sec:layerB}

\\begin{theorem}[Defect decomposition]\\label{thm:main_reduction}
For any weighted sampling model $(\\mathbf p,D)$ with $D>0$,
\\[
  \\EN
  = M(\\mathbf p,c)^*(I-D)M(\\mathbf p,c)
   +\\bigl(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\\mathbf p,D)-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}\\bigr),
\\]
and
\\[
  \\norm{\\EN}_2
  \\le \\norm{I-D}_2\\cdot N\\omega/\\pi
   + \\norm{G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\\mathbf p,D)-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}}_2.
\\]
The first term is the \\emph{weighted-frame defect}; the second is the \\emph{spectral mismatch defect}.
\\end{theorem}
\\begin{proof}
Insert and subtract $G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}$:
$\\EN = M^*(I-D)M + (G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c})$.
Subadditivity and Lemma~\\ref{lem:lambda_max_bound} give the norm bound.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{remark}[Algebraic vs.\\ analytic content]
The identity in Theorem~\\ref{thm:main_reduction} is purely algebraic.
Its analytic content emerges only when one controls the spectral mismatch defect
$\\norm{G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}}_2$ for a specific class of nodes.
This is precisely what Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay} provides.
\\end{remark}

\\begin{corollary}[Frame-stable regime from defect bounds]\\label{cor:two_defects}
If $\\norm{I-D}_2\\le\\delta_N$,
$\\norm{G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}-G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}}_2\\le\\eta_N$,
and $\\varepsilon_N:=\\delta_N N\\omega/\\pi+\\eta_N<\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$,
then $\\mathbf p$ is in the frame-stable regime with parameter $\\varepsilon_N$.
\\end{corollary}
\\begin{proof}
Direct from Theorem~\\ref{thm:main_reduction} and Lemma~\\ref{lem:lambda_max_bound}.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{lemma}[Weyl perturbation bound]\\label{lem:weyl_stability}
In the frame-stable regime with parameter $\\varepsilon_N$,
$\\lambda_{\\min}(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})\\ge\\lambda_{N-1}(c)-\\varepsilon_N>0$.
\\end{lemma}
\\begin{proof}
Hermitian perturbation inequality
$\\lambda_{\\min}(A+E)\\ge\\lambda_{\\min}(A)-\\norm{E}_2$
with $A=G^{\\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}$, $E=\\EN$.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{theorem}[Conditional frame stability]\\label{thm:conditional_frame}
Assume Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay} holds with constants $\\alpha>0$ and $\\varepsilon(N,c)$
satisfying
\\[
  \\varepsilon(N,c)\\cdot C(\\alpha,N) < \\lambda_{N-1}(c),
  \\qquad
  C(\\alpha,N):=\\sum_{k=-(N-1)}^{N-1}\\sqrt{N-|k|}\\,e^{-\\alpha|k|}.
\\]
Then the XRY quadrature system $\\{(p_j,w_j)\\}$ is a weighted frame for $V_{N,c}$ with bounds
\\[
  A(N,c) := \\lambda_{N-1}(c) - \\varepsilon(N,c)\\,C(\\alpha,N),
  \\qquad
  B(N,c) := \\lambda_0(c) + \\varepsilon(N,c)\\,C(\\alpha,N),
\\]
and in particular
\\[
  A(N,c)\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2
  \\le \\sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2
  \\le B(N,c)\\norm{f}_{L^2}^2
  \\qquad \\forall f\\in V_{N,c}.
\\]
No claim beyond finite-dimensional perturbation theory is made.
\\end{theorem}
\\begin{proof}
From Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay},
$|E_{mn}|\\le\\varepsilon(N,c)e^{-\\alpha|m-n|}$.
Bounding the spectral norm via Frobenius on the banded structure,
\\[
  \\norm{\\EN}_2
  \\le \\norm{\\EN}_F
  = \\Bigl(\\sum_{m,n}|E_{mn}|^2\\Bigr)^{1/2}
  \\le \\varepsilon(N,c)\\Bigl(\\sum_{k}(N-|k|)e^{-2\\alpha|k|}\\Bigr)^{1/2}
  = \\varepsilon(N,c)\\,C(\\alpha,N).
\\]
Since $C(\\alpha,N)=O(\\sqrt{N})$ for fixed $\\alpha>0$, the frame-stable regime condition
$\\norm{\\EN}_2<\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$ is satisfied by hypothesis.
Lemma~\\ref{lem:weyl_stability} gives the lower frame bound $A(N,c)$,
and Lemma~\\ref{lem:lambda_max_bound} gives the upper bound $B(N,c)$.
The spectral comparison
$\\QM(f)=\\ip{\\Kop f}{f}_{L^2}+R_N(f)$ with $|R_N(f)|\\le\\varepsilon(N,c)C(\\alpha,N)\\norm{f}^2$
follows from the PSWF eigenbasis decomposition.
\\end{proof}

\\begin{remark}[Structure of $C(\\alpha,N)$]
For fixed $\\alpha>0$,
$C(\\alpha,N)=\\sum_{k}\\sqrt{N-|k|}e^{-\\alpha|k|}\\sim \\sqrt{N}\\cdot\\frac{2}{1-e^{-\\alpha}}$
as $N\\to\\infty$,
confirming the $O(\\sqrt{N})$ gain over the crude $O(N)$ bound.
The threshold condition $\\varepsilon(N,c)=o(N^{-1/2}\\lambda_{N-1}(c))$ is
therefore sufficient for the frame-stable regime.
\\end{remark}

\\section{Open problems}\\label{sec:open}

\\begin{problem}[Proof of the banded decay conjecture]
Establish Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay} rigorously,
i.e.\ prove off-diagonal decay $|E_{mn}|\\le\\varepsilon(N,c)e^{-\\alpha|m-n|}$
for XRY quadrature nodes in the regime $N\\sim c$.
A natural route would exploit the time--frequency localization of PSWFs
and the near-orthogonality of $\\psi_m^{(c)}\\overline{\\psi_n^{(c)}}$ for $|m-n|\\gg 1$,
combined with the quadrature accuracy of XRY nodes for bandlimited integrands.
\\end{problem}

\\begin{problem}[Sharp rate for $\\varepsilon(N,c)$]
Determine whether $\\varepsilon(N,c)$ in Conjecture~\\ref{conj:banded_decay}
decays exponentially in $c$ or superalgebraically in $N$, and identify the
precise threshold between these regimes.
\\end{problem}

\\begin{problem}[Uniform-grid unisolvence]
Determine whether uniform grids are unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$.
\\end{problem}

\\begin{problem}[Coupled limits]
Analyze $\\lambda_{\\min}(G^{\\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})$ and the competition between
$\\norm{\\EN}_2$ and $\\lambda_{N-1}(c)$ in regimes where $N$ and $c$ vary simultaneously.
\\end{problem}
