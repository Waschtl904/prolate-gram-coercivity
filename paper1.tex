\begin{abstract}
Let $\PW$ be the Paley--Wiener space of $\omega$-bandlimited functions
and $V_{N,c}:=\operatorname{span}\{\psi_0^{(c)},\dots,\psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\}$
the $N$-dimensional prolate subspace at time-bandwidth product $c=\omega T$.
We study the discrete sampling form
$\QM(f)=\sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2$ as a finite frame form on $V_{N,c}$.

We establish coercivity results for random sampling configurations and for weighted
sampling models satisfying a Marcinkiewicz--Zygmund lower frame bound.
Our main structural result is an exact defect decomposition reducing frame
analysis to simultaneous control of a weighted-frame defect and a quadrature
defect.

Assuming a standard first-order quadrature consistency estimate,
we derive a global quadrature defect bound in the PSWF eigenbasis:
\[
  |E_{mn}| \le C\,h c
  \qquad \text{for all } 0\le m,n\le N-1,
\]
where $h$ denotes the mesh size of the quadrature rule.
The exact identity
$E_{mn} = 0 + R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}$ (Proposition~\ref{prop:xry_split})
clarifies that the first term vanishes by PSWF orthogonality, so the
quadrature defect is fully isolated. Separately, the continuous PSWF
product integral exhibits algebraic decay via a quantified phase-gap estimate
and iterated integration by parts. Thus the analytic bulk--turning mechanism
is isolated from the quadrature floor, while all results remain
finite-dimensional and restricted to fixed parameters $(N,c)$. The
bulk--turning analysis is developed as an independent structural component;
it identifies the oscillatory mechanism underlying decay in continuous PSWF
interactions but does not by itself transfer this structure to the discrete
sampling level. This transfer is encapsulated in the DSTP interface hypothesis
and is addressed in a companion paper.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%% ==================================================================

The prolate spheroidal wave functions (PSWFs) form an orthonormal basis
for the finite-dimensional subspace $V_{N,c}$ of the Paley--Wiener space
$\PW$ that is optimally concentrated in a time--frequency window
\cite{Slepian1978,BonamiKaroui}.
From the perspective of frame theory and sampling in reproducing kernel
Hilbert spaces, the weighted evaluation map
$f\mapsto(\sqrt{w_j}f(p_j))_j$ defines a finite frame operator on
$V_{N,c}$, whose associated Gram matrix encodes the stability of the
sampling scheme.

This paper addresses two questions. First, which classes of sampling
configurations yield coercive Gram operators on $V_{N,c}$?
Second, how does the discrete sampling Gram matrix compare to the
reference spectral matrix $G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}=\Kop|_{V_{N,c}}$
in the PSWF eigenbasis?

For the first question we prove coercivity for random sampling and for
weighted MZ frames. For the second, we establish an exact algebraic
defect decomposition, derive a global first-order quadrature bound for the
discrete defect, and separately analyse the oscillatory decay mechanism of
the continuous PSWF product integral via a WKB--bulk / Airy--turning decomposition.
A companion paper studies a PSWF-adapted XRY quadrature regime and
conditional spectral compatibility; see~\cite{PaperII}.

\subsection*{Main results}
The key new ingredient is the replacement of the earlier conditional
quadrature-compatibility hypothesis by a verifiable first-order quadrature estimate.
The proof architecture has four explicit components:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Quadrature--projection split} (Proposition~\ref{prop:xry_split}):
  $E_{mn}$ is a pure quadrature remainder; the PSWF orthogonality term is
  exactly zero.
\item \textbf{Spectral parameter control} (Proposition~\ref{prop:spectral_gap}):
  the transition-regime asymptotics for $\chi_n(c)$ are stated explicitly
  and used later in the phase-gap argument.
\item \textbf{Uniform phase gap and IBP}
  (Lemmas~\ref{lem:bulk_spectral_sep}, \ref{lem:phase_gap_final},
  \ref{lem:bulk_derivative}, \ref{lem:ibp_uniform},
  and~\ref{lem:bulk_decay_continuous}):
  bulk-to-spectral separation makes the phase-gap estimate explicit,
  bulk-restricted semiclassical derivative bounds control PSWF derivatives
  away from turning regions, and bulk-localised iterated integration by
  parts yields algebraic decay for the continuous PSWF product integral.
\item \textbf{Quadrature reduction by derivative control}
  (Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order}, Lemma~\ref{lem:derivative_bound},
  and Theorem~\ref{thm:banded_decay}):
  the full quadrature remainder is controlled by a standard first-order
  error term involving $\int |f'_{mn}|$, which is estimated using PSWF
  regularity.
\end{enumerate}
A key conceptual point is that the oscillatory bulk--turning analysis
developed below is not used to derive the discrete quadrature defect bound
itself. Rather, it isolates the analytic decay mechanism of continuous PSWF
interactions and thereby identifies the missing structural ingredient
required for transferring such decay to the discrete level.

In particular, the first-order quadrature bound yields a structure-blind
estimate of size $hc$, while the bulk--turning analysis explains why any
refinement must rely on additional oscillation-compatible quadrature
structure. This observation motivates the Discrete Spectral Transfer
Property (DSTP) introduced later, which serves as the precise axiomatic
interface between continuous oscillatory decay and discrete frame stability.

\section{Setup and notation}\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{Paley--Wiener space and concentration operator}

We use the Fourier convention
$\widehat{f}(\xi)=\int_{\mathbb{R}}f(x)e^{-2\pi ix\xi}\,dx$.
The Paley--Wiener space $\PW$ consists of all $f\in L^2(\mathbb{R})$
with Fourier support in $[-\omega/(2\pi),\omega/(2\pi)]$. It is a
reproducing-kernel Hilbert space with kernel
\[
  K_{\PW}(x,y)=\tfrac{\omega}{\pi}\operatorname{sinc}(\omega(x-y)/\pi),
  \qquad K_{\PW}(x,x)=\omega/\pi
\]
\cite{BonamiKaroui}.

The prolate concentration operator is
\[
  \Kop f(x)=\int_{-T}^{T}\frac{\sin\omega(x-y)}{\pi(x-y)}f(y)\,dy,
  \qquad \Kop:\PW\to\PW,
\]
with eigenvalues $1>\lambda_0(c)>\lambda_1(c)>\cdots>0$,
PSWF eigenfunctions $\{\psin\}_{n\ge 0}$ forming an orthonormal basis
of $\PW$ \cite{Slepian1978}, and associated spectral parameters
$\chi_n(c)$ (eigenvalues of the PSWF differential operator;
see \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao,BonamiKaroui2014}).
We set $c=\omega T$ and
$V_{N,c}:=\operatorname{span}\{\psi_0^{(c)},\dots,\psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\}$.
The transition regime $N\sim c$ is the natural operating range.

\subsection{Frame operator and defect matrix}

\begin{definition}[Weighted evaluation frame and Gram matrix]\label{def:GramForm}
For distinct $\mathbf{p}=(p_1,\dots,p_N)\subset[-T,T]$ and weights $w_j>0$,
the evaluation matrix is $M(\mathbf{p},c)_{jn}:=\psi_n^{(c)}(p_j)$ and
$D:=\operatorname{diag}(w_1,\dots,w_N)$. The weighted Gram matrix is
\[
  G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\mathbf{p},D):=M(\mathbf{p},c)^*D\,M(\mathbf{p},c).
\]
For $f=\sum_{n=0}^{N-1}a_n\psin\in V_{N,c}$,
$\QM(f):=\sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2=\ip{G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}\mathbf{a}}{\mathbf{a}}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Reference spectral matrix and defect]\label{def:ref_matrix}
\[
  G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}:=\operatorname{diag}(\lambda_0(c),\dots,\lambda_{N-1}(c)),
  \qquad
  \EN:=G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}(\mathbf{p},D)-G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}.
\]
The defect entries are
\[
  E_{mn}:=\sum_{j=1}^N w_j\,\psi_m^{(c)}(p_j)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(p_j)}
            -\lambda_n(c)\,\delta_{mn}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Frame-stable regime]\label{def:framestable}
$\mathbf{p}$ is in the frame-stable regime if $\norm{\EN}_2<\lambda_{N-1}(c)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Spectral parameter asymptotics]\label{prop:spectral_gap}
Let $N\sim c$. Then there exists $C>0$ such that
\[
  \chi_n(c) = (2n+1)c + r_n(c),
  \qquad |r_n(c)| \le C c^{4/3},
\]
uniformly for $0\le n\le N-1$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
See \cite[Section~2.5]{OsipovRokhlinXiao} together with the uniform
transition-regime bounds in \cite{BonamiKaroui2014}.
\end{proof}

\section{Unisolvence and coercivity}\label{sec:coercivity}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{definition}[Frame condition]\label{def:frame}
$\{(p_j,w_j)\}_{j=1}^N$ is a weighted frame for $V_{N,c}$ if
$A\norm{f}_{L^2}^2\le\sum_j w_j|f(p_j)|^2\le B\norm{f}_{L^2}^2$
for all $f\in V_{N,c}$ with $0<A\le B<\infty$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Unisolvent set]
$\mathbf p$ is unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$ if
$f\mapsto(f(p_1),\dots,f(p_N))$ is injective on $V_{N,c}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Identity principle]\label{lem:identity}
If $f\in\PW$ vanishes on a set with a limit point in $\mathbb R$,
then $f\equiv 0$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Every $f\in\PW$ is entire of exponential type; apply the identity theorem.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[MZ lower bound implies unisolvence]\label{prop:mz_unisolvent}
If $\{(p_j,w_j)\}$ is a weighted frame with lower bound $A>0$,
then $\mathbf p$ is unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$f(p_j)=0$ for all $j$ implies $A\norm{f}^2\le 0$, hence $f\equiv 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Random sampling]\label{prop:random}
Let $p_1,\dots,p_N$ be i.i.d.\ absolutely continuous on $[-T,T]$.
Then $\mathbf{p}$ is unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$ almost surely.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
$(p_1,\dots,p_N)\mapsto\det M(\mathbf{p},c)$ is real-analytic and
non-identically zero, so its zero set has measure zero.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Pointwise RKHS bound]\label{lem:upper}
For $f\in\PW$ and $x\in\mathbb R$,
$|f(x)|^2\le(\omega/\pi)\norm{f}_{L^2}^2$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Cauchy--Schwarz with the reproducing kernel.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Frame coercivity]\label{thm:coercivity}
Let $\mathbf{p}$ be unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$ and $D=I$. Then
\[
  \sqrt{\lambda_{\min}(\GVM)}\,\norm{f}_{L^2}
  \le\QM(f)^{1/2}
  \le\sqrt{N\omega/\pi}\,\norm{f}_{L^2}
  \qquad\forall f\in V_{N,c}.
\]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
Lower bound: Rayleigh quotient. Upper bound: sum Lemma~\ref{lem:upper} over nodes.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Operator norm bound]\label{lem:lambda_max_bound}
$\lambda_{\max}(G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})\le N\omega/\pi$.
\end{lemma}

\section{Quadrature decomposition and oscillatory decay}\label{sec:decay}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{proposition}[Quadrature--Projection Decomposition]\label{prop:xry_split}
Let $M(\mathbf{p},c)$ be the evaluation matrix and $D=\mathrm{diag}(w_j)$.
Then
\[
  E_{mn}
  =
  \underbrace{
  \Bigl(
  \int_{-T}^{T}\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}\,dx
  -\lambda_n(c)\delta_{mn}
  \Bigr)}_{=\,0}
  +
  R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}},
\]
where the first term vanishes by orthogonality of PSWFs as eigenfunctions
of $\Kop$, and the quadrature remainder is
\[
  R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}
  =
  \sum_{j=1}^N w_j f_{mn}(p_j)
  -\int_{-T}^{T}f_{mn}(x)\,dx,
  \qquad f_{mn}(x):=\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}.
\]
Since $\Kop$ is self-adjoint on $\PW$ and $\{\psi_n^{(c)}\}$ is an
orthonormal eigenbasis of $\PW$, we have
\[
\langle \Kop \psi_n^{(c)}, \psi_m^{(c)} \rangle_{L^2(\mathbb{R})}
= \lambda_n(c)\,\delta_{mn}.
\]
Using the kernel representation of $\Kop$ and the restriction of the
integration to $[-T,T]$, this inner product rewrites explicitly as
\[
\int_{-T}^{T} \psi_n^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_m^{(c)}(x)}\,dx
= \langle \Kop \psi_n^{(c)}, \psi_m^{(c)} \rangle_{L^2(\mathbb{R})}
= \lambda_n(c)\,\delta_{mn},
\]
which proves that the projection term vanishes. Consequently,
\[
  E_{mn} = - R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}
\]
under the sign convention of Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order}, so
all later estimates use only $|R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}|$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}
Proposition~\ref{prop:xry_split} corrects an implicit error in earlier
drafts: $E_{mn}$ is \emph{not} an algebraically exact split of the form
``$G^{\mathrm{w}}=G^{\mathrm{ref}}+\text{defect}$'' arising from some
identity; it is purely a quadrature error for the PSWF product integral.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Bulk and turning regions]\label{def:bulk}
Let $x_n^*>0$ denote the positive turning point of $\psi_n^{(c)}$ and
fix $\rho>0$. The Airy neighbourhood is
\[
  U_n:=\{x\in[-T,T]:|x-x_n^*|\le\rho c^{-1/3}\}
       \cup\{x\in[-T,T]:|x+x_n^*|\le\rho c^{-1/3}\}.
\]
For a pair $(m,n)$:
\[
  \Rturning^{(m,n)}:=U_m\cup U_n,
  \qquad
  \Rbulk^{(m,n)}:=[-T,T]\setminus\Rturning^{(m,n)}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}
The width $O(c^{-1/3})$ is the standard Airy transition scale
\cite{Olver1974}: beyond the neighborhoods of the two turning points
$\pm x_n^*$ the quadratic approximation to the square root is valid and
the WKB phase is non-stationary for $|m-n|\ge 1$.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Bulk spectral separation via turning-point geometry]%
\label{lem:bulk_spectral_sep}
Let $x\in \Rbulk^{(m,n)}$. Then there exists $\delta_0>0$, depending
only on the Airy exclusion parameter $\rho$, such that
\[
  \left|\chi_m(c)-\frac{c^2x^2}{T^2}\right|\ge \delta_0 c^{2/3},
  \qquad
  \left|\chi_n(c)-\frac{c^2x^2}{T^2}\right|\ge \delta_0 c^{2/3}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For $k\in\{m,n\}$, the turning point $x_k^*>0$ is defined by
$\chi_k(c)-c^2(x_k^*)^2/T^2=0$, hence
\[
  \chi_k(c)-\frac{c^2x^2}{T^2}
  = \frac{c^2}{T^2}(x_k^*-x)(x_k^*+x).
\]
By Definition~\ref{def:bulk}, the bulk region excludes Airy-scale
neighborhoods of both turning points $\pm x_k^*$, so
$|x_k^*\pm x|\ge \rho c^{-1/3}$. Therefore
\[
  \left|\chi_k(c)-\frac{c^2x^2}{T^2}\right|
  \ge \frac{c^2}{T^2}\,\rho^2 c^{-2/3}
  = \frac{\rho^2}{T^2}c^{2/3},
\]
which proves the claim with $\delta_0:=\rho^2/T^2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Uniform phase gap in the bulk, two-regime form]%
\label{lem:phase_gap_final}
Let $N\sim c$ and fix $\rho>0$. Then there exist constants $c_0>0$ and
$\kappa>0$ such that for all $c\ge c_0$, uniformly in
$0\le m\ne n\le N-1$:
\begin{align*}
  |\Phi_{mn}'(x)|
  &\ge \frac{\kappa}{T}|m-n|,
  &&\text{if } |m-n|\le c^{1/3},\\
  |\Phi_{mn}'(x)|
  &\ge \frac{\kappa}{T}c^{1/3},
  &&\text{if } |m-n|\ge c^{1/3},
\end{align*}
for all $x\in\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$. Consequently,
\[
  |\Phi_{mn}'(x)|
  \ge
  \frac{\kappa}{T}\,\frac{|m-n|}{1 + c^{-2/3}|m-n|}
  \qquad\text{for all }x\in\Rbulk^{(m,n)}.
\]
The final bound is used only as a unified lower envelope and is not
claimed to be sharp.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Set $\mu:=\chi_m(c)-c^2x^2/T^2$ and $\nu:=\chi_n(c)-c^2x^2/T^2$.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:bulk_spectral_sep}, $\mu,\nu\ge\delta_0 c^{2/3}$.
Using $|\sqrt{\mu}-\sqrt{\nu}|=|\mu-\nu|/(\sqrt{\mu}+\sqrt{\nu})$,
\[
  |\Phi_{mn}'(x)|
  = \frac{|\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c)|}{T(\sqrt{\mu}+\sqrt{\nu})}.
\]

\noindent\textbf{Step 1: Spectral gap.}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:spectral_gap},
$|\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c)|\ge 2c|m-n|-2C_*c^{4/3}$.
For $|m-n|\le c^{1/3}$ and $c\ge c_0$ large enough,
$|\chi_m-\chi_n|\ge c|m-n|$.
For $|m-n|\ge c^{1/3}$, $2c|m-n|\ge 2c^{4/3}$, so
$|\chi_m-\chi_n|\ge c^{4/3}$.

\noindent\textbf{Step 2: Denominator.}
$\sqrt{\mu}+\sqrt{\nu}\le 2\sqrt{\chi_{N-1}(c)}\le C_3 c$
since $\chi_{N-1}(c)\lesssim c^2$ in the regime $N\sim c$.

\noindent\textbf{Step 3.}
Combining the two steps yields the stated two-regime bounds. The unified
envelope follows by observing
$c|m-n|\,\mathbf{1}_{|m-n|\le c^{1/3}}+c^{4/3}\,\mathbf{1}_{|m-n|\ge c^{1/3}}
\gtrsim c|m-n|/(1+c^{-2/3}|m-n|)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bulk semiclassical derivative control]\label{lem:bulk_derivative}
Fix $\rho>0$ and let $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$ be as in Definition~\ref{def:bulk}.
For every integer $k\ge 0$ there exists a constant $C_k>0$, depending
only on $k$ and $\rho$, such that
\[
\|\partial_x^k \psi_n^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty(\Rbulk^{(m,n)})}
\le C_k\, c^{k/2}
\]
uniformly for all $0\le n\le N-1$ and all $c\ge 1$ in the transition
regime $N\sim c$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
This follows from uniform semiclassical estimates for PSWFs as developed
in \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao} and \cite{BonamiKaroui2014}, together with
classical WKB regularity theory \cite[Ch.~6]{Olver1974}.

On $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$, the PSWF differential equation lies in the
non-stationary semiclassical regime away from the turning points.
The WKB phase satisfies
\[
\Phi_n'(x)
= \frac{1}{T}\sqrt{\chi_n(c)-\frac{c^2x^2}{T^2}}
= O(c^{1/2})
\]
by Lemma~\ref{lem:bulk_spectral_sep}.

The cited results provide uniform $C^k$ bounds for the PSWFs themselves
on compact subsets of the bulk region separated from the turning points
by Airy-scale neighbourhoods of size $\rho c^{-1/3}$. In particular,
for every fixed $k\ge 0$,
\[
\|\partial_x^k \psi_n^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty(\Rbulk^{(m,n)})}
\le C_k'\, c^{k/2},
\]
with constants uniform in $n$ in the transition regime $N\sim c$.
This can be interpreted equivalently at the level of the WKB ansatz,
but we do not require an explicit amplitude-phase decomposition.

Each application of $\partial_x$ contributes at most a factor
$\Phi_n'(x)=O(c^{1/2})$, consistent with the stated bound. Collecting
the $k$-fold contributions yields
\[
\|\partial_x^k \psi_n^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty(\Rbulk^{(m,n)})}
\le C_k\, c^{k/2}.
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bulk-localised uniform IBP control]\label{lem:ibp_uniform}
Let $a_{mn}(x)=A_m(x)\overline{A_n(x)}$ be the WKB amplitude product
and $L:=(i\Phi_{mn}'(x))^{-1}\partial_x$. Then for every $p\ge 1$,
\[
  \|(L^*)^p a_{mn}\|_{L^1(\Rbulk^{(m,n)})}
  \le C_p\Bigl(\frac{1+c^{-2/3}|m-n|}{|m-n|}\Bigr)^p,
\]
where all estimates are restricted to the bulk region $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$.
Moreover, $(L^*)^p a_{mn}$ is a finite linear combination of terms of
the form
\[
  c_{p,k,\ell}(x)\,\frac{\partial_x^k a_{mn}(x)}{(\Phi_{mn}'(x))^{\ell}},
  \qquad 0\le k\le p,\; 1\le \ell\le p,
\]
with coefficients $c_{p,k,\ell}$ depending only on $p$ and finitely many
derivatives of $\Phi_{mn}'$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The formal adjoint has the form
$L^*=-(i\Phi_{mn}')^{-1}\partial_x+b_1(x)(\Phi_{mn}')^{-2}$,
where $b_1$ is smooth on $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$ and depends on $\Phi_{mn}''$.
By induction on $p$, $(L^*)^p a_{mn}$ is a finite linear combination of
terms of the stated form, with the number of terms depending only on $p$.
By Lemma~\ref{lem:phase_gap_final},
\[
  |(\Phi_{mn}'(x))^{-1}|
  \le \frac{T}{\kappa}\cdot\frac{1+c^{-2/3}|m-n|}{|m-n|}
  \qquad\text{on }\Rbulk^{(m,n)}.
\]
Lemma~\ref{lem:bulk_derivative} provides uniform $C^k$ bounds for the
PSWFs on $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$, from which all derivatives of $a_{mn}$ are
uniformly controlled, with polynomial dependence on $c$ already accounted
for by the phase-gap factors. All terms in the expansion of
$(L^*)^p a_{mn}$ are therefore uniformly integrable on
$\Rbulk^{(m,n)}\subset[-T,T]$. Integrating yields the stated estimate.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Bulk decay for the continuous integral]\label{lem:bulk_decay}
Under the hypotheses of Lemma~\ref{lem:ibp_uniform}, for $m\neq n$,
\[
  \left|\int_{\Rbulk^{(m,n)}} f_{mn}(x)\,dx\right|
  \le C_p\Bigl(\frac{1+c^{-2/3}|m-n|}{|m-n|}\Bigr)^p
  \le C_p'(1+|m-n|)^{-p},
\]
with constants independent of $m,n,N,c$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Apply $L$ iteratively on $\Rbulk^{(m,n)}$; oscillatory boundary terms
are absorbed into the bulk localisation. Lemma~\ref{lem:ibp_uniform}
controls the resulting amplitude expressions in $L^1$. The simplification
$(1+c^{-2/3}|m-n|)/|m-n|\le 2/(1+|m-n|)$ yields the final bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Turning-point estimate]\label{lem:turning_revised}
Let $U_m$ be the Airy neighbourhood with $|U_m|=O(c^{-1/3})$.
For all $0\le m,n\le N-1$,
\[
  \int_{U_m}|\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\psi_n^{(c)}(x)|\,dx\le C\,c^{-1/3}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By \cite{BonamiKaroui2014}, $\|\psi_k^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty([-T,T])}\le
C_\infty$ uniformly for $0\le k\le N-1$. Hence
$\|\psi_k^{(c)}\|_{L^2(U_m)}\le C_\infty|U_m|^{1/2}=O(c^{-1/6})$.
Cauchy--Schwarz gives
$\int_{U_m}|\psi_m^{(c)}\psi_n^{(c)}|\,dx
\le C|U_m|^{1/2}\cdot C|U_m|^{1/2}=C|U_m|=O(c^{-1/3})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[First-order quadrature consistency]%
\label{ass:quad_first_order}
Let $\{(p_j,w_j)\}_{j=1}^N$ be a quadrature rule on $[-T,T]$ with mesh
size $h:=\max_{1\le j\le N-1}|p_{j+1}-p_j|$. We assume there exists
$C_Q>0$ such that for all $f\in W^{1,1}([-T,T])$,
\[
\left|\int_{-T}^{T} f(x)\,dx - \sum_{j=1}^N w_j f(p_j)\right|
\le C_Q\,h\int_{-T}^{T}|f'(x)|\,dx.
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}[Scope of the quadrature input]\label{rem:scope_quad}
Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order} is intentionally coarse. It
controls only the absolute size of the quadrature remainder and does not
encode oscillation-sensitive cancellation, PSWF-adapted exactness, or any
transfer of continuous off-diagonal decay to the discrete defect matrix.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Derivative bound for PSWF products]\label{lem:derivative_bound}
For $f_{mn}(x):=\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}$,
\[
|f'_{mn}(x)|
\le |\psi_m^{(c)\,\prime}(x)|\,|\psi_n^{(c)}(x)|
+|\psi_m^{(c)}(x)|\,|\psi_n^{(c)\,\prime}(x)|.
\]
If the Bonami--Karoui bounds
$\|\psi_k^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty}\le C_0$ and
$\|\psi_k^{(c)\,\prime}\|_{L^\infty}\le C_1 c$
hold uniformly for $0\le k\le N-1$, then
\[
\int_{-T}^{T}|f'_{mn}(x)|\,dx \le C c,
\]
with a constant independent of $m,n,N,c$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Differentiate the product and apply the triangle inequality:
$|f'_{mn}|\le C_1 c\,C_0+C_0\,C_1 c=2C_0C_1 c$.
Integration over the fixed interval $[-T,T]$ yields the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Role of the first-order quadrature assumption]
Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order} is a standard, verifiable
quadrature hypothesis of Riemann- or trapezoidal-rule type, independent
of the PSWF structure. All PSWF-specific information enters only through
Lemma~\ref{lem:derivative_bound} and the oscillatory bulk analysis.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Quadrature defect bound]\label{thm:banded_decay}
Let Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order} hold. Then for all
$0\le m,n\le N-1$,
\[
  |E_{mn}| \le C h c,
\]
where $C>0$ is independent of $m,n,N,c$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:xry_split}, $|E_{mn}|=|R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}|$.
Applying Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order} to $f_{mn}$ and then
Lemma~\ref{lem:derivative_bound} gives
$|E_{mn}|\le C_Q h\int_{-T}^T|f'_{mn}|\,dx\le C_Q C\,hc$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Sharpness under first-order quadrature]\label{rem:sharpness_quad}
The bound $|E_{mn}|\le Chc$ is intentionally structure-blind. Without
additional oscillation-sensitive or PSWF-adapted quadrature structure,
no off-diagonal decay in $|m-n|$ can be expected at the discrete level.
In this sense the estimate is essentially sharp under the present
assumptions.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Continuous bulk--turning decay]\label{lem:bulk_decay_continuous}
For all $p\ge 1$ and all $0\le m,n\le N-1$,
\[
  \left|\int_{-T}^{T} \psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}\,dx\right|
  \le C_p (1+|m-n|)^{-p} + C\,c^{-1/3}.
\]
For $m=n$, the integral equals $\lambda_n(c)$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For $m=n$ the statement is trivial. For $m\neq n$ decompose:
\[
  \int_{-T}^{T} = \int_{\Rbulk^{(m,n)}} + \int_{\Rturning^{(m,n)}}.
\]
The bulk term is bounded by $C_p(1+|m-n|)^{-p}$ by
Lemma~\ref{lem:bulk_decay}. The turning term is bounded by
$C\,c^{-1/3}$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:turning_revised}. Combining yields
the result.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}
This decay describes the oscillatory structure of the continuous PSWF
kernel. It does \emph{not} transfer to the discrete defect matrix
$E_{mn}$ without additional oscillation-compatible quadrature
assumptions; see~\cite{PaperII}.
\end{remark}

\section{Defect decomposition and quantitative frame stability}%
\label{sec:stability}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Defect decomposition]\label{thm:main_reduction}
For any $(\mathbf p,D)$ with $D>0$,
\[
  \EN=M(\mathbf p,c)^*(I-D)M(\mathbf p,c)+(G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}-G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}),
\]
and $\norm{\EN}_2\le\norm{I-D}_2\cdot N\omega/\pi
+\norm{G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c}-G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}}_2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{lemma}[Weyl perturbation]\label{lem:weyl}
If $\norm{\EN}_2<\lambda_{N-1}(c)$ then
$\lambda_{\min}(G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})\ge\lambda_{N-1}(c)-\norm{\EN}_2>0$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Hermitian perturbation: $\lambda_{\min}(A+E)\ge\lambda_{\min}(A)-\norm{E}_2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{definition}[Discrete Spectral Transfer Property]\label{def:dstp}
Let $\{(p_j,w_j)\}_{j=1}^N$ satisfy Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order}
with defect matrix $\EN=(E_{mn})$. We say the rule satisfies the
\emph{Discrete Spectral Transfer Property} (DSTP) on $V_{N,c}$ if there
exist constants $C>0$ and $p>1$ such that
\[
  |E_{mn}|\le C h c\,(1+|m-n|)^{-p}
  \qquad \text{for all } 0\le m,n\le N-1.
\]
DSTP is an \emph{axiomatic interface hypothesis}: it asserts that the
discrete defect inherits a Schur-summable off-diagonal profile at scale
$hc$, in a form that mirrors the continuous oscillatory decay of PSWF
products established in Section~\ref{sec:decay}. DSTP is \emph{not}
implied by those continuous estimates; it precisely identifies the
structural gap that a companion quadrature framework must close.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Conditional frame stability under Schur-decay hypothesis]%
\label{thm:conditional_frame_schur}
Let $\{(p_j,w_j)\}_{j=1}^N$ satisfy Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order}
with $N\sim c$, and assume in addition that the rule satisfies DSTP
(Definition~\ref{def:dstp}). If
\[
  C h c\sum_{k\in\mathbb Z}(1+|k|)^{-p}<\lambda_{N-1}(c),
\]
then $\{(p_j,w_j)\}$ is a weighted frame for $V_{N,c}$ with bounds
\begin{align*}
  A(N,c)&:=\lambda_{N-1}(c)-C h c\sum_{k\in\mathbb Z}(1+|k|)^{-p}>0,\\
  B(N,c)&:=\lambda_0(c)+C h c\sum_{k\in\mathbb Z}(1+|k|)^{-p}.
\end{align*}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By DSTP, the Schur test gives
$\norm{\EN}_2\le C h c\sum_{k\in\mathbb Z}(1+|k|)^{-p}$.
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:weyl} for the lower bound and the reference
diagonal bound for the upper bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Interpretive consequence]\label{cor:fixed_param_scope}
Theorem~\ref{thm:conditional_frame_schur} yields a finite-dimensional
stability statement under DSTP together with first-order quadrature
control. Assumption~\ref{ass:quad_first_order} alone does not imply the
Schur-summable defect structure required above.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Separation of scales]
The continuous oscillatory decay of PSWF products is independent of the
quadrature-induced defect floor. Without additional structure on the
sampling rule, this decay does not propagate to the discrete Gram defect.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[DSTP as interface axiom]\label{rem:dstp_interface}
The present paper does not establish DSTP. DSTP is the axiomatic
interface between the present paper and~\cite{PaperII}: once DSTP holds,
frame stability follows by the Schur-test argument above. Standard
Riemann- or trapezoidal-type rules do not in general satisfy DSTP.
Identifying concrete schemes with this property is developed
in~\cite{PaperII}.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Norm control]\label{rem:schur}
The operator-norm estimate in Theorem~\ref{thm:conditional_frame_schur}
is based on the Schur test, which is the natural mechanism once an
explicit off-diagonal decay profile is assumed. Frobenius estimates are
too coarse and are not used.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Scaling of the stability condition]\label{rem:scaling_stability}
Under DSTP, $\norm{\EN}_2=O(hc)$, so the stability condition is governed
directly by $hc$ relative to $\lambda_{N-1}(c)$, without additional
polynomial growth in $N$.
\end{remark}

\section{Boundary to the companion framework}\label{sec:bridge}
%% ==================================================================

The present paper proves a robust finite-dimensional stability statement
under a standard first-order quadrature consistency assumption. Its role
is to separate three distinct mechanisms: continuous PSWF orthogonality,
oscillatory decay of the continuous product kernel, and
quadrature-induced defect control.

This separation is deliberate. The continuous bulk--turning analysis
explains why off-diagonal PSWF interactions decay analytically, but the
proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:conditional_frame_schur} does not transfer that
decay to the discrete defect matrix $E_{mn}$. Instead, the unconditional
discrete estimate is governed only by the coarse quadrature bound
$|E_{mn}|\le Chc$, while the frame theorem requires the additional
interface hypothesis DSTP.

As a consequence, the present framework is robust but not spectrally
sharp. It yields frame stability for fixed $(N,c)$ once the defect is
dominated by $\lambda_{N-1}(c)$, but does not provide an asymptotically
uniform stability theorem in the transition regime $N\sim c$.

The companion paper~\cite{PaperII} addresses a genuinely different
question: whether oscillatory structure of PSWF products can be preserved
at the discrete level by PSWF-adapted quadrature rules. Establishing DSTP
for concrete quadrature schemes is deferred entirely to that paper.

Thus the two papers address different levels of structure: the present
work isolates stability under minimal first-order assumptions, while
\cite{PaperII} studies when oscillatory structure of PSWF products is
reflected at the discrete level. DSTP is the explicit interface axiom
between them.

\section{Open problems}\label{sec:open}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{problem}[Sharp decay rate]
Determine whether the algebraic decay in Lemma~\ref{lem:bulk_decay_continuous}
can be upgraded to exponential, or whether $p\to\infty$ yields
superalgebraic decay.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Sharper turning bounds]
It is plausible that the turning contribution can be improved from
$O(c^{-1/3})$ via refined Airy asymptotics and microlocal analysis near
the turning points.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Quantitative constants]
Make the constants in Lemmas~\ref{lem:bulk_spectral_sep},
\ref{lem:phase_gap_final}, \ref{lem:ibp_uniform},
and \ref{lem:derivative_bound} fully explicit in $N$ and $c$.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Oscillation-compatible quadrature]
Determine concrete quadrature schemes for which algebraic decay of the
continuous bulk integral transfers to the quadrature error. A
PSWF-Gauss/XRY implication framework is developed in~\cite{PaperII}.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Uniform-grid unisolvence]
Determine whether uniform grids are unisolvent for $V_{N,c}$.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Coupled limits]
Analyse $\lambda_{\min}(G^{\mathrm{w}}_{N,c})$ and frame stability when
$N$ and $c$ vary simultaneously.
\end{problem}

\section{Airy uniformisation near turning points}\label{app:airy}

\begin{lemma}[PSWF Airy approximation]\label{lem:airy_approx}
For each $0\le n\le N-1$ and $x\in U_n$,
\[
  \psi_n^{(c)}(x)
  =\kappa_n^{(c)}\,\mathrm{Ai}\!\bigl(-\zeta_n(x)\bigr)+O(c^{-1/3}),
\]
where $\zeta_n(x)=c^{2/3}(x-x_n^*)/T+O(c^{-1/3})$ and
$|\kappa_n^{(c)}|\le C_\kappa$ uniformly in $n,c$
\cite{Olver1974,OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
Standard semiclassical analysis \cite[Ch.~11]{Olver1974}.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Turning-point integral via Airy]\label{lem:airy_bound}
For all $0\le m,n\le N-1$,
\[
  \left|\int_{U_m\cup U_n}\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}\,dx\right|
  \le C\,c^{-1/3}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By the uniform sup-norm bound and the Airy-scale width
$|U_k|=O(c^{-1/3})$,
$\|\psi_k^{(c)}\|_{L^2(U_m\cup U_n)}\le C(|U_m|+|U_n|)^{1/2}=O(c^{-1/6})$.
Cauchy--Schwarz on $U_m\cup U_n$ gives the bound $O(c^{-1/3})$.
\end{proof}
