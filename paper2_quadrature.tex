\begin{abstract}
In the companion paper \cite{PaperI}, frame coercivity and a conditional
banded decay estimate for the quadrature defect matrix $\EN$ were
established for the prolate sampling operator on $V_{N,c}$, conditional
on an explicit quadrature compatibility assumption (Assumption~3.10 of
\cite{PaperI}).

The present paper does not verify that assumption unconditionally.
Instead, for PSWF-Gauss quadrature rules of Xiao--Rokhlin--Yarvin
\cite{Xiao2001}, it reduces the compatibility problem to an explicit
XRY spectral stability hypothesis via a two-step spectral mechanism.
First, analytic product integrands
$f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$ have exponentially decaying
PSWF expansion tails in the relevant index regime. Second, assuming an
XRY stability bound of the form
\[
  \left| Q_N(f) - \int_{-T}^T f \right|
  \le \CXRY\,\|(I-\PN)f\| + C e^{-\alpha N}\norm{f}_{L^2},
\]
one obtains the conditional defect estimate
\[
  |E_{mn}| \le C e^{-\alpha N} \qquad (0\le m,n\le N-1).
\]
Therefore Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI} is verified conditionally on
this XRY stability hypothesis together with explicit technical hypotheses
on the PSWF side. No algebraic closure of PSWF product spaces is assumed
or required.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
%% ==================================================================

\subsection*{Context}

The companion paper \cite{PaperI} established the following structure
for the quadrature defect matrix $\EN = \GVM - G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}$:
\begin{enumerate}
\item An exact identity $E_{mn} = R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}$
  (pure quadrature remainder; PSWF orthogonality term vanishes exactly).
\item A conditional banded decay estimate: under Assumption~3.10 of
  \cite{PaperI},
  \[
    |E_{mn}| \le C_p(1+|m-n|)^{-p} + Cc^{-1/3}
    \qquad (0 \le m,n \le N-1).
  \]
\item A conditional frame stability theorem with explicit bounds.
\end{enumerate}
The open problem left by \cite{PaperI} was to identify a concrete
quadrature class satisfying Assumption~3.10.

\subsection*{Contribution of this paper}

This paper does not solve the full construction problem unconditionally.
Its contribution is a reduction result: for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature,
Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI} follows from a separate XRY spectral
stability hypothesis together with PSWF spectral tail estimates for the
product functions $f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$.
The argument is therefore a conditional verification framework rather
than a complete closure theorem.

\subsection*{Strategy of this paper}

The reduction rests on two ingredients:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
\item \emph{PSWF product analysis.} The product integrands
  $f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$ are analytic on $[-T,T]$.
  Under the corresponding analyticity and growth hypotheses, their
  PSWF expansion coefficients decay exponentially in the regime
  $0\le m,n\le N\sim c$.
\item \emph{XRY quadrature stability.} The XRY quadrature functional $Q_N$
  is assumed to satisfy a stability bound controlled by the PSWF spectral
  truncation error associated with $\PN$.
\end{enumerate}
We deliberately avoid any claim that PSWF product spaces are closed under
pointwise multiplication, and we do not identify XRY with any spectral
projector. The substantive output is a clean separation between a PSWF
spectral tail analysis and an explicit XRY stability bridge.

\subsection*{Main result}

\begin{theorem}[Main theorem, informal]\label{thm:main_informal}
Assume the technical hypotheses stated in
Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability}. Then for
PSWF-Gauss quadrature rules \cite{Xiao2001}, the quadrature defect satisfies
$|E_{mn}|\le Ce^{-\alpha N}$ for all $0\le m,n\le N-1$, with constants
depending on the PSWF analyticity regime, the allowed growth in the index
range, and the XRY stability constants. Consequently, Assumption~3.10 of
\cite{PaperI} holds conditionally on the same hypotheses, and the frame
stability theorem of \cite{PaperI} applies to PSWF-Gauss sampling.
\end{theorem}

The formal version is Theorem~\ref{thm:compatibility_verified} together
with Theorem~\ref{thm:frame_stability}.

\subsection*{Positioning}

This paper isolates the only missing analytic ingredient needed to make the
conditional framework of \cite{PaperI} effective for a concrete quadrature
class. The PSWF spectral side of the argument --- analytic regularity of
product integrands and exponential tail decay --- is carried out explicitly
under stated hypotheses. The remaining open task is a single, clearly
formulated operator stability conjecture for the XRY quadrature functional
(Assumption~\ref{ass:xry_stability}), which represents the precise point
where the present analysis reaches its current boundary. All other
ingredients of the frame stability argument are fully reduced to this one
place.

\subsection*{Organisation}

Section~\ref{sec:pswf_gauss} sets up PSWF-Gauss quadrature and the
spectral projector. Section~\ref{sec:spectral_decay} states the technical
PSWF-side hypotheses and explains the scope of the product-tail step.
Section~\ref{sec:xry_accuracy} states the XRY spectral stability assumption.
Section~\ref{sec:verification} derives the conditional verification of
Assumption~3.10. Section~\ref{sec:stability} states the conditional frame
stability theorem. Section~\ref{sec:open} records open problems.

\section{PSWF-Gauss Quadrature and Spectral Projector}\label{sec:pswf_gauss}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{definition}[PSWF spectral projector]\label{def:projector}
Let $\{\psi_k^{(c)}\}_{k\ge0}$ be the orthonormal PSWF basis on $[-T,T]$.
For $N\ge1$, define
\[
  \PN f := \sum_{k=0}^{N-1}\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}\,\psi_k^{(c)}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[PSWF-Gauss quadrature operator]\label{def:xry}
Let $(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{w})=((p_1,\dots,p_N),(w_1,\dots,w_N))$ denote
the PSWF-Gauss nodes and weights of Xiao--Rokhlin--Yarvin \cite{Xiao2001}.
The quadrature functional is $Q_N(f):=\sum_{j=1}^N w_j f(p_j)$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[No algebraic closure claim]\label{rem:no_algebra}
We make no claim that $f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$
lies in a finite-dimensional PSWF subspace $V_{K,c}$ for any controlled
$K$. Such a claim would require algebraic closure of the PSWF system
under pointwise multiplication, which does not hold. All arguments
below use only analytic regularity and spectral approximation properties.
\end{remark}

\begin{assumption}[Node separation]\label{ass:node_sep}
The PSWF-Gauss nodes satisfy
$\min_{j\ne k}|p_j-p_k|\ge c_0 T/N$
for some constant $c_0>0$ in the regime $N\sim c$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Weight positivity and boundedness]\label{ass:weights}
The weights satisfy $w_j>0$ and $C_1 T/N\le w_j\le C_2 T/N$
uniformly in $j$ in the regime $N\sim c$.
\end{assumption}

\section{Technical PSWF Hypotheses and Product Tails}\label{sec:spectral_decay}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{assumption}[PSWF analyticity in the relevant regime]\label{ass:pswf_analyticity}
For $0\le m,n\le N\sim c$, the product functions
$f_{mn}(x)=\psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}(x)}$ admit holomorphic
extensions to complex strips of width $\rho_{m,n}(c)>0$, with Cauchy bounds
\[
  \norm{f_{mn}^{(k)}}_{L^\infty([-T,T])}\le M_{m,n}(c)\,k!\,\rho_{m,n}(c)^{-k}
  \qquad(k\ge0).
\]
No uniform strip width independent of $(m,n,c)$ is assumed.
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}[Why the PSWF product assumptions are plausible]
The PSWFs are bandlimited eigenfunctions of a time-frequency concentration
operator, so the product $f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$ should
heuristically behave like a function of effective bandwidth of order $2c$.
This does not imply finite-dimensional closure in the PSWF basis, but it
suggests that higher PSWF modes should be increasingly suppressed once one
combines analyticity with control of the dependence on the indices $m,n$.
The purpose of Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity} and
\ref{ass:uniform_bounds} is to isolate exactly the quantitative version of
this heuristic that is needed in the proof.
\end{remark}

\begin{assumption}[Controlled growth of PSWF product constants]\label{ass:uniform_bounds}
There exist constants $C_*>0$ and $k_*>0$ such that, in the regime
$0\le m,n\le N\sim c$,
\[
  M_{m,n}(c)\le C_* N^{k_*},
  \qquad
  \rho_{m,n}(c)^{-1}\le C_* N^{k_*},
  \qquad
  C_{m,n}\le C_* N^{k_*}.
\]
Any comparable family of explicit polynomial bounds sufficient to keep the
product-tail estimate asymptotically dominated by an exponential rate after
possibly reducing that rate would also suffice for the arguments below.
\end{assumption}

\begin{lemma}[Polynomial factors are absorbed by exponential decay]
\label{lem:poly_exp}
Let $\alpha>0$ and $k\in\mathbb{N}$. Then for every $\alpha'\in(0,\alpha)$
there exists $N_0\ge1$ and $C_{k,\alpha,\alpha'}>0$ such that
\[
  N^k e^{-\alpha N}\le C_{k,\alpha,\alpha'} e^{-\alpha' N}
  \qquad(N\ge N_0).
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
This is immediate from
$N^k e^{-(\alpha-\alpha')N}\to0$ as $N\to\infty$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Conditional exponential PSWF coefficient decay]\label{lem:coeff_decay}
Assume Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity} and~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds}.
Then there exist constants $C_{m,n}$ and a rate $\alpha=\alpha(N)>0$,
possibly depending on $N$, such that the resulting exponential decay remains
uniform over the index range $0\le m,n\le N\sim c$ after incorporating the
growth control from Assumption~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds}, and
\[
  |\widehat f_{mn}(k)|
  :=|\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|\le C_{m,n}\,e^{-\alpha k}
  \qquad(k\ge0).
\]
Consequently,
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}\le C'_{m,n}\,e^{-\alpha N}.
\]
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
For functions analytic in a strip, the coefficients in the PSWF basis decay
exponentially; this follows from the spectral approximation theory of
\cite{BonamiKaroui2014,BonamiKaroui} together with
Assumption~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}. To our knowledge, uniform control of
this type for the product family $f_{mn}=\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psi_n^{(c)}}$
in the full index range $0\le m,n\le N\sim c$ is not explicitly available
in the literature and is therefore isolated here through
Assumption~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds}. The controlled-growth hypothesis in
Assumption~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds} ensures that the prefactors remain
compatible with an overall exponential decay statement uniform over the
index range $0\le m,n\le N\sim c$, after incorporating the allowed growth
into the constants and, if necessary, into a slightly reduced exponential
rate via Lemma~\ref{lem:poly_exp}. The tail bound follows by summing the
geometric series $\sum_{k\ge N}|\widehat f_{mn}(k)|^2$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Status of the PSWF step]\label{rem:pswf_status}
The PSWF part of the argument is not purely formal. Its force comes from
analytic continuation of the product functions $f_{mn}$ together with
quantitative control of the dependence on $m$, $n$, and $c$. Thus the PSWF
product analysis is itself a substantive ingredient of the paper, not a
routine closure argument.
\end{remark}

\section{XRY Spectral Stability Hypothesis}\label{sec:xry_accuracy}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{remark}[Why the XRY stability hypothesis is structurally plausible]
The XRY construction is built from the same PSWF spectral data that underlie
the projector $\PN$, so it is reasonable to expect its quadrature error to
be governed primarily by the PSWF spectral tail of the test function, up to
a residual term that is exponentially small in the regime $N\sim c$. This
does not prove Assumption~\ref{ass:xry_stability}, but it explains why a
bound of the form
\[
  |Q_N(f)-\int_{-T}^T f|\lesssim \|(I-\PN)f\| + e^{-\alpha N}\norm{f}_{L^2}
\]
represents a natural spectral stability statement rather than an ad hoc
reformulation of the original compatibility problem.
\end{remark}

\begin{assumption}[XRY spectral stability]\label{ass:xry_stability}
There exist constants $\CXRY,C,\alpha>0$, depending on the XRY
construction parameters and on the regime $N\sim c$, such that for every
analytic $f\in L^2([-T,T])$,
\[
  \left|Q_N(f)-\int_{-T}^T f(x)\,dx\right|
  \le \CXRY\,\|(I-\PN)f\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
      + C\,e^{-\alpha N}\,\norm{f}_{L^2([-T,T])}.
\]
This hypothesis formalises the stability bridge between the PSWF spectral
truncation and the XRY quadrature rule. It is consistent with the structural
formulas and numerical behaviour of \cite{Xiao2001,OsipovRokhlinXiao}, but
is not claimed here as a theorem extracted verbatim from those sources.
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}[Why this is a reduction, not a closure]
Assumption~\ref{ass:xry_stability} isolates the only XRY-specific input
used in this paper beyond standard PSWF spectral theory. Accordingly, the
present work should be read as reducing the quadrature compatibility problem
of \cite{PaperI} to a more explicit XRY stability hypothesis, not as an
unconditional verification of that compatibility condition.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why the new hypothesis is meaningful]
The XRY stability hypothesis is not introduced merely to rename the original
problem. It has a more operational form than Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI},
because it directly compares the quadrature functional $Q_N$ with the PSWF
spectral truncation error and separates a truncation term from an intrinsic
quadrature residual. This makes the remaining analytic task more specific,
even though it is not yet fully resolved here.
\end{remark}

\section{Conditional Verification of Assumption~3.10}\label{sec:verification}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{proposition}[Conditional exponential quadrature defect bound]\label{prop:defect_bound}
Assume Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability}.
Then for all $0\le m,n\le N-1$,
\[
  |E_{mn}|=|R_{mn}^{\mathrm{quad}}|\le C\,e^{-\alpha N},
\]
where $C,\alpha>0$ depend only on the PSWF analyticity regime and the XRY
stability constants.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Applying Assumption~\ref{ass:xry_stability} to $f=f_{mn}$ gives
\[
  |E_{mn}|\le \CXRY\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|+Ce^{-\alpha N}\norm{f_{mn}}_{L^2}.
\]
By Lemma~\ref{lem:coeff_decay}, $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|\le C'_{m,n}e^{-\alpha N}$.
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds}, these prefactors are compatible
with absorption into a single exponential rate, possibly reduced, using that
any polynomial growth in $N$ can be absorbed into a slightly reduced
exponential rate $e^{-\alpha N}$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:poly_exp}.
All exponential contributions are therefore absorbed into a single rate
$\alpha$, possibly reduced, and all multiplicative constants are absorbed
into $C$.
This yields the stated bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Uniform domination over the index range]\label{rem:index_domination}
For $0\le m,n\le N-1$ one has $|m-n|\le N-1$. Hence any comparison between
an $N$-dependent exponential quantity and an algebraic factor
$(1+|m-n|)^{-p}$ is understood in the worst-case sense over the admissible
index range, namely via $(1+|m-n|)^{-p}\ge (1+N)^{-p}$. This is the precise
sense in which an exponential bound in $N$ dominates the algebraic defect
profile required in Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI}.
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[Conditional verification of Assumption~3.10]\label{thm:compatibility_verified}
Assume Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability}.
Let $(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{w})$ be the XRY PSWF-Gauss rule of order $N\sim c$.
Then for every $p\ge1$ there exist constants $C_p,C>0$ such that
\[
  |E_{mn}|\le C_p(1+|m-n|)^{-p}+Cc^{-1/3}
  \qquad(0\le m,n\le N-1).
\]
Hence Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI} holds conditionally for PSWF-Gauss
sampling under the stated hypotheses.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:defect_bound}, $|E_{mn}|\le Ce^{-\alpha N}$.
By Remark~\ref{rem:index_domination}, the exponential bound dominates any
algebraic factor $(1+|m-n|)^{-p}$ uniformly over the index range after
adjusting constants. Similarly, $e^{-\alpha N}\le Cc^{-1/3}$ in the regime
$N\sim c$ for $c$ sufficiently large. Thus both terms in
Assumption~3.10 are dominated.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What has been achieved]
The preceding theorem does not close the construction problem of
\cite{PaperI} unconditionally. What it provides is a clean reduction:
once Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability} are
established, the compatibility assumption of \cite{PaperI} follows for XRY
PSWF-Gauss quadrature.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Conditional defect norm bound]\label{cor:defect_norm}
Assume Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability}.
Then there exists $C_*>0$ such that
\[
  \norm{\EN}_2\le\norm{\EN}_F\le N C_* e^{-\alpha N}\xrightarrow{N\to\infty}0.
\]
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:defect_bound}, each of the $N^2$ matrix entries of
$\EN$ is bounded by $C_*e^{-\alpha N}$. Summing their squares yields
$\norm{\EN}_F^2\le N^2 C_*^2 e^{-2\alpha N}$, hence
$\norm{\EN}_F\le N C_* e^{-\alpha N}$.
\end{proof}

\section{Conditional Frame Stability for PSWF-Gauss Sampling}\label{sec:stability}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Conditional frame stability for PSWF-Gauss sampling]
\label{thm:frame_stability}
Assume Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity}--\ref{ass:xry_stability}.
Let $(\mathbf{p},\mathbf{w})$ be the XRY PSWF-Gauss rule of order $N\sim c$.
Once $NC_*e^{-\alpha N}<\lambda_{N-1}(c)$, $\{(p_j,w_j)\}_{j=1}^N$ is a weighted
frame for $V_{N,c}$ with bounds
\[
  A(N,c)\,\norm{f}_{L^2}^2
  \le \sum_{j=1}^N w_j|f(p_j)|^2
  \le B(N,c)\,\norm{f}_{L^2}^2,
  \qquad f\in V_{N,c},
\]
where
\[
  A(N,c)=\lambda_{N-1}(c)-NC_*e^{-\alpha N}>0,
  \qquad
  B(N,c)=\lambda_0(c)+NC_*e^{-\alpha N}.
\]
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The Weyl perturbation bound applied to
$\GVM=G^{\mathrm{ref}}_{N,c}+\EN$ gives
$\lambda_{\min}(\GVM)\ge\lambda_{N-1}(c)-\norm{\EN}_2\ge A(N,c)>0$
by Corollary~\ref{cor:defect_norm}.
The upper bound is analogous.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Meaning of the result]
The defect $\EN$ is controlled by two separate ingredients: the PSWF
spectral tail of analytic product integrands and the assumed XRY stability
hypothesis. Under these inputs, the abstract conditional framework of
\cite{PaperI} becomes effective for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Critical density regime]\label{rem:critical}
The condition $A(N,c)>0$ holds in the Landau--Widom bulk regime
$N<(2/\pi-\varepsilon)c$, where $\lambda_{N-1}(c)\ge1-Ce^{-\eta c}$
dominates the exponential defect. Near the critical density
$N\to(2/\pi)c$, the eigenvalue $\lambda_{N-1}(c)\to0$ and the
stability condition may fail; see Open Problem~\ref{op:critical}.
\end{remark}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%% ==================================================================

\begin{problem}[Derive the XRY stability hypothesis]
Prove Assumption~\ref{ass:xry_stability} rigorously from the structural
formulas and numerical analysis of \cite{Xiao2001,OsipovRokhlinXiao},
with explicit constants in terms of $N$, $c$, and the XRY construction
parameters.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Quantify PSWF product tails]
Derive Assumptions~\ref{ass:pswf_analyticity} and~\ref{ass:uniform_bounds}
from PSWF analyticity theory with explicit dependence on $m$, $n$, $c$, and
$T$. In particular, clarify the admissible degeneration of strip widths and
the prefactor growth compatible with the exponential defect estimate.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Node separation and weight bounds]
Prove Assumptions~\ref{ass:node_sep} and~\ref{ass:weights} quantitatively,
with explicit constants in $N$ and $c$.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Critical density regime]\label{op:critical}
Analyse frame stability for PSWF-Gauss sampling near the critical density
$N\to(2/\pi)c$, where $\lambda_{N-1}(c)\to0$ and the Weyl argument
of Theorem~\ref{thm:frame_stability} degenerates.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Alternative compatible quadrature classes]
Characterise quadrature rules beyond the XRY PSWF-Gauss class satisfying
Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI}.
\end{problem}
