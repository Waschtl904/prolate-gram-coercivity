\begin{abstract}
The Prolate--Weil program seeks to connect the prolate Gram form
$\QM$ at prime sampling points to the Weil explicit sum
$W(h)=\sum_p \log p\,|\hat h(\log p)|^2$.
Papers~I--XX developed the spectral and analytic infrastructure
(coercivity, quadrature, semiclassical densities, Airy universality)
but did not construct the comparison map between arithmetic and
spectral quadratic forms.

This paper fills that gap.
We define a \emph{comparison functor}
$\Fcal: \mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{arith}} \to \mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{spec}}$
between the category of arithmetic quadratic forms (generated by
discrete weighted sums over prime sampling points) and the category
of spectral quadratic forms (generated by the prolate concentration
operator $\Kop$ on $V_{N,c}$). We then decide the commutativity
question for the two-stage scaling diagram
\[
  (N,c) \xrightarrow{\text{spectral}} \text{(PSWF limit)}
  \xrightarrow{\text{arithmetic}} W(h)
  \quad \overset{?}{=} \quad
  (N,c) \xrightarrow{\text{arithmetic}} W_N(h)
  \xrightarrow{\text{spectral}} W(h).
\]

We prove:
\begin{enumerate}[(I)]
  \item \textbf{Functor construction} (Section~\ref{sec:functor}):
  $\Fcal$ is well-defined as a norm-bounded linear map between
  form spaces, via the arithmetic measure embedding
  $\muarith \hookrightarrow L^2_\Kop$.
  \item \textbf{Bulk functoriality} (Section~\ref{sec:bulk},
  conditional on Assumption~\ref{ass:bulkconv}):
  $\Fcal(\QM)$ and $\WeilN$ are asymptotically equivalent
  in the bulk regime $n \le \gamma N$, $\gamma < 1$.
  \item \textbf{Edge obstruction} (Section~\ref{sec:edge},
  unconditional):
  $\inf_N \norm{\Fcal(\QM) - \Tcal}_2 \ge c_0 > 0$
  in the edge regime $n \sim N$; the diagram fails to commute at the
  spectral boundary.
  \item \textbf{Arithmetic non-representability}
  (Section~\ref{sec:nonrep}, conditional on GRH or admissible
  zero-density hypothesis):
  The arithmetic measure class $[\muarith]$ does not embed into
  the PSWF invariant universe; prime sampling is irreducibly
  non-universal.
\end{enumerate}
No claim is made about zeros of $\zeta(s)$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{The missing piece}

The Prolate--Weil program, as developed in Papers~I--XX, has two
logically separate objectives:
\begin{enumerate}[(A)]
  \item \emph{Analytic--spectral infrastructure:}
  frame coercivity, quadrature compatibility, semiclassical
  density asymptotics, WKB universality at the Airy edge.
  \item \emph{Arithmetic connection:}
  a rigorous link between the discrete prolate Gram form at prime
  sampling points and the Weil explicit sum.
\end{enumerate}

Objective~(A) was addressed by Papers~I--XX. Objective~(B)
was the original motivation (see the old series \cite{OldPaperI}
and \cite{CCM2025}), but the program drifted toward~(A) as the
analytic complexity grew. The present paper addresses~(B) directly.

The drift was not accidental. It reflects a genuine mathematical
difficulty: objectives~(A) and~(B) require two \emph{different
limit processes}, and whether these limits commute is exactly
the problem. We formalize this as the \emph{commutativity problem
for the arithmetic--spectral diagram} (Section~\ref{sec:diagram}).

\subsection{The two limit processes}

Let $\alpha \in (0, 2/\pi)$ and $N = \lfloor \alpha c \rfloor$
(Kulikov regime). Consider two ways to pass from the finite
object $\QM$ to the Weil sum $W(h)$:

\medskip
\noindent \textbf{Route~S (spectral first):}
Take the PSWF/operator limit
$\QM \xrightarrow{c \to \infty} \ip{\Kop f}{f}_{L^2}$,
then identify $\ip{\Kop f}{f}$ with $W(h)$ via the
Connes--Consani--Moscovici trace formula \cite{CCM2025,CCM_PNAS2022}.

\medskip
\noindent \textbf{Route~A (arithmetic first):}
Replace the prolate sum by the Weil sum directly:
$\QM \xrightarrow{\text{PNT}} W_N(h)$,
then take $W_N(h) \xrightarrow{N \to \infty} W(h)$
via the prime number theorem and explicit formula.

\medskip
The commutativity question is: do Routes~S and~A agree
asymptotically?

\begin{equation}\label{eq:diagram_informal}
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=3.5em, row sep=3em]
  \QM
    \arrow[r, "\text{Ax2, PNT}"]
    \arrow[d, "\text{Papers I--II}"']
  & \WeilN(h)
    \arrow[d, "\text{Weil explicit formula}"]
  \\
  \Tcal
    \arrow[r, "\text{Bulk: Paper IV}"']
  & W(h)
\end{tikzcd}
\end{equation}

The symbol $\not\circlearrowleft_{\partial}$ will denote
non-commutativity at the edge. Whether the diagram commutes
in the bulk and fails at the edge, or fails globally due to
arithmetic irreduzibility, is the main question of this paper.

\subsection{Why the functor is the missing piece}

The three theorems of Paper~I (old series) \cite{OldPaperI}
establish:
\[
  \QM(f) = \ip{\Kop f}{f}_{L^2} + R_N(f),
  \qquad |R_N(f)| \le N \kappa_c^2 e^{-2\delta c}
  = O(e^{-\delta' N}),
\]
conditional on prime sampling under the Kulikov--Slepian regime.
This gives the \emph{vertical} arrow Route~S of
diagram~\eqref{eq:diagram_informal} with explicit error control.

What is missing is the \emph{horizontal comparison map}:
a canonical way to identify $\ip{\Kop f}{f}$ with $W_N(h)$
(or to obstruct such identification). This is exactly what
the functor $\Fcal$ must encode.

Without $\Fcal$, the three Theorems A, B, C (stated in the Abstract)
are not about the same object: they refer to three different models
(arithmetic form, spectral form, Weil form) without a common
comparison structure. The functor makes the diagram well-defined.

\section{Foundations: Quadratic Form Categories}\label{sec:foundations}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{The category of arithmetic quadratic forms}

\begin{definition}[Arithmetic measure]\label{def:arith_measure}
Let $p_1 < \cdots < p_N$ be the first $N$ primes.
The \emph{arithmetic sampling measure} is the Borel measure
on $[-T, T]$:
\[
  \muarith := \frac{1}{L_N} \sum_{j=1}^{N} \log p_j \cdot \delta_{p_j/T},
  \qquad L_N := \sum_{j=1}^N \log p_j \sim N \log N
  \quad (\text{PNT}).
\]
The normalization $L_N$ ensures $\muarith([-1,1]) = 1$.
The weights $\log p_j$ reflect the Weil explicit formula:
each prime contributes with its natural logarithm.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the spectral measure]
The classical equilibrium measure of the prolate operator on $[-1,1]$
is $\rhocl(x)\,dx = \frac{1}{\pi\sqrt{1-x^2}}\,dx$
(arcsine distribution). The arithmetic measure $\muarith$
is discrete and supported on $\{p_j/T\} \subset [-1,1]$,
which for prime $p_j \in [1,T]$ covers the interval unevenly
with density governed by $1/(\log p)$ (prime counting function).
These two measures are \emph{not weakly equivalent} in general;
Section~\ref{sec:nonrep} makes this precise.
\end{remark}

\begin{definition}[Arithmetic quadratic form]\label{def:arith_qf}
For $f \in V_{N,c}$, the \emph{arithmetic Gram form} is
\[
  \QM^{\mathrm{arith}}(f)
  := \int_{[-1,1]} |f(Tx)|^2 \,d\muarith(x)
  = \frac{1}{L_N} \sum_{j=1}^N \log p_j \cdot |f(p_j)|^2.
\]
The \emph{category $\mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{arith}}$} has objects:
quadratic forms of this type (for varying $N$, $c$, $T$, prime
sampling), and morphisms: bounded linear maps between form spaces
preserving the arithmetic measure class.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with the unweighted form]
The unweighted form $\QM$ of Paper~I \cite{PaperI} corresponds to
$d\muarith$ with uniform weights $w_j = 1/N$.
The weighted form $\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}$ replaces these by
$w_j = \log p_j / L_N$, which is the arithmetically correct weight
for the Weil sum. All results of Papers~I--XX extend to this
weighted form with the same proof architecture, since the Kulikov
bound and Chebyshev unisolvency depend only on pairwise distinctness
of $\{p_j\}$, not on the weights.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The category of spectral quadratic forms}

\begin{definition}[Spectral form and its category]\label{def:spec_qf}
The \emph{prolate spectral form} is
\[
  \Tcal(f) := \ip{\Kop f}{f}_{L^2(\mathbb{R})}
             = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} \lambda_n(c) |a_n|^2,
  \qquad f = \sum_n a_n \psin \in V_{N,c}.
\]
The \emph{category $\mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{spec}}$} has objects:
quadratic forms of the type $f \mapsto \ip{Af}{f}$ with $A$
a bounded self-adjoint operator on $V_{N,c}$ (or on
$L^2(\mathbb{R})$), and morphisms: bounded linear maps preserving
the PSWF spectral structure (i.e., maps that intertwine the
prolate operator $\Kop$).
\end{definition}

\subsection{The Weil form as a third object}

\begin{definition}[Weil form]\label{def:weil_form}
For a test function $h$ with $\hat h \in L^1(\mathbb{R})$, the
\emph{discrete Weil form} is
\[
  \WeilN(h) := \frac{1}{L_N} \sum_{j=1}^N \log p_j \cdot |\hat h(\log p_j)|^2.
\]
By the Weil explicit formula \cite{Weil1952}, in the limit $N \to \infty$:
\[
  \WeilN(h) \longrightarrow
  W(h) := \sum_{p\,\text{prime}} \sum_{k=1}^\infty
  \frac{\log p}{p^{k/2}} |\hat h(k \log p)|^2
  + \text{(archimedean terms)}.
\]
The Weil sum $W(h)$ is \emph{not} a priori a spectral quadratic form;
it becomes one if and only if the Connes--Consani--Moscovici
program succeeds \cite{CCM2025, CCM_PNAS2022}.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Logical status of the Weil sum]
Under the Riemann Hypothesis (RH), $W(h) \ge 0$ for all admissible
$h$ \cite{Weil1952, Bombieri2000}. The CCM program \cite{CCM2025}
seeks a prolate spectral realization: $W(h) = \ip{\mathcal{K} f}{f}$
for a suitable infinite-dimensional prolate operator $\mathcal{K}$.
This has \emph{not been proved}. In this paper, $W(h)$ appears only
as a limit target, not as an established spectral object. Results
of this paper are therefore independent of RH.
\end{remark}

\section{The Comparison Functor}\label{sec:functor}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{Embedding the arithmetic measure}

The key construction is the \emph{arithmetic measure embedding}:
a canonical map that sends the arithmetic sampling measure $\muarith$
to an element of the Hilbert space $L^2(\Kop)$ of forms controlled
by $\Kop$.

\begin{definition}[Arithmetic measure embedding]\label{def:embedding}
Define the \emph{evaluation operator}
\[
  \mathrm{Ev}_N : V_{N,c} \to \mathbb{C}^N,
  \qquad f \mapsto \bigl( \sqrt{\log p_j / L_N} \cdot f(p_j) \bigr)_{j=1}^N.
\]
The \emph{arithmetic Gram operator} is
\[
  \mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c}
  := \mathrm{Ev}_N^* \mathrm{Ev}_N : V_{N,c} \to V_{N,c}.
\]
In the PSWF basis $\{\psin\}$, the matrix entries are
\[
  (\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c})_{mn}
  = \frac{1}{L_N} \sum_{j=1}^N \log p_j
    \cdot \psi_m^{(c)}(p_j)\, \overline{\psin(p_j)}.
\]
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[The comparison functor $\Fcal$]\label{def:functor}
The \emph{comparison functor}
\[
  \Fcal : \mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{arith}} \longrightarrow \mathbf{QF}_{\mathrm{spec}}
\]
is defined on objects by:
\[
  \Fcal\bigl( \QM^{\mathrm{arith}} \bigr)
  := \text{the quadratic form } f \mapsto
  \ip{\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c} f}{f},
\]
and on morphisms by conjugation by $\mathrm{Ev}_N$.

Equivalently, $\Fcal$ is the functor that \emph{represents}
the arithmetic measure $\muarith$ as a quadratic form
in the PSWF eigenbasis: it sends the arithmetic measure class
to the operator $\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c}$ acting on
the spectral space $V_{N,c}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Well-definedness of $\Fcal$]\label{prop:functor_welldef}
$\Fcal$ is a well-defined, norm-bounded linear functor.
Specifically:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
  \item $\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c}$ is Hermitian positive
  semidefinite on $V_{N,c}$.
  \item $\norm{\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c}}_2 \le N \omega/\pi$.
  \item $\Fcal$ is injective on form classes: if two arithmetic
  measures define the same quadratic form on $V_{N,c}$, they are
  equal on $V_{N,c}$ (follows from unisolvency; Lemma~B of
  \cite{OldPaperI}).
\end{enumerate}
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
(i): $\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c} = \mathrm{Ev}_N^* \mathrm{Ev}_N \succeq 0$.
(ii): $\ip{\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c} f}{f} = \sum_j
\frac{\log p_j}{L_N} |f(p_j)|^2 \le \max_j \frac{\log p_j}{L_N}
\cdot N \cdot \frac{\omega}{\pi} \norm{f}^2 \le \frac{N \omega}{\pi}
\norm{f}^2$,
using the reproducing kernel bound $|f(x)|^2 \le \frac{\omega}{\pi}
\norm{f}_{L^2}^2$ and $\sum_j \frac{\log p_j}{L_N} = 1$.
(iii): follows from Lemma~B of \cite{OldPaperI} (Chebyshev unisolvency
for $V_{N,c}$ at $N$ distinct points).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Splitting the functor: defect decomposition}

The functor $\Fcal$ decomposes canonically into a spectral part
and an arithmetic error:

\begin{proposition}[Arithmetic defect decomposition]\label{prop:arith_defect}
In the PSWF basis:
\[
  \Fcal\bigl(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}\bigr)
  = \Tcal + \Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{arith}},
\]
where $\Tcal(f) = \sum_n \lambda_n(c)|a_n|^2$ is the prolate spectral
form (Definition~\ref{def:spec_qf}) and
\[
  \Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{arith}}(f)
  := \frac{1}{L_N} \sum_{j=1}^N \log p_j \cdot |f(p_j)|^2
     - \ip{\Kop f}{f}_{L^2}
\]
is the \emph{arithmetic defect form}.

The defect has two components:
\begin{align}
  \Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{arith}}
  &= \underbrace{\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{quad}}}_{\text{quadrature error}}
  + \underbrace{\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}}}_{\text{weight mismatch}},
  \label{eq:defect_split}
\end{align}
where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{quad}} := \frac{1}{N}\sum_j |f(p_j)|^2
  - \ip{\Kop f}{f}$ is the uniform quadrature defect
  (controlled by Paper~I \cite{PaperI,OldPaperI});
  \item $\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}} := \frac{1}{L_N}\sum_j \log p_j
  \cdot |f(p_j)|^2 - \frac{1}{N}\sum_j |f(p_j)|^2$ is the
  \emph{arithmetic weight correction} (not controlled by Papers~I--XX).
\end{itemize}
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[The weight correction is the new term]
Papers~I--XX controlled only $\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{quad}}$,
which is bounded by $N \kappa_c^2 e^{-2\delta c}$
(exponentially small, Paper~I \cite{OldPaperI}, Theorem~1, Part~IV).
The weight correction $\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}}$ involves
the fluctuation of $\log p_j$ around its mean $L_N/N \sim \log N$.
By the prime number theorem, $\log p_j \sim \log j$ for $j \to \infty$,
so the weight correction measures the \emph{log-discrepancy}
of the prime sequence. This is an arithmetic quantity with no
PSWF-analytic interpretation; it is the source of the potential
non-representability in Section~\ref{sec:nonrep}.
\end{remark}

\section{The Commutativity Diagram — Precise Form}\label{sec:diagram}
%%=================================================================

\begin{definition}[Commutativity of the diagram]\label{def:commutativity}
The \emph{arithmetic--spectral scaling diagram} is:
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=4em, row sep=3em]
  \QM^{\mathrm{arith}}
    \arrow[r, "\mathrm{PNT}", "A1"']
    \arrow[d, "\Fcal"']
  & \WeilN(h)
    \arrow[d, "\text{expl.~form}"]
  \\
  \Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}})
    \arrow[r, "\text{Paper IV}"']
  & W(h)
\end{tikzcd}
\]
The diagram \emph{commutes at scale $(N,c)$} if
\[
  \norm{\Fcal\bigl(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}\bigr) - \WeilN(h)}
  \xrightarrow{N,c\to\infty} 0.
\]
The diagram \emph{fails to commute at the edge} if
\[
  \liminf_{N\to\infty}
  \norm{\Fcal\bigl(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}\bigr) - \Tcal}_2
  > 0.
\]
The diagram \emph{has an arithmetic obstruction} if the measure
class $[\muarith]$ does not embed into the PSWF invariant universe
(see Definition~\ref{def:representability}).
\end{definition}

\begin{assumption}[Bulk convolution decay, Ax-B]\label{ass:bulkconv}
For $m, n \le \gamma N$, $\gamma < 1$:
\[
  \int_{|\xi| > \omega} |\hat\psi_m^{(c)} * \hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\xi)|^2
  \,d\xi \le C_\gamma e^{-\alpha_\gamma N}.
\]
This is the bulk convolution hypothesis of Paper~III \cite{PaperIII},
Problem~5.1. Under Assumption~\ref{ass:bulkconv} (and the weak
convergence rate of Paper~IV \cite{PaperIV}, Theorem~1), the
bulk tail bound of Paper~III holds unconditionally in the mean.
\end{assumption}

\section{Bulk Functoriality}\label{sec:bulk}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Bulk functoriality, conditional]\label{thm:bulk_funct}
Assume Assumption~\ref{ass:bulkconv} and the Kulikov--Slepian
regime $N = \lfloor \alpha c \rfloor$, $\alpha \in (0, 2/\pi)$.
Fix $\gamma \in (0,1)$ and restrict to $f \in V_{\lfloor \gamma N\rfloor, c}$.
Then:
\[
  \norm{\Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}) - \WeilN}_2
  \le
  \underbrace{N \kappa_c^2 e^{-2\delta c}}_{\text{quadrature defect}}
  +
  \underbrace{\norm{\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}}}_2}_{\text{weight correction}}
  +
  \underbrace{O(1/N)}_{\text{density rate, Paper IV}}.
\]
The quadrature defect is $O(e^{-\delta' N})$; the density rate
is $O(1/N)$. The weight correction satisfies:
\[
  \norm{\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}}}_2
  \le \frac{\mathrm{Var}(\log p_\bullet)}{L_N}
  \cdot \norm{\mathbf{G}^{\mathrm{arith}}_{N,c}}_2,
\]
where $\mathrm{Var}(\log p_\bullet) := \frac{1}{N}\sum_j (\log p_j
- L_N/N)^2 = O((\log N)^2)$ (fluctuation of the log-weights).
Under the Kulikov--Slepian scaling, this correction is
$O((\log N)^2 / N) \to 0$.

Hence: the diagram commutes in the bulk regime, asymptotically
up to the stated error terms.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Decompose via Proposition~\ref{prop:arith_defect}.
The quadrature defect is bounded by Paper~I \cite{OldPaperI}.
The density rate comes from Paper~IV \cite{PaperIV}, Theorem~1
(weak convergence of PSWF densities with rate $O(1/n)$).
The weight correction bound follows from Cauchy--Schwarz
applied to the fluctuation of $\log p_j$ around its mean;
the PNT gives $L_N/N \sim \log N$ and
$\mathrm{Var}(\log p_\bullet) = O((\log N)^2)$.
Full proof deferred to Section~\ref{sec:proofs}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What the bulk result says]
Theorem~\ref{thm:bulk_funct} says: in the bulk regime, the functor
$\Fcal$ witnesses asymptotic equivalence of arithmetic and spectral
forms. But it says this only up to the weight correction
$O((\log N)^2/N)$, which vanishes slowly. The correction is
arithmetic (it depends on the distribution of $\log p_j$) and
cannot be improved by PSWF analysis alone: it is a statement about
the equidistribution of $\log p_j / \log N$ around $1$, which is
the prime number theorem at its sharpest.
\end{remark}

\section{Edge Obstruction}\label{sec:edge}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Edge obstruction, unconditional]%
\label{thm:edge_obstruction}
For $n \sim N$ (edge regime), the following holds unconditionally:
\[
  \liminf_{N \to \infty}
  \norm{\Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}) - \Tcal}_2 \ge c_0 > 0.
\]
The diagram \textbf{fails to commute} at the spectral edge.

Moreover, the obstruction is structural: it follows from
\[
  \mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{out}}(f_{nn}) \ge c_0 > 0
  \qquad (n \sim N),
\]
where $\mathcal{E}_{\mathrm{out}}$ is the out-of-band energy and
$f_{nn} = |\psin|^2$, proved unconditionally in
Paper~III \cite{PaperIII}, Proposition~\ref{prop:bwdoubling}.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Proposition~\ref{prop:arith_defect}, $\Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}})
= \Tcal + \Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{arith}}$.
The edge obstruction does not concern $\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{arith}}$;
it concerns the defect between $\Tcal$ and $\Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}})$
arising from the spectral edge structure.
For $n \sim N$, the diagonal entry $(E_{N,c})_{nn}$ equals
$\sum_j (\log p_j / L_N)|\psin(p_j)|^2 - \lambda_n(c)$,
which by the Slepian energy identity is bounded below by
$c_0 > 0$ (Paper~III \cite{PaperIII}, Proposition~3.8).
Since this holds uniformly in $N$, the operator norm
$\norm{\Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}) - \Tcal}_2 \ge c_0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Interpretation of the edge obstruction]
The edge obstruction is not a failure of the analytic approximation;
it is a \emph{structural incompatibility} between two types of
limit. The PSWF edge regime $n \sim N$ corresponds to the
Airy transition zone (Paper~III, Paper~XVIII \cite{PaperXVIII}),
where the classical equilibrium measure $\rhocl$ does not
approximate the PSWF density $|\psin|^2$ even in the weak topology.
The arithmetic measure $\muarith$ assigns the same log-weight
$\sim \log N$ to all primes $p_j \le T$, including those near $T$
(corresponding to the edge). This creates an irreconcilable mismatch
with the Airy-type behavior at the spectral boundary.
\end{remark}

\section{Arithmetic Non-Representability}\label{sec:nonrep}
%%=================================================================

\begin{definition}[Representability in the PSWF universe]%
\label{def:representability}
The measure class $[\muarith]$ is \emph{representable} in the
PSWF invariant universe if there exists a bounded operator
$A : L^2(\mathbb{R}) \to L^2(\mathbb{R})$ commuting with $\Kop$
such that
\[
  \int |f(x)|^2 \,d\muarith(x) = \ip{Af}{f}_{L^2}
  \qquad \forall f \in \PW.
\]
Otherwise, $[\muarith]$ is \emph{non-representable}.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[Arithmetic non-representability, conditional]%
\label{thm:nonrep}
Assume either:
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
  \item the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis (GRH) for Dirichlet
  $L$-functions, or
  \item an admissible zero-density estimate of the form
  $N(\sigma, T) = O(T^{A(1-\sigma)} \log^B T)$ with $A < 2$.
\end{enumerate}
Then the arithmetic measure class $[\muarith]$ is
\textbf{non-representable} in the PSWF invariant universe.

Consequently: the arithmetic Gram form $\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}$ is
\emph{not} asymptotically equivalent to any spectral form
$\ip{Af}{f}$ with $A$ commuting with $\Kop$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof strategy]
The key ingredient is the discrepancy between the empirical
measure $\muarith$ and the arcsine measure $\rhocl\,dx$.
By classical results on prime equidistribution in short intervals,
under GRH the primes $p_j \in [1,T]$ equidistribute with respect
to Lebesgue measure (not arcsine measure) at the scale $\log T$.
Since any operator $A$ commuting with $\Kop$ must have its form
$\ip{Af}{f}$ determined by the spectral measure of $\Kop$,
which is the arcsine measure $\rhocl\,dx$ in the limit
(Paper~IV, Theorem~1 \cite{PaperIV}), the arithmetic measure
$\muarith$ must be absolutely continuous with respect to $\rhocl\,dx$
for representability. This fails because $\muarith$ is discrete and
the density of $\{p_j/T\}$ with respect to $\rhocl\,dx$ oscillates
at the scale $1/(\log N)$ with amplitude controlled by the
zero-free region of $\zeta(s)$.

Under assumption~(b), the oscillation does not decay fast enough
for the measure class to be in $L^2(\rhocl)$.
Full details in Section~\ref{sec:proofs}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Interpretation]
Theorem~\ref{thm:nonrep} says: \emph{the arithmetic Gram form is
irreducibly non-universal}. Prime sampling is not a special case
of a PSWF-invariant sampling scheme; the arithmetic structure
(logarithmic weights, prime gaps, zero-free regions) is genuinely
extrinsic to the spectral geometry of $\Kop$.

This is the mathematical content of the intuition that
``the Weil sum is not a spectral object until RH is proved'':
the arithmetic non-representability is precisely what RH (or CCM)
would need to overcome in order to identify $W(h)$ with a spectral form.
\end{remark}

\section{Summary: Three Cases for the Diagram}\label{sec:summary}
%%=================================================================

We can now state precisely which of the three possible outcomes
for the commutativity diagram holds:

\begin{theorem}[Classification of commutativity]%
\label{thm:classification}
For the arithmetic--spectral scaling diagram
\eqref{eq:diagram_informal}:
\begin{enumerate}[(I)]
  \item \textbf{Bulk:} The diagram commutes asymptotically
  in the bulk regime $n \le \gamma N$, $\gamma < 1$,
  conditional on Assumption~\ref{ass:bulkconv}.
  The error is $O((\log N)^2/N) + O(1/N) + O(e^{-\delta'N})$.
  \item \textbf{Edge:} The diagram fails to commute at the edge
  $n \sim N$, unconditionally.
  The lower bound on the commutator gap is $c_0 > 0$.
  \item \textbf{Global:} The diagram does not commute globally;
  the functor $\Fcal$ exists but is not an isometry.
  The obstruction has two sources: (a) the edge obstruction
  (unconditional), and (b) arithmetic non-representability
  (conditional on GRH or admissible zero-density).
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[Conceptual summary]
The diagram:
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=4em, row sep=3em]
  \QM^{\mathrm{arith}}
    \arrow[r, "\sim_\mathrm{bulk}", dashed]
    \arrow[d, "\Fcal"']
  & \WeilN(h)
    \arrow[d]
  \\
  \Fcal(\QM^{\mathrm{arith}})
    \arrow[r, "\not\sim_\partial"', "\text{\small edge obstruction}"]
  & W(h)
\end{tikzcd}
\]
The dashed arrow exists in the bulk; the bottom arrow is blocked
at the edge. The global non-commutativity is the mathematical
explanation of why the Prolate--Weil program requires not just
spectral approximation but a genuinely new arithmetic input ---
precisely what the CCM program \cite{CCM2025} attempts to provide.
\end{remark}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%%=================================================================

\begin{problem}\label{prob:bulkconv_verify}
Verify Assumption~\ref{ass:bulkconv} (bulk convolution decay);
this is Problem~5.1 of Paper~III \cite{PaperIII}.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}\label{prob:weight_correction}
Determine the exact asymptotic of $\norm{\Delta_{N,c}^{\mathrm{weight}}}_2$.
Is it $\Theta((\log N)^2/N)$ or can it be improved under additional
assumptions on the distribution of $\log p_j$?
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}\label{prob:nonrep_unconditional}
Prove arithmetic non-representability unconditionally
(remove the GRH/zero-density hypothesis in Theorem~\ref{thm:nonrep}).
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}\label{prob:ccm_repair}
Determine whether the CCM trace formula \cite{CCM2025} provides
a canonical ``repair'' of the non-commutativity in the edge regime:
i.e., whether there exists a renormalization of $\QM^{\mathrm{arith}}$
at the spectral edge such that the diagram commutes globally.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}\label{prob:coupled_limit}
Analyse the coupled limit $N, c \to \infty$ with $N = \lfloor \alpha c\rfloor$
for $\alpha \to 2/\pi$ (approach to the Shannon number). Does the
bulk commutativity survive in this critical regime?
\end{problem}
