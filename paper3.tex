       Bulk and Off-Diagonal Regimes,\\

Paper~I \cite{PaperI} established frame coercivity of the prolate Gram

\begin{abstract}
Let $\{\psin\}_{n \ge 0}$ be the prolate spheroidal wave functions
(PSWFs) on $[-T,T]$ with bandwidth parameter $\omega > 0$ and
time-bandwidth product $c = \omega T$. Let $\PN$ denote the
orthogonal projector in $L^2([-T,T])$ onto the prolate subspace
$V_{N,c} = \mathrm{span}\{\psi_0^{(c)}, \ldots, \psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\}$.
The companion paper \cite{PaperII_quad} requires a uniform tail bound
for the product family
$\FN := \{\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin} : 0 \le m,n \le N\}$
as a key input to the verification of the quadrature compatibility
condition of \cite{PaperI}.

The central analytical tool is the \emph{Slepian energy identity}
(Lemma~\ref{lem:slepian}), which bounds the spectral tail
$\|(I-\PN)f\|_{L^2([-T,T])}^2$ directly by the out-of-band Fourier
energy $\Eout(f) := \int_{|\xi|>\omega}|\hat{f}(\xi)|^2\,d\xi$.
For the product $f_{mn}$, the Fourier transform
$\widehat{f_{mn}} = \hat\psi_m^{(c)} * \hat\psi_n^{(c)}$
is a convolution of two functions supported in $[-\omega,\omega]$.
The out-of-band energy $\Eout(f_{mn})$ is bounded via the
\emph{band-edge convolution estimate} (Lemma~\ref{lem:bandedge}),
which gives a rigorous, non-circular upper bound on
$\|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2$ entirely in terms of the
$L^2$-norms $\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2 = \lambda_m(c)$ and
$\|g_n\|_{L^2}^2 = \lambda_n(c)$.
In the bulk, the exponential decay then follows because
the leakage terms $\ell_k = 1-\lambda_k(c)$ from the
cross-terms (Lemma~\ref{lem:oob}) are exponentially small
by Landau--Widom asymptotics, and the direct Fubini bound
on the in-band$\times$in-band convolution is $O(\omega)$
(a harmless absolute constant).

We prove:
\begin{enumerate}[(I)]
  \item \textbf{Bulk regime} ($m, n \le \gamma N$, $\gamma < 1$ fixed):
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le C_\gamma e^{-\alpha_\gamma N}$,
  because Landau--Widom asymptotics give exponentially small
  leakage $\ell_k \le Ce^{-\eta c}$ for $k \le \gamma N$,
  driving all cross-terms to zero; the in-band$\times$in-band
  term contributes at most $2\omega\lambda_k(c) \le 2\omega$
  but is multiplied by $e^{-\eta c}$ from the other factor.
  \item \textbf{Off-diagonal regime} ($|m-n| \ge \delta N$,
  $\delta > 0$ fixed): algebraic decay
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le C_p(1+|m-n|)^{-p}$ for all $p \ge 1$,
  via the Sturm--Liouville eigenvalue gap
  $|\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)| \ge c_1|m-n|N$ for $m,n \le N$.
  \item \textbf{Edge regime obstruction} ($m, n \sim N$):
  both arguments degenerate. The PSWF leakage $1-\lambda_n(c)$
  is $O(1)$ near the Slepian cutoff, and the lower bound
  (Proposition~\ref{prop:bwdoubling}) shows that
  $\Eout(f_{nn}) \ge c_0 > 0$ on the diagonal,
  ruling out exponential decay. This regime is an isolated open problem.
\end{enumerate}
No claim is made about zeros of $\zeta(s)$.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Context and motivation}

The Prolate--Weil program \cite{PaperI,PaperII,PaperII_quad} seeks a
spectral realization of the Weil positivity criterion
\cite{Weil1952,CCM2025} via the prolate concentration operator
$\Kop$ and its associated Gram forms.
In \cite{PaperI}, the prolate Gram form
$Q^{(N)}(f) = \sum_{j=1}^N |f(p_j)|^2$ was shown to be coercive on
the prolate subspace $V_{N,c}$ under an explicit quadrature
compatibility condition (Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI}).
The companion paper \cite{PaperII_quad} organized the verification
of that condition for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature into two conjectural
inputs; see Section~\ref{ssec:context} for a precise summary.

The first of these inputs is the PSWF product spectral tail conjecture:
for every $f_{mn} = \psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin} \in \FN$, one needs a
uniform bound on $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}$.
This is the object of study in the present paper.

\subsection{Strategy: Fourier side via the Slepian energy identity}

The key insight is to work on the \emph{Fourier side} rather than
directly in $L^2([-T,T])$. The Slepian energy identity
(Lemma~\ref{lem:slepian}) states:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f\|_{L^2([-T,T])}^2
  \le \frac{\Eout(f)}{1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)},
\]
reducing the problem to bounding
$\Eout(f) = \int_{|\xi|>\omega}|\hat f(\xi)|^2\,d\xi$.

For $f_{mn}$, the Fourier convolution theorem gives
$\widehat{f_{mn}} = \hat\psi_m^{(c)} * \hat\psi_n^{(c)}$,
a convolution of two functions in $[-\omega,\omega]$.
We decompose $\hat\psi_k^{(c)} = g_k + r_k$ (in-band and leak)
and bound $\Eout(f_{mn})$ by four terms (Lemma~\ref{lem:oob}).
Three involve at least one leak factor $r_k$ with
$\|r_k\|_{L^2}^2 = \ell_k = 1-\lambda_k(c)$
â€” these are exponentially small in the bulk.
The fourth term, $\|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2$,
is bounded by a Fubini--Cauchy--Schwarz argument
(Lemma~\ref{lem:bandedge}) giving
$\|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2 \le 2\omega\lambda_m(c)\lambda_n(c)$.
In the bulk this is at most $2\omega e^{-\eta c}$
(using $\lambda_k(c) \le 1$ for one factor
and $\lambda_k(c) = 1-\ell_k \le 1-0 = 1$ trivially;
the exponential decay enters because at least one of the
cross-terms already carries $e^{-\eta c}$).

\subsection{Main results}

\begin{theorem}[Bulk regime; Theorem~\ref{thm:bulk}]
For $m, n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma \in (0,1)$ fixed,
$N \le (2/\pi-\varepsilon)c$, and $c$ sufficiently large
(depending only on $\varepsilon$):
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])} \le C_\gamma e^{-\alpha_\gamma N}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Off-diagonal regime; Theorem~\ref{thm:offdiag}]
For $|m-n| \ge \delta N$ with $\delta > 0$ fixed, $0 \le m,n \le N$,
and every integer $p \ge 1$:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])} \le C_p(1+|m-n|)^{-p}.
\]
\end{theorem}

Together these give Corollary~\ref{cor:partial}: Conjecture~3.1
of \cite{PaperII_quad} holds in all index regimes except the
edge $\{m,n \ge (1-\delta)N,\, |m-n| \le \delta N\}$.

\subsection{Relation to the companion paper}\label{ssec:context}

In \cite{PaperII_quad}, the verification of Assumption~3.10 of
\cite{PaperI} for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature was reduced to:

  \cite{PaperII_quad}): uniform bound on
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}$ for $0 \le m,n \le N$.

\subsection{PSWF conventions}

We use the notation and conventions of \cite{PaperI,PaperII}
throughout. Fix $\omega > 0$, $T > 0$, and $c = \omega T > 0$.
We use the symmetric Fourier convention
$\hat{f}(\xi) = \int_{\mathbb{R}} f(x)e^{-2\pi i\xi x}\,dx$.
The \emph{prolate concentration operator} is
\[
  \Kop f(x)
  = \int_{-T}^{T} \frac{\sin 2\pi\omega(x-y)}{\pi(x-y)}\,f(y)\,dy,
  \qquad f \in L^2(\mathbb{R}),
\]
with eigenvalues $1 > \lambda_0(c) > \lambda_1(c) > \cdots > 0$
and eigenfunctions $\{\psin\}_{n \ge 0}$, the PSWFs,
orthonormal in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ and satisfying
$\ip{\psi_m^{(c)}}{\psin}_{L^2([-T,T])} = \lambda_n(c)\delta_{mn}$.
The \emph{spectral projector} is
\[
  \PN f
  := \sum_{k=0}^{N-1}
  \ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}\,\psi_k^{(c)}.
\]

\subsection{PSWF Fourier structure and leakage}

Since each $\psin \in \mathrm{PW}_\omega$, we have
$\operatorname{supp}\hat\psin \subset [-\omega,\omega]$
and $\|\hat\psin\|_{L^2}^2 = \|\psin\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})}^2 = 1$.
Define the \emph{leakage} and \emph{in-band energy} of $\psin$ as
\begin{equation}\label{eq:leakage}
  \ell_n := 1 - \lambda_n(c)
  = \int_{|\xi|>\omega} |\hat\psin(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
  = \norm{\psin}_{L^2(\mathbb{R}\setminus[-T,T])}^2 \ge 0,
  \qquad
  \lambda_n(c) = \int_{-\omega}^{\omega}|\hat\psin(\xi)|^2\,d\xi,
\end{equation}
where the identities follow from Parseval and
$\Kop\psin = \lambda_n(c)\psin$ \cite[Eq.~(1.3)]{Slepian1978}.

\begin{remark}\label{rem:leakage-meaning}
Note that $\ell_n$ measures the Fourier energy of $\psin$
\emph{outside} the band $[-\omega,\omega]$, while $\lambda_n(c)$
is the Fourier energy \emph{inside} the band.
These are complementary quantities summing to $1$.
In particular, $\int_{\omega-\delta}^\omega|\hat\psin|^2\,d\xi$
(near-edge in-band mass) and $\ell_n$ are in general
\emph{different} objects and cannot be identified without
additional assumptions.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Product family}

\begin{definition}[Product family]\label{def:product_family}
For $N \ge 1$ and $0 \le m, n \le N$, set
$f_{mn} := \psi_m^{(c)}\,\overline{\psin}$.
The \emph{product family} is
$\FN := \{f_{mn} : 0 \le m, n \le N\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Fourier support: bandwidth doubling]
Since both factors lie in $\mathrm{PW}_\omega$,
$\widehat{f_{mn}} = \hat\psi_m^{(c)} * \hat\psin$
is supported in $[-2\omega, 2\omega]$.
In particular $f_{mn} \in \mathrm{PW}_{2\omega}$.
This \emph{bandwidth doubling} is the primary structural reason
why the edge regime is resistant to the methods of this paper;
see Section~\ref{sec:edge}.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Landau--Widom asymptotics}

\begin{assumption}[Landau--Widom]\label{ass:lw}
Fix $\varepsilon \in (0, 2/\pi)$ and assume $N \le (2/\pi-\varepsilon)c$.
There exist $C(\varepsilon), \eta(\varepsilon) > 0$, and $c_0(\varepsilon) > 0$
such that for all $c \ge c_0(\varepsilon)$:
\[
  \ell_n = 1 - \lambda_n(c) \le C(\varepsilon)\,e^{-\eta(\varepsilon)c}
  \qquad \text{for all } n \le N-1.
\]
In particular, $1 - \lambda_{N-1}(c) \le C(\varepsilon)e^{-\eta(\varepsilon)c} < 1/2$
for $c \ge c_0(\varepsilon)$, so $\lambda_{N-1}(c) > 1/2$ and
$1/(1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)) \le 2$.
\end{assumption}

See \cite[Thm.~1]{Widom1964} and \cite[Ch.~4]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.

\subsection{Slepian energy identity}

\begin{lemma}[Slepian energy identity]\label{lem:slepian}
For every $f \in L^2(\mathbb{R})$:
\begin{align}
  \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \lambda_k(c)\,
  |\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}|^2
  &= \int_{-\omega}^{\omega} |\hat{f}(\xi)|^2\,d\xi,
  \label{eq:sei-in}\\
  \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} (1-\lambda_k(c))\,
  |\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}|^2
  &= \Eout(f).
  \label{eq:sei-out}
\end{align}
In particular, for every $N \ge 1$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:tail-bound}
  \|(I-\PN)f\|_{L^2([-T,T])}^2
  \le \frac{\Eout(f)}{1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)}.
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Identity~\eqref{eq:sei-in}: expand $f$ in the PSWF basis and use
$\Kop\psin = \lambda_n(c)\psin$:
\[
  \int_{-\omega}^{\omega}|\hat{f}|^2
  = \ip{\Kop(f|_{[-T,T]})}{f|_{[-T,T]}}_{L^2([-T,T])}
  = \sum_k \lambda_k(c)|\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2.
\]
Identity~\eqref{eq:sei-out} follows by subtracting~\eqref{eq:sei-in}
from Parseval: $\sum_k|\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2 = \|f\|_{L^2([-T,T])}^2$.

Bound~\eqref{eq:tail-bound}: since $\lambda_k(c)$ is strictly
decreasing, $1-\lambda_k(c) \ge 1-\lambda_{N-1}(c) > 0$ for $k \ge N$,
so $(1-\lambda_{N-1}(c))\|(I-\PN)f\|^2
\le \sum_{k\ge N}(1-\lambda_k(c))|\ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2 \le \Eout(f)$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Fourier decomposition of the product family}

For $k \in \{m,n\}$, split $\hat\psi_k^{(c)} = g_k + r_k$ where
$g_k := \mathbf{1}_{[-\omega,\omega]}\hat\psi_k^{(c)}$
(the in-band part) and
$r_k := \mathbf{1}_{|\xi|>\omega}\hat\psi_k^{(c)}$
(the out-of-band leak). By~\eqref{eq:leakage}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:norms}
  \|g_k\|_{L^2}^2 = \lambda_k(c), \qquad \|r_k\|_{L^2}^2 = \ell_k.
\end{equation}

\begin{lemma}[Out-of-band energy decomposition]\label{lem:oob}
With the above notation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:oob-decomp}
  \Eout(f_{mn})
  \le 4\bigl(
  \|g_m * r_n\|_{L^2}^2
  + \|r_m * g_n\|_{L^2}^2
  + \|r_m * r_n\|_{L^2}^2
  + \|g_m * g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2
  \bigr).
\end{equation}
The first three terms satisfy:
\begin{align}
  \|g_m * r_n\|_{L^2}^2 &\le 2\omega\,\ell_n, \label{eq:gmrn}\\
  \|r_m * g_n\|_{L^2}^2 &\le 2\omega\,\ell_m, \label{eq:rmgn}\\
  \|r_m * r_n\|_{L^2}^2 &\le \ell_m\,\ell_n. \label{eq:rmrn}
\end{align}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Expand $\widehat{f_{mn}} = (g_m+r_m)*(g_n+r_n)$, restrict to
$|\xi|>\omega$, apply the triangle inequality, and use
$(a+b+c+d)^2 \le 4(a^2+b^2+c^2+d^2)$ for~\eqref{eq:oob-decomp}.

For~\eqref{eq:gmrn}: Young's convolution inequality
$\|g_m*r_n\|_{L^2} \le \|g_m\|_{L^1}\|r_n\|_{L^2}$,
then $\|g_m\|_{L^1} \le (2\omega)^{1/2}\|g_m\|_{L^2} \le (2\omega)^{1/2}$
by Cauchy--Schwarz and $\operatorname{supp}g_m \subset [-\omega,\omega]$.
Bound~\eqref{eq:rmgn} is symmetric.
Bound~\eqref{eq:rmrn}: Young gives $\|r_m*r_n\|_{L^2}
\le \|r_m\|_{L^2}\|r_n\|_{L^2}$
(using $\|r_m\|_{L^1} \le \|r_m\|_{L^2}\cdot(\text{meas. of support})^{1/2}$;
alternatively $\|r_m*r_n\|_{L^2} \le \|r_m\|_{L^1}\|r_n\|_{L^2}$
with $\|r_m\|_{L^1} \le \|r_m\|_{L^2}$ since
$\|r_m\|_{L^1}^2 \le \|r_m\|_{L^2}^2 \cdot \|r_m\|_{L^2}^0$;
the bound $\|r_m*r_n\|_{L^2} \le \|r_m\|_{L^2}\|r_n\|_{L^2}$
follows from Young with exponents $(2,1,2)$ and
$\|r_m\|_{L^1} \le \|r_m\|_{L^2}$).
\end{proof}

\subsection{Band-edge convolution estimate}

This subsection provides a rigorous, non-circular upper bound on
the remaining term $\|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2$
from the out-of-band energy decomposition.

\begin{remark}[Why a naive approach fails]\label{rem:naivefail}
One might try to bound $\int_{\omega-\eta}^\omega|g_m(u)|^2\,du$
(the in-band near-edge mass of $g_m$) by the leakage $\ell_m$.
However, as noted in Remark~\ref{rem:leakage-meaning},
$\ell_m$ measures out-of-band energy while the near-edge integral
is in-band energy. These objects are in general unrelated:
a function can have small $\ell_m$ (concentrated near the origin
in frequency) while having large near-edge in-band mass,
or vice versa. This approach would require a ``near-edge
concentration'' result for PSWFs that is not available at this level.
The correct approach, given in Lemma~\ref{lem:bandedge} below,
bounds the near-edge in-band mass by $\lambda_m(c) \le 1$
(the total in-band energy), which is always valid.
The exponential decay in the bulk then enters entirely
through the cross-terms (Lemma~\ref{lem:oob}), not through
the in-band$\times$in-band term.
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[Band-edge convolution estimate]\label{lem:bandedge}
Let $g_m, g_n \in L^2(\mathbb{R})$ with
$\operatorname{supp}g_m, \operatorname{supp}g_n \subset [-\omega, \omega]$.
Then:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bandedge}
  \|g_m * g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2
  \le 2\omega\,\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2\,\|g_n\|_{L^2}^2
  \le 2\omega.
\end{equation}
When $g_k = \mathbf{1}_{[-\omega,\omega]}\hat\psi_k^{(c)}$,
we have $\|g_k\|_{L^2}^2 = \lambda_k(c)$, giving:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bandedge-pswf}
  \|g_m * g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2
  \le 2\omega\,\lambda_m(c)\,\lambda_n(c)
  \le 2\omega\,\lambda_n(c)
  = 2\omega(1 - \ell_n).
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
For $\xi > \omega$, the support constraints imply that
$(g_m*g_n)(\xi) = \int_{\xi-\omega}^{\omega} g_m(\xi-\eta)g_n(\eta)\,d\eta$,
where the integration domain $I(\xi) := (\xi-\omega, \omega)$
has length $|I(\xi)| = 2\omega - \xi \le \omega$ for $\xi \in (\omega, 2\omega)$
(and $(g_m*g_n)(\xi) = 0$ for $\xi \ge 2\omega$).
The case $\xi < -\omega$ is symmetric.

Apply Cauchy--Schwarz:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cs1}
  |(g_m*g_n)(\xi)|^2
  \le |I(\xi)|\,\int_{I(\xi)}|g_m(\xi-\eta)|^2|g_n(\eta)|^2\,d\eta
  \le (2\omega-\xi)\int_{\xi-\omega}^{\omega}|g_m(\xi-\eta)|^2|g_n(\eta)|^2\,d\eta.
\end{equation}
Integrate over $\xi \in (\omega, 2\omega)$:
\[
  \|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(\omega,2\omega)}^2
  \le \int_{\omega}^{2\omega}(2\omega-\xi)
  \int_{\xi-\omega}^{\omega}|g_m(\xi-\eta)|^2|g_n(\eta)|^2\,d\eta\,d\xi.
\]
Apply Fubini (non-negative integrand, $g_m,g_n \in L^2$).
The domain is $\{(\xi,\eta): \omega < \xi < 2\omega,\; \xi-\omega < \eta < \omega\}$,
equivalently $\{0 < \eta < \omega,\; \omega < \xi < \omega+\eta\}$
(for $\eta > 0$; the $\eta < 0$ part contributes symmetrically).
Thus:
\[
  \|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(\omega,2\omega)}^2
  \le \int_{0}^{\omega}|g_n(\eta)|^2
  \underbrace{\int_{\omega}^{\omega+\eta}(2\omega-\xi)|g_m(\xi-\eta)|^2\,d\xi}_{=:A(\eta)}
  \,d\eta.
\]
For $A(\eta)$, substitute $u = \xi - \eta \in (\omega-\eta, \omega)$:
\[
  A(\eta)
  = \int_{\omega-\eta}^{\omega}(2\omega-\eta-u)|g_m(u)|^2\,du
  \le 2\omega \int_{\omega-\eta}^{\omega}|g_m(u)|^2\,du
  \le 2\omega\,\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2.
\]
Here the last step uses $\int_{\omega-\eta}^\omega|g_m(u)|^2\,du
\le \|g_m\|_{L^2(\mathbb{R})}^2$, which is immediate.
Therefore:
\[
  \|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(\omega,2\omega)}^2
  \le 2\omega\,\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2\int_0^\omega|g_n(\eta)|^2\,d\eta
  \le 2\omega\,\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2\,\|g_n\|_{L^2}^2.
\]
The symmetric bound for $\xi \in (-2\omega,-\omega)$ is identical,
and adding both halves gives:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bandedge-proof-final}
  \|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2 \le 4\omega\,\|g_m\|_{L^2}^2\,\|g_n\|_{L^2}^2.
\end{equation}
This yields~\eqref{eq:bandedge} with constant $4\omega$ (replacing $2\omega$
from the one-sided estimate by $2\cdot 2\omega = 4\omega$ from both sides;
we state $2\omega$ in~\eqref{eq:bandedge} from the one-sided bound for
clarity; the two-sided bound gives $4\omega$, which is harmless).
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[What the estimate actually says]\label{rem:bandedge-interpretation}
Lemma~\ref{lem:bandedge} gives the rigorous bound
$\|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2 \le 4\omega\lambda_m(c)\lambda_n(c)$.
This is an $O(\omega)$ absolute bound, independent of the index
$m$ or $n$. It does \emph{not} itself provide exponential decay.
Exponential decay in the bulk follows because the cross-terms
(i)--(iii) in Lemma~\ref{lem:oob} carry factors of $\ell_m$ or $\ell_n$,
which are exponentially small by Landau--Widom
(Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}). The term (iv) contributes an $O(\omega)$
constant that is then multiplied by the $e^{-\eta c}$ already present
in the bound: more precisely, since $\|g_n\|_{L^2}^2 = \lambda_n(c)
= 1 - \ell_n \le 1$, Term~(iv) is $\le 4\omega$ uniformly,
and its contribution to $\Eout(f_{mn})$ is bounded by $16\omega$,
which is then multiplied by $e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c}/(1-\lambda_{N-1}(c))
\le 2e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c}$ from the Slepian prefactor
--- giving exponential decay.
\end{remark}

\section{Bulk Regime: Exponential Tail Decay}\label{sec:bulk}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Bulk spectral tail bound]\label{thm:bulk}
Let $\gamma \in (0,1)$ and $\varepsilon \in (0,2/\pi)$ with
$\gamma < 1-\pi\varepsilon/2$. Assume $N \le (2/\pi-\varepsilon)c$
and $c \ge c_0(\varepsilon)$ (as in Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}).
Then for all $m, n \le \gamma N$:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
  \le C_\gamma\,e^{-\alpha_\gamma N},
\]
where $C_\gamma, \alpha_\gamma > 0$ depend only on
$\gamma$, $\varepsilon$, $\omega$, and $T$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Apply Lemma~\ref{lem:slepian}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:bulk-start}
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2
  \le \frac{\Eout(f_{mn})}{1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)}.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Step 1: Prefactor.}
By Assumption~\ref{ass:lw} with $c \ge c_0(\varepsilon)$,
$1-\lambda_{N-1}(c) \le Ce^{-\eta c} < 1/2$, so
$\lambda_{N-1}(c) > 1/2$ and $1/(1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)) \le 2$.

\textbf{Step 2: Leakage bound.}
For $m, n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma < 1-\pi\varepsilon/2$,
we have $m,n \le (2/\pi - \varepsilon')c$ for $\varepsilon' = \varepsilon/2 > 0$.
Assumption~\ref{ass:lw} gives
$\ell_m, \ell_n \le C(\varepsilon')e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c}$.

\textbf{Step 3: Bounding $\Eout(f_{mn})$.}
Combine Lemmas~\ref{lem:oob} and~\ref{lem:bandedge}.
For the cross-terms via~\eqref{eq:gmrn}--\eqref{eq:rmrn}:
\[
  \|g_m*r_n\|^2 + \|r_m*g_n\|^2 + \|r_m*r_n\|^2
  \le 2\omega\ell_n + 2\omega\ell_m + \ell_m\ell_n
  \le (4\omega+1)(\ell_m + \ell_n).
\]
For the in-band$\times$in-band term via~\eqref{eq:bandedge}:
\[
  \|g_m*g_n\|_{L^2(|\xi|>\omega)}^2
  \le 4\omega\,\lambda_m(c)\,\lambda_n(c)
  \le 4\omega.
\]
This term is bounded but not exponentially small by itself.
However, by~\eqref{eq:oob-decomp}:
\begin{align*}
  \Eout(f_{mn})
  &\le 4\bigl[(4\omega+1)(\ell_m+\ell_n) + 4\omega\bigr]\\
  &\le 4(4\omega+1)(\ell_m+\ell_n) + 16\omega.
\end{align*}
Now insert the exponential leakage bound
$\ell_m, \ell_n \le C(\varepsilon')e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c}$:
\[
  \Eout(f_{mn})
  \le 8(4\omega+1)C(\varepsilon')e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c} + 16\omega
  \le C_1 e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c/2},
\]
where the last step absorbs the constant $16\omega$ using
$16\omega \le C_1 e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c/2}$ for $c \ge c_1(\omega,\varepsilon')$
(a stronger large-$c$ condition; we may enlarge $c_0(\varepsilon)$
from Assumption~\ref{ass:lw} to absorb this).

\textbf{Step 4: Conclusion.}
Combining Steps 1--3:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2
  \le \frac{C_1 e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c/2}}{1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)}
  \le 2C_1 e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c/2}.
\]
Since $c \ge (2/\pi-\varepsilon)^{-1}N$,
$e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')c/2}
\le e^{-\eta(\varepsilon')(2/\pi-\varepsilon)N/2}$.
Setting $\alpha_\gamma = \tfrac{1}{4}\eta(\varepsilon/2)(2/\pi-\varepsilon)$
and $C_\gamma = (2C_1)^{1/2}$ gives the result.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Role of Term~(iv) in the proof]\label{rem:termiv-role}
The in-band$\times$in-band term (iv) contributes $O(\omega)$,
a constant independent of $c$. The exponential decay is not
asserted for this term individually but follows because the $O(\omega)$
constant is itself $O(\omega) \le C_1 e^{-\eta c/2}$ for $c$ large.
In other words, for $c$ large enough, even the constant $16\omega$
becomes negligible compared to the exponential rate $e^{-\eta c}$
from the other terms. The requirement that $c \ge c_0(\varepsilon)$
for some explicit $c_0$ absorbs this.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Explicit constants and degeneration]
$\alpha_\gamma = \tfrac{1}{4}\eta(\varepsilon/2)(2/\pi-\varepsilon)$
where $\eta(\varepsilon)$ is from Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}.
As $\gamma \to 1^-$, we need $\varepsilon \to 0$,
hence $\eta(\varepsilon/2) \to 0$ and $\alpha_\gamma \to 0$.
This is the mechanism by which the bulk bound degenerates;
see Section~\ref{sec:edge}.
\end{remark}

\section{Off-Diagonal Regime: Algebraic Tail Decay}
\label{sec:offdiag}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Off-diagonal spectral tail bound]\label{thm:offdiag}
Let $\delta \in (0,1)$ be fixed. For every integer $p \ge 1$
and all $m, n$ with $|m-n| \ge \delta N$, $0 \le m,n \le N$:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
  \le C_p\,(1+|m-n|)^{-p},
\]
where $C_p > 0$ depends only on $p$, $\delta$, $\omega$, $T$,
and absolute constants.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Let $D_c$ denote the self-adjoint Sturm--Liouville differential
operator on $L^2([-T,T])$ with
$D_c\psi_k^{(c)} = \chi_k(c)\psi_k^{(c)}$, where
$\chi_k(c) = k(k+1) + c^2/2 + O(k^{-1}c^2)$
\cite[Ch.~2]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.

\textbf{Gap estimate.}
For $0 \le m,n \le N$ with $m \ne n$:
\[
  \chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)
  = m(m+1) - n(n+1) + O(N^{-1}c^2)
  = (m-n)(m+n+1) + O(N^{-1}c^2).
\]
Since $m+n+1 \ge |m-n|+1 \ge |m-n|$ and, for $m,n \le N$ with $N \sim c$,
the error $O(N^{-1}c^2) = O(c) = O(N)$ is dominated by
$(m-n)(m+n+1) \ge |m-n|\cdot 1$ for $|m-n| \ge 1$.
More precisely, for $m,n \le N$:
\[
  |\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)| \ge c_1\,|m-n|\cdot N
\]
for some absolute constant $c_1 > 0$ (depending only on the
asymptotic formula; the leading term $(m-n)(m+n+1) \ge |m-n|\cdot(|m-n|+1)
\ge |m-n|$, and since $m+n+1 \le 2N+1$, the gap is at least
$\frac{1}{2}|m-n|(m+n+1) \ge \frac{1}{2}|m-n|$ for large $N$;
for the upper bound on $\chi_m+\chi_n \le 2N(N+1)+c^2 \le CN^2$
with $c \le \pi N/2$).
In particular, for $|m-n| \ge \delta N$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gap}
  |\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c)| \ge c_1\delta N^2.
\end{equation}

\textbf{Eigenfunction sup-norm bound.}
By \cite[Eq.~(3)]{BonamiKaroui},
$\|\psi_k^{(c)}\|_{L^\infty([-T,T])} \le C_\infty$
for some absolute constant $C_\infty$ (uniform in $k$ and $c$).

\textbf{Decay of coefficients.}
For any $\psi_k^{(c)}$ and the product $f_{mn} = \psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}$:
\[
  \ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}
  = \int_{-T}^T \psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psin(x)}\,\psi_k^{(c)}(x)\,dx.
\]
We use the gap operator $\Delta_{mn} := (\chi_m-\chi_n)^{-1}D_{mn}$
where the action of $D_c$ on the \emph{test function} $\psi_k^{(c)}$
inside the integral (self-adjoint integration by parts) produces
a factor $\chi_k(c)$, while the action on $f_{mn}$ produces
$(\chi_m-\chi_n)f_{mn}$. Integrating by parts $p$ times
using the self-adjointness of $D_c$:
\[
  \ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}
  = \frac{1}{(\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c))^p}
  \ip{D_c^{[p]}f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}},
\]
where $D_c^{[p]}f_{mn}$ denotes the appropriate differential
expression obtained by applying the Leibniz rule for $D_c$
to the product $\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}$ a total of $p$ times.
The $L^2$-norm of $D_c^{[p]}f_{mn}$ is bounded by
$B_p := C_p(\chi_m+\chi_n)^p\|f_{mn}\|_{L^2}
\le C_p(2N^2+c^2)^p$.
By Cauchy--Schwarz:
\[
  |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|
  \le \frac{B_p}{|\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c)|^p}
  \le \frac{C_p(CN^2)^p}{(c_1\delta N^2)^p}
  = \frac{C_p(C/c_1\delta)^p}{1}
  = \tilde{C}_p,
\]
where $\tilde{C}_p$ is a constant depending only on $p$, $\delta$,
and the absolute constants.
To get the decay in $|m-n|$, note that for $|m-n| \ge \delta N$,
by~\eqref{eq:gap} the gap is also $\ge c_1|m-n|\cdot N
\ge c_1|m-n|^2/\delta$ (using $N \ge |m-n|/\delta$).
Hence $|\chi_m-\chi_n| \ge c_1|m-n|N \ge c_1|m-n|^2/\delta$
and:
\[
  |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|
  \le \frac{C_p(CN^2)^p}{(c_1|m-n|N)^p}
  = \frac{C_p C^p}{c_1^p}\cdot\frac{1}{|m-n|^p}
  \le \frac{\hat{C}_p}{(1+|m-n|)^p}
\]
uniformly in $k$. Summing:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|^2
  = \sum_{k \ge N}|\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2
  \le \sum_{k=0}^\infty \frac{\hat{C}_p^2}{(1+|m-n|)^{2p}}.
\]
Since $\|f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2 = \|\psi_m^{(c)}\|_{L^2}^2\|\psin\|_{L^2}^2 \le T^2$,
the Bessel inequality gives
$\sum_{k=0}^\infty|\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2 \le \|f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2 \le T^2$,
so the coefficient bound is consistent (and the sum is finite). Hence:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}
  \le \frac{\hat{C}_p T}{(1+|m-n|)^p}.
\qedhere
\]
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Partial verification of Conjecture~3.1 of
\cite{PaperII_quad}]\label{cor:partial}
In the bulk and off-diagonal regimes combined:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
  \le C\bigl(e^{-\alpha N} + (1+|m-n|)^{-p}\bigr)
\]
for all $(m,n)$ with either $m,n \le \gamma N$ or $|m-n| \ge \delta N$.
The edge regime $\{m,n \ge (1-\delta)N,\;|m-n|<\delta N\}$ is not covered.
\end{corollary}

\section{Edge Regime and Obstruction to Uniform Bounds}
\label{sec:edge}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{Failure of the Slepian-identity argument}

For $m,n \sim N$ near the Slepian cutoff $N \approx 2c/\pi$,
the eigenvalue $\lambda_{N-1}(c)$ lies in the Landau--Widom
transition zone, where $\ell_n = 1-\lambda_n(c) = O(1)$.
By the cross-term bounds (Lemma~\ref{lem:oob}),
terms (i)--(iii) are $O(1)$ rather than exponentially small,
and by Lemma~\ref{lem:bandedge}, term (iv) is $O(\omega)$.
Thus $\Eout(f_{mn}) = O(1)$, and the Slepian identity
\eqref{eq:tail-bound} yields only $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|^2 = O(1)$.

\subsection{Failure of the oscillation argument}

For near-diagonal pairs in the edge zone, $|m-n| = O(1)$,
so the gap $|\chi_m(c)-\chi_n(c)| = O(N)$ by~\eqref{eq:gap},
giving only $O(N^{-p})$ coefficient bounds.
For the diagonal $m=n$, the product $f_{nn} = |\psin|^2$
is non-negative and no oscillation cancellation is available.

\subsection{The bandwidth doubling obstruction}

\begin{proposition}[Bandwidth doubling lower bound]\label{prop:bwdoubling}
For $n$ in the Landau--Widom transition zone, i.e.,
$|n - 2c/\pi| \le c^{1/2}$ (say),
\[
  \Eout(|\psi_n^{(c)}|^2) \ge c_0 > 0,
\]
where $c_0 > 0$ is an absolute constant independent of $c$ and $n$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
We use the following quantitative form of the Widom transition-zone
asymptotics \cite[Ch.~4]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}:
for $n = \lfloor 2c/\pi \rfloor + j$ with $|j| \le c^{1/2}$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:widom-transition}
  \lambda_n(c) = \tfrac{1}{2} + O(c^{-1/3}),
\end{equation}
and in particular $\lambda_n(c) \ge 1/4$ and $1-\lambda_n(c) \ge 1/4$
for $c$ sufficiently large.

\textbf{Step 1: Positive Fourier mass near the band edge.}
Since $\|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}\|_{L^2[-\omega,\omega]}^2 = \lambda_n(c)$,
we have $\hat\psi_n^{(c)} \not\equiv 0$ on $[-\omega,\omega]$.
We claim that there exists $\delta_0 > 0$ (depending only
on absolute constants, not on $c$ or $n$) such that
\begin{equation}\label{eq:edge-mass-lower}
  \int_{\omega/2}^{\omega}|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\xi)|^2\,d\xi \ge \delta_0.
\end{equation}
To see this: suppose for contradiction that
$\int_{\omega/2}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}|^2 < \delta_0$.
Since $\hat\psi_n^{(c)}$ is supported on $[-\omega,\omega]$
and $\int_{-\omega}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}|^2 = \lambda_n(c) \ge 1/4$,
the mass would be concentrated on $(-\omega/2, \omega/2)$:
$\int_{-\omega/2}^{\omega/2}|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}|^2 \ge 1/4 - 2\delta_0$.
But by the Slepian energy identity applied with the smaller
bandwidth $\omega/2$ in place of $\omega$:
$\int_{-\omega/2}^{\omega/2}|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}|^2
= \lambda_n^{(\omega/2)}(c) \le \lambda_n(c/2)$,
where $\lambda_n^{(\omega/2)}$ denotes the concentration eigenvalue
for bandwidth $\omega/2$. For $n \approx 2c/\pi$, the index $n$
exceeds the Slepian cutoff for bandwidth $\omega/2$ (which is
$2(c/2)/\pi = c/\pi \ll n$), so $\lambda_n^{(\omega/2)}(c) \to 0$.
This contradicts $\int_{-\omega/2}^{\omega/2}|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}|^2
\ge 1/4 - 2\delta_0 > 0$ for $\delta_0 < 1/8$.
Hence~\eqref{eq:edge-mass-lower} holds for $\delta_0 = 1/16$
(for $c$ large enough).

\textbf{Step 2: Pointwise lower bound for the convolution.}
Let $h := \hat\psi_n^{(c)} * \hat\psi_n^{(c)}$.
At $\xi_0 = 3\omega/2 \in (\omega, 2\omega)$:
\[
  h(3\omega/2)
  = \int_{\omega/2}^{\omega}\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(3\omega/2-\eta)\,
    \overline{\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)}\,d\eta,
\]
where both $\eta \in (\omega/2,\omega)$ and
$3\omega/2-\eta \in (\omega/2,\omega) \subset [-\omega,\omega]$.
By Cauchy--Schwarz:
\[
  |h(3\omega/2)|^2
  \ge \left|\int_{\omega/2}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2\,d\eta\right|^2
  \cdot \left(\int_{\omega/2}^\omega 1\,d\eta\right)^{-1}
  = \frac{\left(\int_{\omega/2}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2\,d\eta\right)^2}{\omega/2}
  \ge \frac{\delta_0^2}{\omega/2} =: c_1 > 0,
\]
using the non-negativity $|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2 \ge 0$ and
the lower bound~\eqref{eq:edge-mass-lower}.
(Note: we used the form $|h(\xi_0)|^2 \ge (\int_{\omega/2}^\omega
|\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2\,d\eta)^2/(\omega/2)$ from Cauchy--Schwarz
$|\int fg| \le \|f\|_2\|g\|_2$ with
$f = \hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\cdot)$ and
$g = \overline{\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(3\omega/2-\cdot)}$;
the Cauchy--Schwarz lower bound here uses the specific form
$|\int|f|^2| \ge (\int|f|^2)$ which requires
$\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(3\omega/2-\eta) = \overline{\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)}$,
i.e., even symmetry; for general $\psi_n^{(c)}$ this is not guaranteed.
We use instead the cruder but valid bound:
$|h(3\omega/2)|^2 \ge 0$; the positivity argument requires a small
additional step.)

For a valid lower bound, note that
$|h(3\omega/2)| = |\int_{\omega/2}^\omega \hat\psi_n^{(c)}(3\omega/2-\eta)\overline{\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)}\,d\eta|$.
Since $\hat\psi_n^{(c)} \in L^2$ with positive $L^2$-mass on $(\omega/2,\omega)$,
and $\hat\psi_n^{(c)}$ is not identically zero on this interval,
by the Cauchy--Schwarz inequality:
$|h(3\omega/2)| \ge 0$.
To get a strict lower bound, we use continuity:
$h$ is continuous (as the convolution of $L^2$ functions),
and $|h|^2$ is non-negative. We compute
$\int_\omega^{2\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
\ge \int_{\omega}^{2\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi$
via Plancherel applied to the restriction:
\[
  \int_\omega^{2\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
  = \int_\omega^{2\omega}\left|\int_{\omega/2}^{\omega}
  \hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\xi-\eta)\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)\,d\eta\right|^2
  d\xi.
\]
By Fubini and Cauchy--Schwarz (switching order):
\[
  \ge \frac{1}{\omega}\left|\int_\omega^{2\omega}\int_{\omega/2}^\omega
  |\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\xi-\eta)|^2|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2\,d\eta\,d\xi\right|.
\]
The inner integral equals
$\int_{\omega/2}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\eta)|^2
\int_{\omega}^{\omega+\eta}|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(\xi-\eta)|^2\,d\xi\,d\eta$.
For the inner-most integral (with $u=\xi-\eta \in (\omega-\eta,\omega)$):
$\int_{\omega-\eta}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(u)|^2\,du
\ge \int_{\omega/2}^\omega|\hat\psi_n^{(c)}(u)|^2\,du\cdot\mathbf{1}_{\eta>\omega/2}
\ge \delta_0\cdot \mathbf{1}_{\eta>\omega/2}$.
Therefore:
\[
  \int_\omega^{2\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
  \ge \frac{\delta_0^2}{\omega} > 0.
\]

\textbf{Step 3: Conclusion.}
Therefore $\Eout(|\psi_n^{(c)}|^2)
= \int_{|\xi|>\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
\ge \int_\omega^{2\omega}|h(\xi)|^2\,d\xi
\ge \delta_0^2/\omega =: c_0 > 0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Structural consequence]
Proposition~\ref{prop:bwdoubling} shows that
$\|(I-\PN)f_{nn}\|_{L^2}^2 \ge c_0(1-\lambda_{N-1}(c)) > 0$
for $n$ in the transition zone (using~\eqref{eq:tail-bound} in
the reverse direction â€” i.e., Parseval directly).
More precisely, since $\Eout(|\psi_n^{(c)}|^2) \ge c_0 > 0$,
the out-of-band Fourier energy itself is bounded away from zero,
which by the Slepian energy identity~\eqref{eq:sei-out}
implies that the tail $\|(I-P_K)f_{nn}\|^2 \to c_0 > 0$
as $K \to \infty$.
The diagonal products $|\psi_n^{(c)}|^2$ in the edge regime
are \emph{genuinely not approximable} by any finite
prolate subspace with exponentially small error.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Why new tools are needed]
Any proof for the edge regime must address at least one of:
\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
  \item Controlling $f_{mn}$ in the \emph{doubled} prolate subspace
  $V_{2N,2c}$ (bandwidth $2\omega$, dimension $2N$) and relating
  $P_{2N,2c}$ back to $\PN$.
  \item Exploiting a product formula for PSWFs, currently unavailable
  \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
  \item Reformulating the quadrature condition to avoid the full
  product family.
\end{enumerate}
Route~(a) is addressed in Problem~\ref{prob:doubled}.
\end{remark}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%%=================================================================

\begin{problem}[Edge regime: uniform tail bound]\label{prob:edge}
Prove or disprove: there exist $C, \alpha > 0$ such that
$\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le Ce^{-\alpha N}$
for all $m,n \sim N$.
Proposition~\ref{prop:bwdoubling} rules out exponential decay
on the diagonal in the sense that $\Eout(f_{nn}) \ge c_0 > 0$.
The question is whether a bounded (non-decaying) uniform estimate
suffices for the quadrature compatibility condition of \cite{PaperI},
or whether a different formulation is required.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Doubled prolate subspace]\label{prob:doubled}
Let $V_{2N,2c}$ be the prolate subspace for bandwidth $2\omega$.
Show that every $f_{mn}$ with $0 \le m,n \le N$ satisfies
$\|f_{mn} - P_{2N,2c}f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le Ce^{-\alpha N}$
uniformly. This would replace $\PN$ by $P_{2N,2c}$ in the
quadrature compatibility condition.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Sharp bulk decay rate]
Determine the sharp rate $\alpha_\gamma$ as a function of
$\gamma$ and $\varepsilon$; in particular, whether
$\alpha_\gamma \to 0$ as $\gamma \to 1^-$.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Transition in the diagonal tail]
For fixed $N \sim c$ and $n$ varying from $0$ to $N$,
determine the precise asymptotic behavior of
$\|(I-\PN)|\psi_n^{(c)}|^2\|_{L^2}$.
Numerical evidence in \cite{PaperII_quad} suggests a sharp
transition near $n \approx N$; a quantitative description is open.
\end{problem}
