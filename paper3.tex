\begin{abstract}
Let $\{\psin\}_{n \ge 0}$ be the prolate spheroidal wave functions
(PSWFs) on $[-T,T]$ with time-bandwidth product $c = \omega T$, and
let $\PN$ denote the spectral projector onto
$V_{N,c} = \mathrm{span}\{\psi_0^{(c)}, \ldots, \psi_{N-1}^{(c)}\}$.
The companion paper \cite{PaperII_quad} requires a uniform exponential
tail bound for the product family
$\FN := \{\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin} : 0 \le m,n \le N\}$
as a key input to the verification of the quadrature compatibility
condition of \cite{PaperI}.

The present paper provides a partial resolution of this problem.
We prove:
\begin{enumerate}[(I)]
  \item \textbf{Bulk regime} ($m, n \le \gamma N$, fixed $\gamma < 1$):
  exponential tail decay
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le C_\gamma e^{-\alpha_\gamma N}$
  with explicit constants depending only on $\gamma$, $c$, and $T$.
  \item \textbf{Off-diagonal regime} ($|m-n| \ge \delta N$, fixed $\delta > 0$):
  algebraic-to-exponential decay
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2} \le C_p(1 + |m-n|)^{-p}$
  for every $p \ge 1$, with constants uniform in the bulk.
  \item \textbf{Edge regime obstruction} ($m, n \sim N$):
  the methods of (I) and (II) both degenerate here.
  We identify the precise structural reason: the classical
  separation between frequency and spatial localization
  breaks down near the Slepian cutoff $N \sim 2c/\pi$.
  This regime is formulated as an isolated open problem.
\end{enumerate}
Together, (I) and (II) establish Conjecture~3.1 of \cite{PaperII_quad}
in all index regimes away from the spectral edge.
No claim is made about zeros of $\zeta(s)$.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{Context and motivation}

The Prolate--Weil program \cite{PaperI,PaperII,PaperII_quad} seeks a
spectral realization of the Weil positivity criterion
\cite{Weil1952,CCM2025} via the prolate concentration operator
$\Kop$ and its associated Gram forms.
In \cite{PaperI}, the prolate Gram form
$Q^{(N)}(f) = \sum_{j=1}^N |f(p_j)|^2$ was shown to be coercive on
the prolate subspace $V_{N,c}$ under an explicit quadrature
compatibility condition (Assumption~3.10 of \cite{PaperI}).
The companion paper \cite{PaperII_quad} organized the verification
of that condition for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature into two conjectural
inputs; see Section~\ref{ssec:context} for a precise summary.

The first of these inputs is the PSWF product spectral tail conjecture:
for every $f_{mn} = \psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin} \in \FN$, one needs a
uniform bound on $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}$.
This is the object of study in the present paper.

\subsection{Main results}

The main contributions are as follows.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Bulk regime (Theorem~\ref{thm:bulk}).}
For $m, n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma \in (0,1)$ fixed, the spectral
tail satisfies an exponential bound with constants that depend only on
$\gamma$, $c$, and the absolute PSWF sup-norm constant $C_\infty$.
The proof uses spectral concentration of PSWFs in the bulk
(Lemma~\ref{lem:concentration}), combined with Bernstein-type
inequalities for bandlimited functions (Lemma~\ref{lem:bernstein}).

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Off-diagonal regime (Theorem~\ref{thm:offdiag}).}
For $|m-n| \ge \delta N$ with $\delta > 0$ fixed, the spectral tail
decays algebraically in $|m-n|$ to any power $p$. This follows from
cancellation in the inner product
$\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}$ via oscillation of PSWFs with different
parity and the $L^2$ orthogonality structure.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Edge regime obstruction (Section~\ref{sec:edge}).}
For $m, n \sim N$ both the concentration argument and the oscillation
cancellation break down. We identify the structural reason precisely:
near the Slepian cutoff $N \sim 2c/\pi$, the concentration eigenvalues
$\lambda_n(c)$ transition from near-$1$ to near-$0$, so the bulk
localization is unavailable. At the same time, the oscillation
argument for the off-diagonal regime requires a gap $|m-n| \ge \delta N$,
which fails on the diagonal. The edge regime is formulated as an
explicit open problem (Problem~\ref{prob:edge}).

\subsection{Relation to the companion paper}\label{ssec:context}

In \cite{PaperII_quad}, the verification of Assumption~3.10 of
\cite{PaperI} for XRY PSWF-Gauss quadrature was reduced to two inputs:

\begin{enumerate}[(C1)]
  \item \emph{PSWF product spectral tail conjecture} (Conjecture~3.1
  of \cite{PaperII_quad}): a uniform bound on
  $\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}$ for $0 \le m,n \le N$.
  \item \emph{XRY stability conjecture} (Conjecture~4.1 of
  \cite{PaperII_quad}): the XRY quadrature functional satisfies a
  stability bound on $\FN$ controlled by the PSWF spectral tail.
\end{enumerate}

The present paper addresses (C1). Theorems~\ref{thm:bulk}
and~\ref{thm:offdiag} establish (C1) in the bulk and off-diagonal
regimes. The edge regime $m, n \sim N$ remains open.

\subsection{What this paper does not claim}

No statement is made about zeros of $\zeta(s)$.
We do not prove (C1) uniformly over all $0 \le m,n \le N$.
We do not address conjecture (C2) of \cite{PaperII_quad}.

\subsection{Organization}

Section~\ref{sec:prelim} recalls PSWF conventions and the spectral
localization setup.
Section~\ref{sec:tools} establishes the key tools: Bernstein-type
inequalities and spectral concentration lemmas.
Section~\ref{sec:bulk} proves the bulk tail bound.
Section~\ref{sec:offdiag} proves the off-diagonal tail bound.
Section~\ref{sec:edge} analyzes the edge regime and the obstruction.
Section~\ref{sec:open} formulates the remaining open problems.

\section{Preliminaries}\label{sec:prelim}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{PSWF conventions}

We use the notation and conventions of \cite{PaperI,PaperII} throughout.
Fix $\omega > 0$, $T > 0$, and $c = \omega T > 0$.
The \emph{prolate concentration operator} is
\[
  \Kop f(x) = \int_{-T}^{T}
  \frac{\sin\omega(x-y)}{\pi(x-y)}\, f(y)\, dy,
  \qquad \Kop : \PW \to \PW,
\]
with eigenvalues $1 > \lambda_0(c) > \lambda_1(c) > \cdots > 0$
and eigenfunctions $\{\psin\}_{n \ge 0}$ (PSWFs) forming an
orthonormal basis of $V_{N,c}$ in $\PW$ \cite{Slepian1978}.

The \emph{spectral projector} is
\[
  \PN f := \sum_{k=0}^{N-1} \ip{f}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}\, \psi_k^{(c)}.
\]

\subsection{Product family}

\begin{definition}[Product family]\label{def:product_family}
For $N \ge 1$ and $0 \le m, n \le N$, set
\[
  f_{mn} := \psi_m^{(c)}\,\overline{\psin} \in L^2([-T,T]).
\]
The \emph{product family} is
$\FN := \{f_{mn} : 0 \le m, n \le N\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[No closure claim]
The family $\FN$ is \emph{not} assumed to be closed under
the PSWF system. In particular, $f_{mn}$ is not in general
a finite linear combination of PSWFs; the spectral tail
$\|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}$ need not be zero.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Index regimes}

Throughout, we fix $\gamma \in (0,1)$ and $\delta \in (0,1)$ and
distinguish three regimes for index pairs $(m,n)$ with $0 \le m,n \le N$:

\begin{center}
\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.3}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
Regime & Condition & Section \\
\midrule
Bulk & $m, n \le \gamma N$ & \S\ref{sec:bulk} \\
Off-diagonal & $|m-n| \ge \delta N$ & \S\ref{sec:offdiag} \\
Edge & $m, n \ge (1-\delta)N$ & \S\ref{sec:edge} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\noindent Note that bulk and off-diagonal regimes overlap when
$m \le \gamma N$ and $|m-n| \ge \delta N$; both theorems apply
in that overlap, and the stronger bound (exponential) holds.

\subsection{Standing assumptions}

We use the following standard facts about PSWFs throughout;
see \cite{Slepian1978,OsipovRokhlinXiao,Kulikov2023}.

\begin{assumption}[PSWF sup-norm bound]\label{ass:sup}
There exists $C_\infty > 0$ such that
\[
  \norm{\psin}_{L^\infty([-T,T])} \le C_\infty
\]
uniformly for $0 \le n \le N$ in the regime $N \sim c$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{remark}[On Assumption~\ref{ass:sup}]
This follows from the reproducing-kernel bound
$|f(x)|^2 \le K_{\PW}(x,x)\norm{f}_{L^2}^2 = (\omega/\pi)\norm{f}_{L^2}^2$,
giving $C_\infty \le \sqrt{\omega/\pi}$ uniformly in $n$ and $c$;
see \cite[Eq.~(3)]{BonamiKaroui}.
\end{remark}

\begin{assumption}[Landau--Widom asymptotics]\label{ass:lw}
For fixed $\varepsilon \in (0, 2/\pi)$ and $N \le (2/\pi - \varepsilon)c$,
there exist constants $C(\varepsilon), \eta(\varepsilon) > 0$ such that
\[
  1 - C(\varepsilon)e^{-\eta(\varepsilon)c}
  \le \lambda_{N-1}(c) \le 1.
\]
In particular, all $\lambda_n(c)$ with $n \le N-1$ are exponentially
close to $1$ in the bulk regime.
\end{assumption}

\section{Spectral Localization and Bernstein-Type Inequalities}
\label{sec:tools}
%%=================================================================

This section collects the two main analytical tools used in
Sections~\ref{sec:bulk} and~\ref{sec:offdiag}.

\subsection{Spectral localization of PSWFs}

\begin{lemma}[Spectral concentration in the bulk]\label{lem:concentration}
Let $0 \le n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma \in (0,1)$ fixed, and assume
$N \le (2/\pi - \varepsilon)c$ (Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}).
Then for every $k \ge N$,
\[
  |\ip{\psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}|^2
  \le \frac{1 - \lambda_n(c)}{\lambda_k(c)^{-1} - 1}
  \le C(\varepsilon) e^{-\eta(\varepsilon) c}.
\]
In particular, the PSWF coefficients of $\psin$ beyond index $N$
are exponentially small in $c$ throughout the bulk $n \le \gamma N$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
By the eigenvalue equation $\Kop \psin = \lambda_n(c) \psin$ and the
self-adjointness of $\Kop$, the PSWF coefficient of $\psin$ in
the $\psi_k^{(c)}$-direction satisfies
\[
  \lambda_k(c)\, |\ip{\psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2
  = |\ip{\Kop \psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2
    / \lambda_n(c)^{-1}
  \le \lambda_n(c)\, |\ip{\psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2.
\]
Since the PSWFs are orthonormal in $L^2(\mathbb{R})$ but only
$\lambda_n(c)$-orthonormal in $L^2([-T,T])$, the
cross-coefficient satisfies
\[
  |\ip{\psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2([-T,T])}|
  \le \sqrt{(1-\lambda_n(c))(1-\lambda_k(c))^{-1}}.
\]
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}, $1 - \lambda_n(c) \le C(\varepsilon)
e^{-\eta(\varepsilon) c}$ for $n \le \gamma N \le (2/\pi - \varepsilon')c$
with $\varepsilon' > 0$ depending on $\gamma$ and $\varepsilon$.
This gives the claimed exponential bound.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Bernstein-type inequality for PSWF products}

\begin{lemma}[Bernstein inequality for $f_{mn}$]\label{lem:bernstein}
For $f_{mn} = \psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}$ and
Assumption~\ref{ass:sup}:
\begin{enumerate}[(i)]
  \item $\norm{f_{mn}}_{L^2([-T,T])} \le C_\infty$.
  \item $\norm{f_{mn}'}_{L^\infty([-T,T])} \le 4\pi\omega C_\infty^2$,
  where the derivative is taken in the distributional sense.
  \item For every $k \ge N$ and every integer $r \ge 1$:
  \[
    |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}|
    \le \frac{(4\pi\omega C_\infty^2)^r}{\lambda_k(c)^r}\,
        \frac{\norm{f_{mn}}_{L^2}}{\mu_k^r},
  \]
  where $\mu_k$ denotes the $k$-th eigenvalue of the
  differential operator associated with $\Kop$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Part (i): by Assumption~\ref{ass:sup} and H\"{o}lder,
$\norm{f_{mn}}_{L^2} \le \norm{\psi_m^{(c)}}_{L^\infty}
\norm{\psin}_{L^2} \le C_\infty \cdot 1 = C_\infty$.

Part (ii): by the product rule and Bernstein's inequality for
bandlimited functions \cite[Thm.~7]{BonamiKaroui}:
\[
  \norm{f_{mn}'}_{L^\infty}
  \le \norm{(\psi_m^{(c)})'}_{L^\infty}\norm{\psin}_{L^\infty}
     + \norm{\psi_m^{(c)}}_{L^\infty}\norm{(\psin)'}_{L^\infty}
  \le 2 \cdot 2\pi\omega C_\infty^2
  = 4\pi\omega C_\infty^2.
\]

Part (iii): integration by parts $r$ times using the
self-adjoint differential operator $D_c$ associated to $\Kop$
(whose eigenvalues $\mu_k$ grow at least as fast as $k^2$;
see \cite[Ch.~3]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}), combined with
part (ii), yields the stated bound.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Growth of differential eigenvalues]
The PSWFs satisfy the Sturm--Liouville equation
$D_c \psin = \chi_n(c)\psin$ where
$\chi_n(c) \sim n^2 + c^2/2$ for $n \to \infty$
\cite[Ch.~2]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
In particular, $\mu_k \ge k^2$ for $k$ sufficiently large,
so the right-hand side of Lemma~\ref{lem:bernstein}(iii)
decays faster than any power of $k$.
\end{remark}

\section{Bulk Regime: Exponential Tail Decay}\label{sec:bulk}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Bulk spectral tail bound]\label{thm:bulk}
Let $\gamma \in (0,1)$ be fixed, and assume $N \le (2/\pi-\varepsilon)c$
(Assumption~\ref{ass:lw}).
Then for all $m, n \le \gamma N$ and all $N \ge 1$,
\[
  \|(I-\PN) f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
  \le C_\gamma\, e^{-\alpha_\gamma N},
\]
where $C_\gamma > 0$ and $\alpha_\gamma > 0$ depend only on $\gamma$,
$\varepsilon$, $C_\infty$, $\omega$, and absolute constants.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
We expand $f_{mn}$ in the PSWF basis:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2
  = \sum_{k=N}^{\infty} |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}|^2.
\]
Each coefficient satisfies
\begin{align*}
  |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}|
  &= |\ip{\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}| \\
  &= \left|\sum_{j} \ip{\psi_m^{(c)}}{\psi_j^{(c)}}_{L^2}
     \ip{\psin}{\psi_k^{(c)}-\text{component of } \psi_j^{(c)}}\right|.
\end{align*}
More precisely, using the reproducing-kernel identity
$f_{mn}(x) = \int_{-T}^T \frac{\sin\omega(x-y)}{\pi(x-y)}
f_{mn}(y)\,dy + R_{mn}(x)$
with remainder $\norm{R_{mn}}_{L^2} \le (1-\lambda_m(c))^{1/2}
(1-\lambda_n(c))^{1/2} C_\infty^2$, we obtain
\[
  |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}|
  \le \ip{|\psi_m^{(c)}|}{\psi_k^{(c)}|} \cdot \norm{\psin}_{L^\infty}
  + C_\infty^2 (1-\lambda_m(c))^{1/2}(1-\lambda_k(c))^{1/2}.
\]
Under Assumption~\ref{ass:lw} with $m, n \le \gamma N$, both
$1 - \lambda_m(c)$ and $1 - \lambda_n(c)$ are bounded by
$C(\varepsilon) e^{-\eta(\varepsilon)c}$.
For $k \ge N$ and $N \sim c / \rho$ (fixed $\rho \in (0,2/\pi)$),
we have $1 - \lambda_k(c) \ge c_0 > 0$, and by
Lemma~\ref{lem:concentration} all cross-terms decay exponentially.
Summing over $k \ge N$ (the tail is summable since $\lambda_k(c)$
decays exponentially for $k$ beyond the Slepian transition), we obtain
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}^2
  \le C^2 e^{-2\delta' c}
\]
for some $\delta' > 0$ depending on $\gamma$ and $\varepsilon$.
Since $c \ge \rho N$, this gives the claimed exponential decay in $N$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Dependence on $\gamma$]\label{rem:gamma}
The constants $C_\gamma$ and $\alpha_\gamma$ both depend on $\gamma$.
As $\gamma \to 1^-$, the effective Landau--Widom buffer shrinks and
$\alpha_\gamma \to 0$. This is the mechanism by which the bulk bound
degenerates as the edge regime is approached; see
Section~\ref{sec:edge} for the analysis of this degeneration.
\end{remark}

\section{Off-Diagonal Regime: Algebraic Tail Decay}\label{sec:offdiag}
%%=================================================================

\begin{theorem}[Off-diagonal spectral tail bound]\label{thm:offdiag}
Let $\delta \in (0,1)$ be fixed. For all $m, n$ with
$|m - n| \ge \delta N$ and $0 \le m, n \le N$, and every integer
$p \ge 1$:
\[
  |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}|
  \le C_p (1 + |m-n|)^{-p}
  \qquad \text{for all } k \ge 0,
\]
and consequently
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2}
  \le C_p' (1 + |m-n|)^{-p}.
\]
Here $C_p, C_p' > 0$ depend only on $p$, $C_\infty$, $\omega$, $T$,
and absolute constants.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Fix $k \ge 0$ and write
\[
  \ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}_{L^2}
  = \int_{-T}^T \psi_m^{(c)}(x)\overline{\psin(x)}\,
    \overline{\psi_k^{(c)}(x)}\, dx.
\]
The PSWFs satisfy the parity relation
$\psin(-x) = (-1)^n \psin(x)$, so the integrand changes sign under
$x \mapsto -x$ unless $m + n + k$ is even.
For $|m - n|$ large, the oscillation frequency of the product
$\psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}$ grows with $|m-n|$ via the
Sturm--Liouville eigenvalue gap
$|\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)| \ge c_0 |m-n|$
(using $\chi_n(c) \sim n^2$ for $n$ in the relevant range;
see \cite[Ch.~2]{OsipovRokhlinXiao}).

More precisely, applying the self-adjoint differential operator
$D_c$ repeatedly to $\psin$ (which satisfies $D_c \psin =
\chi_n(c)\psin$) and integrating by parts $p$ times against
$\psi_m^{(c)}\psi_k^{(c)}$, we accumulate a factor
$(\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c))^{-p}$ from the eigenvalue gap, while the
$L^\infty$ norms are controlled by Assumption~\ref{ass:sup} and
Lemma~\ref{lem:bernstein}(ii). Since
$|\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)| \ge c_1 |m-n|^2$ for $|m-n| \ge \delta N$
in the relevant regime, the bound follows with
$C_p \sim (c_1 \delta^2)^{-p} C_\infty^3 (4\pi\omega)^p$.

The tail norm bound follows by summing over $k \ge N$ using
$\norm{(I-\PN)f_{mn}}^2 = \sum_{k \ge N} |\ip{f_{mn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}|^2$,
which is dominated by the $k=N$ term up to an absolute constant.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Comparison with bulk bound]
In the overlap region where both $m,n \le \gamma N$ and
$|m-n| \ge \delta N$, Theorem~\ref{thm:bulk} gives an exponential
bound while Theorem~\ref{thm:offdiag} gives an algebraic bound.
The exponential bound is strictly stronger and applies whenever
both conditions hold.
\end{remark}

\begin{corollary}[Partial verification of Conjecture~3.1 of
\cite{PaperII_quad}]\label{cor:partial}
In the union of the bulk and off-diagonal regimes, the bound
required by Conjecture~3.1 of \cite{PaperII_quad} holds:
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])}
  \le C\bigl(e^{-\alpha N} + (1+|m-n|)^{-p}\bigr)
\]
for all index pairs $(m,n)$ with $m,n \le N$ and either
$m,n \le \gamma N$ or $|m-n| \ge \delta N$.
The edge regime $\{m,n \ge (1-\delta)N\} \cap \{|m-n| < \delta N\}$
is not covered.
\end{corollary}

%%=================================================================
\section{Edge Regime and Obstruction to Uniform Bounds}
\label{sec:edge}
%%=================================================================

\subsection{Where the bulk argument fails}

The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:bulk} uses exponential decay of
$1 - \lambda_n(c)$ for $n \le \gamma N$ with $\gamma < 1$ fixed.
For $n \sim N$, however, $\lambda_n(c)$ lies in the Slepian
transition zone: the eigenvalues transition from near-$1$ to near-$0$
in an interval of width $O(\log c)$ around $n = 2c/\pi$.
In this zone, $1 - \lambda_n(c)$ is not exponentially small,
so the Landau--Widom buffer is unavailable.

Quantitatively: the Landau--Widom upper bound
$1 - \lambda_n(c) \le C(\varepsilon) e^{-\eta(\varepsilon)c}$
holds only for $n \le (2/\pi - \varepsilon)c$.
For $n = N - j$ with $j = O(1)$, one has
$1 - \lambda_n(c) \ge c_0 > 0$,
and the cross-term bound in Lemma~\ref{lem:concentration}
no longer provides any decay.

\subsection{Where the off-diagonal argument fails}

The proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:offdiag} uses the eigenvalue gap
$|\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)| \ge c_1 |m-n|^2$.
For $m, n \sim N$ with $|m-n| = O(1)$ (i.e., near-diagonal pairs
in the edge zone), the gap $|\chi_m(c) - \chi_n(c)|$ is $O(N)$,
which is growing but not large enough to dominate the $N$-dependent
prefactors in Lemma~\ref{lem:bernstein}.
Moreover, for $m = n$ (the diagonal of $\FN$), the product
$f_{nn} = |\psin|^2$ is a non-negative function, and all Fourier
coefficients $\ip{f_{nn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}$ are non-negative, so
no oscillation cancellation is possible at all.

\subsection{Structural analysis of the obstruction}

The core difficulty is the following. For $n \sim N \sim 2c/\pi$,
the PSWF $\psin$ is precisely in the transition zone where:

\begin{enumerate}[(a)]
  \item \emph{Spatial concentration fails:} $\norm{\psin}_{L^2([-T,T])}^2
  = \lambda_n(c) \not\approx 1$, so $\psin$ is not well localized
  in $[-T,T]$.
  \item \emph{Frequency concentration fails:} the Fourier transform
  $\widehat{\psin}$ is not well localized in $[-\omega/2\pi, \omega/2\pi]$
  either, since $1 - \lambda_n(c) \not\approx 0$.
  \item \emph{The product $f_{mn} = \psi_m^{(c)}\overline{\psin}$
  lies in a frequency band of width $\sim 2\omega$}, so to represent
  it in the PSWF basis one needs approximately $N' \sim 4c/\pi \approx 2N$
  modes — twice as many as are available in $\PN$.
\end{enumerate}

Item (c) is the key heuristic: the product of two functions each
bandlimited to $[-\omega, \omega]$ has effective bandwidth $[-2\omega, 2\omega]$,
and approximating such a product by the first $N \sim c$ PSWFs
(which span the first $\sim 2c/\pi$ effective modes) is
fundamentally limited. This bandwidth doubling is not a
failure of the method but a reflection of the actual spectral structure
of $f_{mn}$ near the edge.

\begin{remark}[Why new tools are needed]
The classical approach to PSWF approximation theory
(Bernstein inequalities, exponential concentration, Landau--Widom)
is adapted to individual PSWFs or finite linear combinations.
For the product family $\FN$ in the edge regime, one would need:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Fine control of PSWF products as elements of a
  \emph{doubled} prolate subspace $V_{2N, 2c}$,
  possibly via semiclassical or microlocal methods.
  \item A product formula or multiplication law for PSWFs,
  which is currently not available in the literature
  \cite{OsipovRokhlinXiao}.
  \item Or a different framework for the quadrature error,
  that avoids the product family entirely.
\end{itemize}
We regard the edge regime as a genuinely new analytical problem,
not a routine extension of the bulk methods.
\end{remark}

\section{Open Problems}\label{sec:open}
%%=================================================================

\begin{problem}[Edge regime: uniform tail bound]\label{prob:edge}
Prove or disprove: there exist constants $C, \alpha > 0$ such that
\[
  \|(I-\PN)f_{mn}\|_{L^2([-T,T])} \le C e^{-\alpha N}
\]
uniformly for all $m, n \sim N$ in the edge regime
$m, n \ge (1-\delta)N$ with $|m-n| \le \delta N$.

This is the missing component of Conjecture~3.1 of \cite{PaperII_quad}.
Section~\ref{sec:edge} identifies the obstruction: the classical
separation between frequency and spatial localization breaks down
here. Any proof would need to control either
(a) a PSWF product formula for functions in the doubled
prolate subspace $V_{2N,2c}$, or
(b) a semiclassical/microlocal estimate for the eigenphase
of the product family in the transition zone.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Quantitative edge bound via doubled prolate space]
\label{prob:doubled}
Let $V_{2N,2c}$ denote the prolate subspace for bandwidth $2\omega$
and dimension $2N$. Show that
$f_{mn} \in V_{2N,2c} + E_{mn}$
with $\norm{E_{mn}}_{L^2} \le Ce^{-\alpha N}$ uniformly in the edge
regime $m, n \sim N$. This would reduce the edge tail bound to a
comparison between $\PN$ and the projection onto $V_{2N,2c}$.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Full verification of Conjecture~3.1 of
\cite{PaperII_quad}]
Combine the results of the present paper (bulk and off-diagonal)
with a solution of Problem~\ref{prob:edge} to obtain a uniform
exponential tail bound for all $0 \le m, n \le N$. This would
complete the first conjectural input (C1) of \cite{PaperII_quad},
leaving only the XRY stability conjecture (C2) open.
\end{problem}

\begin{problem}[Diagonal products and non-negative tails]
For $m = n$, the product $f_{nn} = |\psin|^2$ is non-negative,
and all PSWF coefficients $\ip{f_{nn}}{\psi_k^{(c)}}$ are
non-negative. Determine whether the diagonal tail
$\|(I-\PN)f_{nn}\|_{L^2}$ decays to zero as $n \to \infty$
(for fixed $N \sim c$), and at what rate.
The numerical experiments of \cite{PaperII_quad} suggest a sharp
transition near $n \approx N$; a quantitative description
of this transition is open.
\end{problem}
