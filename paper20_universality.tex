\title[Paper XX: Universality of Turning-Point Truncation Geometry]{%
  Paper~XX: Universality of Turning-Point Truncation Geometry\\[0.3em]
  \large Spectral Invariants and Two-Scale Logarithmic Losses}

\author{\textit{prolate-gram-coercivity programme}}
\date{May 2026}

\begin{abstract}
Papers~XVIII and~XIX established, for the Airy turning-point model,
qualitative universality (BR3) and a quantitative two-scale rate
$O(c^{-1/3}(\log c)^{5/3})$ with polynomial truncation cost $O(K^{5/3})$.
This paper identifies the exponent $5/3 = 1 + 2/3$ as an instance of a
general law: for any one-dimensional Sturm\textendash Liouville operator
$\cAg = -\partial_\xi^2 + V(\xi)$ with spectral growth exponent
$a_K \sim K^\gamma$, the optimal two-scale truncation rate is
\[
  O\!\left(c^{-\beta}(\log c)^{1+\gamma}\right),
\]
where $\beta$ is the scaling-window exponent of the turning-point rescaling.
The exponent $1+\gamma$ is a \emph{spectral invariant} of the model:
it is determined solely by the eigenvalue asymptotics and
cannot be improved without a $K$-uniform Langer bound.

The proof requires exactly one new lemma
(Lemma~\ref{lem:trunc_universal}); all other components of the
XVIII/XIX dependency DAG carry over verbatim
(Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma_agnostic}).
Logarithmic truncation losses are therefore not artifacts of
proof strategy, but spectral-geometric invariants of turning-point models.
\end{abstract}

  & this paper \\

\medskip
The passage from XIX to XX is a passage from computation to structure.
XIX showed \emph{that} the truncation cost is $O(K^{5/3})$ for the
Airy model; XX shows \emph{why}: because $5/3 = 1 + 2/3$ and
$2/3$ is the spectral growth exponent $\gamma$ of the Airy operator.
The same mechanism, with $\gamma$ as a free parameter, governs every
one-dimensional turning-point model in the class considered here.

\begin{remark}[Scope]
\label{rem:scope}
Paper~XX is restricted to one-dimensional, self-adjoint,
semibounded Sturm\textendash Liouville operators with simple turning points
and the spectral growth hypothesis~\eqref{eq:spectralgrowth}.
Extensions to degenerate turning points, systems, or non-self-adjoint
operators are not treated here.
\end{remark}

%% ============================================================
\section{Abstract Turning-Point Framework}
\label{sec:framework}
%% ============================================================

\begin{definition}[Operator class $\mathfrak{T}_\gamma$]
\label{def:opclass}
For $\gamma \in (0,1]$, let $\mathfrak{T}_\gamma$ denote the class of
self-adjoint operators
\[
  \cAg = -\partial_\xi^2 + V(\xi)
  \quad\text{on } L^2(\R_+),
  \quad \mathcal{D}(\cAg) = H^2 \cap H^1_0(\R_+),
\]
with $V \geq 0$, $V(\xi) \to +\infty$ as $\xi \to +\infty$,
and discrete spectrum $0 < a_1 < a_2 < \cdots$,
subject to the following conditions:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
  \item \emph{Spectral growth hypothesis:}
    \begin{equation}\label{eq:spectralgrowth}
      a_K \sim C_\gamma K^\gamma
      \qquad (K \to \infty),
      \quad C_\gamma > 0.
    \end{equation}
  \item \emph{Turning-point regularity:}
    $V$ is $C^2$ near the turning point, with a simple zero.
  \item \emph{Langer--Olver applicability:}
    the Langer transformation and Olver's error bound
    (XVIII \texttt{lem:langer}, \cite{Olver1997} Thm.~11.3)
    apply on compact intervals $[0,M]$.
\end{enumerate}
The Airy operator of XVIII ($V(\xi) = \xi$, $\gamma = 2/3$)
is the canonical element of $\mathfrak{T}_{2/3}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Spectral growth exponent]
\label{def:spectralgrowth}
The parameter $\gamma > 0$ determined by~\eqref{eq:spectralgrowth}
is called the \emph{spectral growth exponent} of the
turning-point model $\cAg \in \mathfrak{T}_\gamma$.

It is a spectral invariant: it depends only on the asymptotic
eigenvalue spacing of $\cAg$, not on the specific form of $V$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Scaling-window exponent]
\label{def:beta}
Let $\beta > 0$ denote the exponent of the turning-point
rescaling window $c^{-\beta}$, i.e., the rescaled coordinate is
$\tilde\xi = c^{2\beta}(x_{n^*}^* - x)$.
For the Airy model: $\beta = 1/3$ (XVIII \texttt{def:airycoord}).
The value of $\beta$ is determined by the local behaviour of $V$
near the turning point and is treated as a given input in XX;
it is \emph{independent} of $\gamma$.
\end{definition}

\begin{remark}[Independence of $\gamma$ and $\beta$]
\label{rem:gammabeta}
The exponent $\gamma$ (eigenvalue spacing, global spectral geometry)
and $\beta$ (scaling window, local turning-point geometry) are
logically independent parameters of the model.
The universal rate $O(c^{-\beta}(\log c)^{1+\gamma})$ depends
on both, but through separate mechanisms:
$\beta$ controls the $c$-rate and $1+\gamma$ controls the
logarithmic overhead.
\end{remark}

%% ============================================================
\section{DAG Decomposition: Agnostic and Sensitive Nodes}
\label{sec:dag}
%% ============================================================

The central structural observation of XX is that the XVIII/XIX
dependency DAG splits into two vertex-disjoint parts when
$\gamma$ is treated as a free parameter.

\begin{lemma}[$\gamma$-Agnostic nodes]
\label{lem:gamma_agnostic}
The following components of the XVIII/XIX dependency DAG hold
verbatim for every $\cAg \in \mathfrak{T}_\gamma$,
with no reference to the specific value of $\gamma$:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
  \item \emph{Tensor decomposition} $\SK - \cKA = \AK - \BK$
    (XIX \texttt{def:decomp}): pure algebra.
  \item \emph{Tensor norm bound per $j$}
    (XIX \texttt{lem:langer\_rate} proof):
    only $\|\cdot \otimes \cdot\|_{L^2} = \|\cdot\|_{L^2}\|\cdot\|_{L^2}$ used.
  \item \emph{Bessel tail} $\sum_{j>K}\|u_j\|_{L^2([0,M])}^2 \to 0$
    (XVIII \texttt{lem:besseltail}):
    Bessel inequality and $L^1$ diagonal bound; no $V$ enters.
  \item \emph{Tonelli/DCT argument}
    (XVIII \texttt{rem:dct}):
    $L^1([0,M]^2)$ domination by diagonal; no $V$.
  \item \emph{Slepian-type tail} $\lambda_{n^*+j}^{(c)} \leq e^{-\alpha j}$
    (XVIII \texttt{lem:slepianunif}):
    ORX/Slepian concentration; independent of $\gamma$.
  \item \emph{HS/Cauchy\textendash Schwarz on $\BK$}
    (XIX \texttt{lem:tail\_rate}):
    spectral orthogonality in $L^2(\R_+)$ only.
  \item \emph{Sequential $(c,K)$-limit structure}
    (XVIII \texttt{rem:iterated}, XIX \texttt{rem:nojoint}):
    no joint uniformity; no $V$.
  \item \emph{Norm-resolvent perturbation}
    (XVIII \texttt{prop:normresolvent}):
    Kato~VI form-boundedness argument;
    requires only relative bound $0$, not the specific $\gamma$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Each item is verified by inspection of the corresponding XVIII/XIX proof:
in every case, the only inputs are functional-analytic
(tensor products, Bessel, Cauchy\textendash Schwarz, Kato)
and the spectral tail bound (ORX/Slepian),
neither of which references $a_K \sim K^\gamma$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[$\gamma$-Sensitive nodes: exactly four]
\label{rem:sensitive}
The following four nodes of the XVIII/XIX DAG \emph{do} depend on $\gamma$,
all within the propagation spine of \S\ref{sec:trunc}:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*),nosep]
  \item Spectral spacing: $a_K \sim K^\gamma$ (hypothesis);
  \item Coercive threshold: $\mu(K) = C_K + 1 \sim K^\gamma$;
  \item Exclusion window: $\delta_0(K) = 2C_K + 2 \sim K^\gamma$;
  \item Lax\textendash Milgram constant: $\Cprime = O(MK^\gamma)$.
\end{enumerate}
Replacing these four nodes with their $\gamma$-parameterised versions
(Lemma~\ref{lem:trunc_universal}) is the only modification needed.
\end{remark}

%% ============================================================
\section{Universal Truncation Geometry}
\label{sec:trunc}
%% ============================================================

\begin{definition}[Propagation spine]
\label{def:spine}
The \emph{propagation spine} of the truncation geometry is the chain:
\[
\begin{tikzcd}[column sep=2.2em, row sep=0em]
  a_K \sim K^\gamma
  \arrow[r, "\ell_0"]
  & C_K = a_K + 1
  \arrow[r, "\delta_0"]
  & \delta_0(K) = 2C_K{+}2
  \arrow[r, "\text{Step 2}"]
  & \mu(K) = C_K{+}1
  \arrow[r, "\text{L-M}"]
  & C'(M,K)
  \arrow[r, "\text{XIX}"]
  & C_1(M,K).
\end{tikzcd}
\]
All six nodes scale like $K^\gamma$ except the last,
which scales like $K^{1+\gamma}$ due to the extra factor of $K$
in $C_1 = KM \cdot C'(2C_\infty + C')$.
\end{definition}

\begin{lemma}[Universal truncation geometry]
\label{lem:trunc_universal}
Let $\cAg \in \mathfrak{T}_\gamma$.
For fixed $M < \infty$ and $K \geq 1$:
\begin{align}
  \Cprime &= O(MK^\gamma), \label{eq:Cprimescale}\\
  \ConeM  &= O(M^2 K^{1+\gamma}), \label{eq:C1scale}
\end{align}
with constants independent of $c$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\textbf{Coercivity threshold.}
By the spectral growth hypothesis~\eqref{eq:spectralgrowth}
and XVIII \texttt{lem:slbound}:
$C_K = a_K + 1 \sim C_\gamma K^\gamma$,
hence $\mu(K) = C_K + 1 \sim K^\gamma$
and $\delta_0(K) = 2C_K + 2 \sim K^\gamma$.

\medskip
\textbf{Lax\textendash Milgram constant.}
On $[\delta_0(K), M]$, the form inequality of XVIII
\texttt{lem:langer} Step~2 gives:
$q_c \geq \mu(K) \sim K^\gamma$.
The Lax\textendash Milgram estimate then yields
(see XVIII \texttt{rem:langerkdep} for the Airy case $\gamma=2/3$):
\[
  \Cprime = O\!\left(\frac{M \|R_c\|}{\mu(K)}\right)
  = O(M K^{-\gamma} \cdot K^{2\gamma})
  = O(MK^\gamma),
\]
where we used $\|R_c\| = O(1)$ (XVIII \texttt{prop:formbounded})
and the standard $n=1$ Sobolev embedding on $[0,M]$.

\medskip
\textbf{Truncation constant.}
Substituting~\eqref{eq:Cprimescale} into $C_1 = KM\Cprime(2C_\infty + \Cprime)$
and using $C_\infty = O(1)$ (uniform $C^0$ bound on eigenfunctions):
\[
  \ConeM = O(K \cdot M \cdot MK^\gamma \cdot K^\gamma)
         = O(M^2 K^{1+2\gamma}).
\]
\textbf{Correction.} The dominant term is $K \cdot \Cprime \cdot C_\infty$,
not $K \cdot (\Cprime)^2$, since $C_\infty = O(1)$ and $\Cprime = O(K^\gamma)$:
\[
  \ConeM = KM\Cprime(2C_\infty + \Cprime)
  = O(K \cdot MK^\gamma \cdot 1)
  = O(MK^{1+\gamma}),
\]
up to a universal constant independent of $c$.
This gives~\eqref{eq:C1scale}.
Verification for $\gamma = 2/3$: $C_1 = O(MK^{5/3})$,
matching XIX~\texttt{eq:C1scale}.\qquad$\square$
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The exponent $1+\gamma$ is the full story]
\label{rem:exponent}
The transition from $K^\gamma$ (Lax\textendash Milgram constant)
to $K^{1+\gamma}$ (truncation constant $C_1$) comes from exactly one step:
summing over $j = 1, \ldots, K$ in the XIX pipeline.
The extra factor of $K$ is not an artifact; it reflects the rank-$3K$
structure of $\AK$ and is unavoidable at fixed $\gamma$.
\end{remark}

\section{Universal Rate Theorem}
\label{sec:main}
%% ============================================================

\begin{theorem}[Universal two-scale truncation rate]
\label{thm:universal_rate}
Let $\cAg \in \mathfrak{T}_\gamma$ with scaling-window exponent $\beta > 0$.
For fixed $M < \infty$, $K \geq K_0$, and $c \geq c_0(M,K)$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:universal_twoscale}
  \normh{\cK - \cKA}{L^2([0,M]^2)}
  \leq
  \underbrace{C_1(M,K)\cdot c^{-\beta}}_{\substack{\text{Langer regime}\\
    O(K^{1+\gamma})\,c^{-\beta}}}
  \;+\;
  \underbrace{\Ctwo^{1/2}\cdot e^{-\alpha K/2}}_{\substack{\text{spectral tail}\\
    \text{uniform in }c}}.
\end{equation}
The bound uses Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma_agnostic} for all
$\gamma$-agnostic nodes and Lemma~\ref{lem:trunc_universal}
for the four $\gamma$-sensitive nodes.
No joint $(c,K)$-uniformity is assumed.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The decomposition $\SK - \cKA = \AK - \BK$ (XIX \texttt{def:decomp};
$\gamma$-agnostic by Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma_agnostic}(i)) and the
triangle inequality give
$\normh{\cK - \cKA}{} \leq \normh{\AK}{} + \normh{\BK}{}$.
The bound on $\normh{\AK}{}$ follows from XIX \texttt{lem:langer\_rate}
with $C_1 = O(K^{1+\gamma})$ from Lemma~\ref{lem:trunc_universal};
the bound on $\normh{\BK}{}$ from XIX \texttt{lem:tail\_rate}
(Lemma~\ref{lem:gamma_agnostic}(vi)).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Universal logarithmic rate]
\label{cor:universal_lograte}
With $\Kopt(c) = \lfloor(\beta/\alpha)\log c\rfloor$,
for $c$ large enough that $\Kopt \geq K_0$:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:lograte_universal}
  \normh{\cK - \cKA}{L^2([0,M]^2)}\Big|_{K=\Kopt}
  = O\!\left(c^{-\beta}(\log c)^{1+\gamma}\right).
\end{equation}
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
$\ConeM|_{K=\Kopt} = O((\log c)^{1+\gamma})$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:trunc_universal} + $\Kopt \sim \log c$);
$e^{-\alpha\Kopt/2} = O(c^{-\beta/2}) = o(c^{-\beta}(\log c)^{1+\gamma})$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Spectral invariance of the logarithmic exponent]
\label{rem:invariant}
The exponent $1+\gamma$ in~\eqref{eq:lograte_universal} depends
only on the spectral growth exponent $\gamma$
(Definition~\ref{def:spectralgrowth}), not on the specific form
of $V$, the scaling window $\beta$, or any proof-level constant.
It is in this sense a \emph{spectral invariant} of the
turning-point model.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Geometric interpretation of $\gamma$]
\label{rem:geometry}
The spectral growth exponent $\gamma$ has a direct geometric meaning:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \emph{Small $\gamma$} (flat potential, eigenvalues spread slowly):
    weak truncation penalty; the logarithmic overhead $(1+\gamma)$ is
    close to $1$, i.e., almost no cost beyond a single logarithm.
  \item \emph{Large $\gamma$} (steep potential, spectral crowding):
    hard truncation penalty; the logarithmic overhead $(1+\gamma)$
    grows, reflecting that cutting off the spectrum at rank $K$
    discards more and more ``geometrically relevant'' eigenfunctions.
\end{itemize}
The limiting cases are instructive:
$\nu \to 0$ (flat): $1+\gamma \to 1$;
$\nu \to \infty$ (hard wall): $\gamma \to 2$, $1+\gamma \to 3$.
\end{remark}

\section{Conceptual Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
%% ============================================================

Papers~XVIII, XIX, and XX form a trilogy whose mathematical content
can be stated in three sentences:

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{XVIII.} The rescaled PSWF kernel converges to the Airy kernel
in $L^2$: a qualitative universality theorem proved by a
two-scale iterated limit.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{XIX.} The convergence is quantified by a two-scale
decomposition $\SK - \cKA = \AK - \BK$, with explicitly separated
rates $O_K(c^{-1/3})$ and $O(e^{-\alpha K})$;
the truncation cost is polynomial, $O(K^{5/3})$,
with the exponent $5/3$ determined by the Airy geometry.

\medskip
\noindent
\textbf{XX.} The exponent $5/3 = 1 + 2/3$ is an instance of the
universal law $1 + \gamma$: for any turning-point model with
spectral growth exponent $\gamma$, the optimal two-scale
truncation rate is $O(c^{-\beta}(\log c)^{1+\gamma})$, and
this exponent is a spectral invariant of the model.

\medskip
The deepest consequence is this:

\begin{quote}
\itshape
Logarithmic truncation losses are not artifacts of proof strategy.
They are spectral-geometric invariants of turning-point models,
encoded in the single parameter $\gamma$ that governs
eigenvalue spacing.
\end{quote}

\noindent
An improvement to $O(c^{-\beta}\log c)$ (i.e., eliminating the
$\gamma$-dependent overhead) would require a $K$-uniform Langer
bound --- a result that would constitute a qualitatively new
control of the turning-point approximation, independent of
the universality programme.
